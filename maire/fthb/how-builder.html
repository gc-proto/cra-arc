How to apply – Home purchased from a builder

How to apply for this rebate if you bought a new or substantially renovated house from a builder or purchased a share of the capital stock of a co-operative housing corporation (co-op).

Determine your application type

There are five rebate application types in Part C of Form GST190. Each application type has its own submission requirements and filing deadline.

The builder pays or credits the rebate to you

Application type 1A
You bought both the house and land from the same builder
Application type 1B
You bought the house and leased the related land from the same builder

For these application types, the builder must send your completed rebate application to the CRA.

You have up to two years after the day ownership of the house is transferred to you to get the application form to the builder and have the builder pay or credit you with the amount of the rebate.

The builder does not pay or credit the rebate to you

Application type 2
You bought the house and the related land from the same builder
Application type 5
You bought the house and leased the related land from the same builder

For these application types, you must file your application yourself.

You have up to 2 years from the date ownership was transferred to you to claim your rebate.

Co-operative housing

Application type 3
You bought a share of the capital stock of a co-op

You must file your application yourself.

You have up to 2 years from the date ownership was transferred to you to claim your rebate.

Choose your application type to complete and submit your form

There are five rebate application types in Part C of Form GST190. Each application type has its own submission options and filing deadline.

You bought the house and land from the same builder

Application 1A: The builder pays or credits the rebate to you

If you and the builder agree that the builder will pay or credit you with the amount of the rebate, the builder must send your completed rebate application and the Ontario rebate schedule (if applicable) to the CRA. You may not file another rebate application directly with us for this house.

Fill out the form, and sign it in the presence of the builder or your lawyer who will send the signed application form to the builder. This is usually done around the time of the closing date for your purchase.

You have up to two years after the day ownership of the house is transferred to you to get the application form to the builder and have the builder pay or credit you with the amount of the rebate.

Application 2: The builder does not pay or credit the rebate to you

You must file your application yourself:

  • Online: Option 1

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your Individual, Business, or Representative account
    3. Select File a GST/HST rebate:
      • In My Account, under “More services”
      • In My Business Account or Represent a Client, in the “Rebates” tab of the appropriate GST/HST program account number

  • By mail: Option 2

    1. Fill out form GST190
    2. Send the completed form to the address on the form

You have up to 2 years from the date ownership was transferred to you to claim your rebate.

You bought the house and lease the land from the same builder

Application 1B: The builder pays or credits the rebate to you

If you and the builder agree that the builder will pay or credit you with the amount of the rebate, the builder must send your completed rebate application and the Ontario rebate schedule (if applicable) to the CRA. You may not file another rebate application directly with us for this house.

Fill out the form, and sign it in the presence of the builder or your lawyer who will send the signed application form to the builder. This is usually done around the time of the closing date for your purchase.

You have up to two years after the day ownership of the house is transferred to you to get the application form to the builder and have the builder pay or credit you with the amount of the rebate.

Application 5: The builder does not pay or credit the rebate to you

You must file your application yourself:

  • Online: Option 1

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your Individual, Business, or Representative account
    3. Select File a GST/HST rebate:
      • In My Account, under “More services”
      • In My Business Account or Represent a Client, in the “Rebates” tab of the appropriate GST/HST program account number

  • By mail: Option 2

    1. Fill out form GST190
    2. Send the completed form to the address on the form

You have up to 2 years from the date ownership was transferred to you to claim your rebate.

If you bought a modular, mobile, or floating home

If you bought a modular home, a mobile home, or a floating home, refer to Modular home, mobile home, and floating home for more information on which application type to use. You cannot use Form GST190 to claim a rebate for a modular home that is not considered to be a mobile home.

If the builder only pays or credits the new housing rebate and not the FTHB rebate to you

You and the builder might agree that the builder will pay or credit only the new housing rebate to you, and not the FTHB rebate (for example, if ownership of the home is transferred prior to receiving Royal Assent for the FTHB rebate). In this case, the builder must only send your rebate application for the new housing rebate and the Ontario rebate schedule (if applicable) to the CRA.

To receive the FTHB rebate, you must file a rebate application and Ontario rebate schedule (if applicable) with the CRA for only the FTHB rebate.

For details on filling out the rebate application and removing the new housing rebate(s) paid or credited by the builder, refer to: Guide RC4028, GST/HST New Housing Rebate

Substantial renovation (for the purposes of new housing rebates)

Major changes have to be made to meet the definition of a substantial renovation. In a major renovation project, the interior of a building is essentially gutted. Generally, 90% or more of the interior of the existing housing has to be removed or replaced to qualify as a substantial renovation (the 90% test).

You do not have to remove or replace the foundation, exterior and interior supporting walls, roof, floors, and staircases to meet the 90% test.

Only livable areas count towards a substantial renovation, including finished basements and finished attics. Livable areas do not include garages or crawl spaces. Work done to partially complete a basement but not make it a livable basement does not count toward the 90% test.

Primary place of residence

Primary place of residence of an individual generally means the residence that the individual lives in on a permanent basis. An individual may have more than one residence, but is considered to have only one primary place of residence.

For more information, refer to: GST/HST Policy Statement P-228, Primary Place of Residence
