Register for My Account for Individuals

Follow these steps to determine the best way to get access to My Account.

Sign in

If you already have a GCOS account, select an option to sign in:

  • Sign in with CRA ID
  • Sign in with your bank
  • Sign in with your Province

Gather your documents

When you register, there is a 15 minute time limit to prevent security risks of unattended devices. Make sure you have the following ready:

  • Your assessed tax returns for the 2 most recent tax years
    • If you don't have either: You may still be able to sign in with a provincial partner.
  • Your Social Insurance Number
    • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
  • Your date of birth
  • Your current postal code or ZIP code

Choose how you want access My Account

The following provides the same information in two different formats:

  • Register with existing online banking credentials
    • Pro 1
    • Pro 2
  • Register with a CRA user ID and password
    • Pro 1
    • Pro 2
  • Sign in with Alberta.ca or a BC Service Card
    • Pro 1

    With a financial institution

    You must have an existing account with one of our Sign-in Partner banks or credit unions.

    Your banking ID and password are never shared with the CRA

    When you use your online banking credential, the information you enter and the identity of the financial institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access.

    The Sign-In Partner does not provide any identity information to the Government and simply validates your username or card number and password.

    My Account details are never shared with your financial institution

    None of your personal tax information will be shared with your financial institution.

    You can only access My Account with a provincial sign-in partner. My Business Account and Register a Client are not supported.

    You do not need to register with the CRA. Each time you sign in with a provincial partner, you will enter your SIN to gain access to your account.

Wait for your CRA security code

Once you complete the first step of your registration, a CRA security code will be sent to you by mail.

Before the expiry date, sign in with the option you registered with and follow the online instructions until you are prompted for the security code.

Keep your security code on file

The CRA security code you receive expires after 16 months. If you're ever locked out, you can use your security code to recover your account.

Get a new CRA security code

If your CRA security code expires, you will have to get a new one.

Get limited access without a security code

You can get limited access immediately, by beginning the registration process. You do not need to enter the CRA security code to access the following services:

  • View status of your tax return
  • Notification preferences
  • Request a remittance voucher
  • View notice of assessment or reassessment
  • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
  • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
