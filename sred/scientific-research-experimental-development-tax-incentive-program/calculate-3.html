Expenditures that qualify for an ITC

To claim a SR&ED investment tax credit (ITC), you will need to determine the SR&ED expenditures that may qualify for a refundable or non-refundable ITC.

Your qualified SR&ED expenditures are based on your total allowable SR&ED expenditures that you adjust according to amounts you paid or received.

Qualified expenditures may also let you report an ITC recapture on an ITC you claimed previously.

Adjusting your qualified expenditures

Add amounts that increase your expenditures

You may add amounts that increase your qualified expenditures, such as:

  • Payment of unpaid amounts from previous year’s
  • Prescribed proxy amount (if you used the proxy method)
  • Qualified expenditures that were transferred to you (use Form T1146, Agreement to Transfer Qualified Expenditures Incurred in Respect of SR&ED Contracts Between Persons Not Dealing at Arm's Length)

Subtract amounts that decrease your expenditures

You may subtract amounts that decrease your qualified expenditures. All assistance and contract payments that you may receive for SR&ED reduce your qualified expenditures:

Other decreases may result from:

  • Provincial & territorial assistance (if not renounced)
  • Other government or non-government assistance
  • Certain contract and third-party payments
  • Certain expenditures not allowed by regulations
    What this may include

    Expenditures not allowed by regulations may include:

    • interest and other financing costs
    • a due or fee for membership in a scientific or technical society or organization
    • legal or accounting fees
    • expenditures for the acquisition of used equipment
  • Non-arm’s length transactions
    Transferring non-arm’s length expenditures

    Non-arm’s length contract costs do not qualify as SR&ED expenditures for ITC purposes. However the performer may transfer all or part of their qualified SR&ED expenditures to you for ITC purposes (they must use Form T1146, Agreement to Transfer Qualified Expenditures Incurred in Respect of SR&ED Contracts Between Persons Not Dealing at Arm's Length).
    Form T1146 should be filed with Form T661
