How your business can benefit

If your business is engaged in scientific research and experimental development (SR&ED), you may be able to earn tax incentives that reduce your taxable income.

Corporations, individuals, trusts, and members of a partnership who conduct SR&ED work in Canada may be able to:

  • Claim a deduction against income
  • Earn an investment tax credit (ITC)

Claim a deduction against income

To claim a deduction against income, you will need to calculate the allowable SR&ED expenditures that you incurred in the year.

You can then pool these expenditures and use them to reduce your net income for the current year or a future year.

Earn an investment tax credit (ITC)

You may earn an ITC to reduce your income tax payable for the year.

Your business may claim an ITC at a basic rate of 15%. Some corporations may claim an ITC at the enhanced rate of 35%.

Some organizations may also earn a refundable ITC.

Provincial and territorial R&D tax credits

Provincial and territorial R&D tax credits may also be available, which you may claim in addition to SR&ED tax incentives.

Receiving provincial and territorial tax credits may reduce the expenditures you can claim for SR&ED. [You will have to report these tax credits in your SR&ED claim.]

For more information: Summary of provincial and territorial R&D tax credits
