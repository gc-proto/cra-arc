Calculate pool of deductible expenditures

On this page

Deduct some or all of your expenditures

Once you have calculated your allowable SR&ED expenditures, you will need to deduct them against business income that you earned. The result will be your pool of deductible expenditures.

You have the option to deduct some or all of the allowable expenditures that are available for the tax year. Any balance you don’t claim can be carried forward to future years.

Your pool of deductible expenditures accumulates over time and can be adjusted depending on your business needs.

If you used the proxy method to calculate your allowable expenditures, the prescribed proxy amount (PPA) is not included in the pool of deductible expenditures.

If you’re a member of a partnership

In a partnership, the pool of deductible expenditures is deducted at the partnership level. Individual members cannot make separate deductions.

Partnerships must claim all of the available pool of deductible expenditures in the year. It will not have a pool of expenditures to carry forward to future tax years.

Review the guide for more information on how partnerships calculate their pool of deductibles:
T4088 - Guide to T661, Partnerships

Adjusting your pool of deductible expenditures         

Deduct amounts you receive

You will need to deduct any government and non-government assistance you received, expect to receive or are entitled to receive in the tax year from your total allowable SR&ED expenditures.

This may include:

  • Provincial & territorial assistance (if not renounced)
  • Other government or non-government assistance
  • Any SR&ED ITC claimed the previous year (refunds, applied or carried back)
  • Any sale of SR&ED capital assets and other deductions

Add repayments and other amounts

You will need to add any repayments you made in the tax year for government or non-government assistance that reduced your pool.

You may also add the previous year’s pool balance of deductible expenditures that you are carrying forward and any SR&ED expenditure transfers or recaptures, if applicable.

If you have a negative pool balance

The pool of deductible SR&ED expenditures at the beginning of a tax year cannot be a negative amount. If there is a negative balance in the pool at the end of a tax year, you must bring it into that tax year’s net income.

This amount is also technically added to the pool to remove the negative amount and results in an opening balance of nil (zero).

Review the policy: Pool of Deductible SR&ED Expenditures Policy
