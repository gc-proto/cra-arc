How a progressive tax system works

The tax rates are split up into income brackets. A different tax rate applies to each bracket, but only to the income within that bracket.

The taxes you pay are based on which one or more of the brackets your income falls into. The higher tax rate does not apply to all your income.

The same brackets are used for all types of income, such as:

  • Employment income
  • Occasional earnings
  • Varied income (such as capital gains or rental income)

You determine your federal tax rate and provincial or territorial tax rate separately. Your combined tax rate would be the sum of the two individual rates but is not the rate used to calculate your taxes.
