Reduce the amount of taxes you owe

You can reduce the amount of tax you owe to the CRA at the end of the tax year by claiming amounts on your tax return and increasing deductions from your income.

  • Claim your deductions, credits, and expenses

    You may be able to pay less tax, which will increase the amount you keep.

    Examples of claims you may be able to make
    • Basic personal amount
    • Common credit
    • Common benefit
    • Common deduction

    For more, refer to: claiming deductions, credits, and expenses

  • Increase your tax deductions

    Increasing deductions from your income source can help you avoid owing at the end of the tax year.

    If you receive paycheques

    Generally, most employers will deduct both federal income tax and provincial or territorial income tax directly from your pay. You can choose to have more tax deducted from your pay by completing a TD1 form and providing it to your employer.

    Refer to: Form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Returns

    If you are an employer, refer to: Increasing income tax deducted at source

    If you receive CPP payments

    Taxes are not automatically deducted from your CPP payments but you can ask that federal tax be deducted.

    For more information, refer to: Deducting federal income tax from your CPP payments
