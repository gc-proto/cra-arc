Determine the employment status

Certain factors have to be considered when deciding if a worker is an employee or a self-employed individual.

These factors differ if the contract is formed in the province of Quebec or in any other province or territory. Usually, the province or territory where the contract was formed will decide which set of factors to use.

Generally, the contract is formed when and where acceptance is received by the offeror. In most cases, the offeror would be the payer.

Use the appropriate factors for your situation based on where the contract was formed, unless the contract states something else. For example, the contract can state that if the worker and payer disagree about the contents of the contract, the contract will be interpreted under Quebec law (civil code), even though the contract was formed in Ontario (common law).

This guidance does not replace a formal request for a CPP/EI ruling.

    • Self-employed and payer

    If you are unsure, either party can request a CPP/EI ruling (decision) from the CRA to have the status determined.
