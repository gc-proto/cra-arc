Determine if employment is pensionable and insurable

After you have determined employment status, the next step is to see if employment is pensionable under the CPP or insurable under the EIA, or both.

Steps

  1. Understand what pensionable employment is

    Pensionable employment under the Canada Pension Plan includes most employment in Canada under a contract of service (employer-employee relationship).

    Certain workers who are employees might not have pensionable employment.

    Example of a situation where employment is not pensionable

    First Nations workers
    The employment of a First Nations employee whose earnings are not included in taxable income under the Income Tax Act is not pensionable employment.

  2. Understand what insurable employment is

    Insurable employment under the Employment insurance legislation includes most employment in Canada under a contract of service (employer-employee relationship).

    Some employments outside Canada are also insurable.

    Certain workers who are not employees might be considered to be in insurable employment. Examples are barbers and hairdressers, or fishers.

    Certain workers who are employees might not be considered to be in insurable employment.

    Examples of situations where employment is not insurable

    Non-arm's length relationship

    If an employee is not dealing at arm's length with the employer, it is possible that their employment is not insurable under the Employment Insurance Act.

    Learn more: Not dealing at arm's length for purposes of the Employment Insurance Act (EIA)

    Control of shares
    If an employee of a corporation controls more than 40% of the voting shares of that corporation, their employment is not insurable; however, the employee can register with the CEIC for access to employment insurance special benefits.

    Determine if the employment is:

    • Pensionable and insurable
    • Only pensionable
    • Only insurable

    If you are unsure, either party can request a CPP/EI ruling (decision) from the CRA to have the status determined.
