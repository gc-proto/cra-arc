Employment status: Employee or self-employed

Workers usually fall into one of two categories:

  • An employee
    What is an employee

    Generally, an employee is hired to perform work under the direction and control of the person or business who hires them under a contract of service (employer/employee relationship). Under the terms and conditions of employment, an employee does not normally have the chance to make a profit or suffer a loss. An employee is not seen as operating their own business, but rather as being an integral part of the payer’s business. An employee may be entitled to benefits, be paid for vacation time, or be covered by a collective agreement.

  • A self-employed worker
    What is a self-employed worker

    A self-employed worker carries on their own business. The self-employed worker enters into a contract for services (business relationship) with the person or business (the payer) they provide the services to. In this relationship, the self-employed worker agrees to provide services, is free to choose the means of performing the contract and is not under the direction or control of the payer.

Use this guidance if you are:

  • A payer or employer
  • A worker

It will help you understand how to determine a worker's employment status.

Payers and workers can set their employment arrangement as they want. However, whether they choose an employee or self-employed status, they must make sure that the facts of their working relationship support that employment status.

Request a ruling

If you are not sure of a worker's employment status, you can ask the CRA for a CPP/EI ruling.
