Basic personal amount: Personal income tax

  • Federal tax and benefit return: Line 30000
  • Provincial or territorial Form 428: Line 58040
  • Tax year: 2024| Prior tax years: 2023 and earlier

The basic personal amount (BPA) is a non-refundable tax credit designed to ensure that you can earn a minimum amount of income before having to pay federal income tax.

How much you can claim

  • Federal amount for 2024

    The amount varies:

    Federal tax and benefit return Amount
    Federal
    • If your net income is $173,205 or less, claim $15,705
    • If your net income is between $173,206 and $246,751, complete the calculation using the Federal Worksheet to determine how much to claim.
    • If your net income is $246,752 or more, claim $14,156

How to claim

  1. Determine your allowable deduction

    • Federal deduction amount

      The federal deduction amount depends on your net income on line 23600 of your return.

    • Provincial or territorial deduction amounts

      The province or territory deduction amount depends on where you resided in on December 31, 2024.

  2. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    • Federal tax and benefit return

      On line 30000 of your Federal tax and benefit return, enter the allowable deduction amount.

    • Provincial or territorial Form 428

      On line 58040 of your provincial or territorial Form 428, enter the deduction amount if you resided in one of the following provinces or territories:

      • Alberta
      • BC
      • Alberta
      • New Brunswick
      • Northwest Territories
      • Nova Scotia
      • Nunavut
      • Ontario
      • Prince Edward Island
      • Saskatchewan
      • Yukon

  3. Keep your supporting documents

    Do not send any supporting documents when you file your tax return. Keep them in case the CRA asks to see them later.
