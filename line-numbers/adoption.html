Line 31300 - Adoption expenses

  • Federal tax return: Line 31300
  • Provincial or territorial tax return (if applicable): Line 58330
  • Tax year: 2024| Prior tax years: 2023 and earlier

You can claim an amount for eligible adoption expenses related to the adoption of a child.

Who is eligible

Start of question

You must meet all 3 criteria below. Check the boxes that apply.

  • You adopted a child
  • You have expenses related to the adoption
  • The adopted child is under 18 years of age at the time that the adoption order is issued or recognized by a government in Canada

  • You need all of the above to be eligible

    To be eligible to claim adoption expenses, you must meet all of the criteria above.

  • Based on your selections above:

    You may not meet all of the criteria

    To be eligible to claim adoption expenses, you must meet all of the criteria above.

  • Based on your selections above:

    You are eligible to claim adoption expenses

    You meet the criteria to claim eligible adoption expenses. You may be able to claim up to the maximum claim amounts for each adopted child.

Expenses you can claim

Eligible adoption expenses include:

  • Fees paid to an adoption agency licensed by a provincial or territorial government
  • Court costs and legal and administrative expenses related to an adoption order for the child
  • Reasonable and necessary travel and living expenses of the child and the adoptive parents
  • Document translation fees
  • Mandatory fees paid to a foreign institution
  • Mandatory expenses paid for the child's immigration
  • Any other reasonable expenses related to the adoption required by a provincial or territorial government or an adoption agency licensed by a provincial or territorial government

Adoption period start and end date

You may claim all of the eligible adoption expenses you incurred, but you must claim them in the tax year that includes the end of the adoption period for the child.

Start of adoption period

The adoption period starts at the earliest of the following dates:

  • When an application is made for registration with a provincial or territorial ministry responsible for adoption, or with an adoption agency licensed by a provincial or territorial government
  • When an application related to the adoption is made to a Canadian court
End of adoption period

The adoption period ends at the latest of the following dates:

  • When an adoption order is issued by, or recognized by, a government in Canada for that child
  • When the child first begins to live permanently with you

How much you can claim

The amount you can claim is the eligible adoption expenses you incurred minus any reimbursements you received, up to the maximum claim amounts.

If you are eligible to claim adoption expenses as a deduction on your federal tax return, you may also claim the same expenses on your province or territorial tax return.

The maximum federal amount is the same for all eligible individuals. The maximum provincial or territorial amount is based on where you resided on December 31 of the tax year when the adoption was finalized.

How to claim

  1. Determine your allowable deduction

    • Federal deduction amount

      Choose whichever amount is less:

      • Your calculated total eligible adoption expenses
      • The maximum federal amount for the tax year the adoption period ends

    • Provincial or territorial deduction amounts

      If you are eligible for the federal deduction, choose whichever amount is less:

      • Your calculated total eligible adoption expenses
      • The maximum amount for the province or territory where you resided on December 31 of the tax year the adoption period ends

    If there are 2 adoptive parents, they can split the amount. The total combined claim for eligible expenses for each child cannot be more than the amount before the split.

  2. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    You can only claim adoption expenses in the tax year that the adoption period ends, even if the adoption process took more than one year.

    • Federal tax return

      On line 31300 of your federal tax return, enter the allowable deduction amount.

    • Provincial or territorial tax return

      On line 58330 of your provincial or territorial Form 428, enter the deduction amount if you resided in one of the following provinces or territories:

      • Alberta
      • BC
      • Manitoba
      • Newfoundland and Labrador
      • Ontario
      • Yukon

  3. Keep your supporting documents

    Do not send any supporting documents when you file your tax return. Keep them in case the CRA asks to see them later.
