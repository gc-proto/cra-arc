Benefits, credits, and financial support: CRA and COVID-19

Financial support is available to Canadians during the COVID-19 pandemic, including new benefits and credits and changes to existing ones. You may be eligible for support via other financial programs.

File your tax return as soon as possible in order to receive the right benefit amounts and to continue benefit payments beyond September 2020. If your tax return is not assessed by early September 2020, your payments will stop in October 2020 and you may have to repay the amounts you received since July 2020.

Benefits, credits and support

Resuming CRA business activities

Some CRA programs and services will be returning in September 2020. As a result, you may receive a call or letter from us with a specific call to action.

Benefit reviews by the CRA

Benefit reviews are on hold until further notice. If you received a letter from CRA that includes a deadline to respond or asks for documents, you do not need to take any action right now. CRA will contact you again when the situation changes.

Canada Child Benefit (CCB)

Individuals who are presently receiving the Canada child benefit (CCB) will continue to receive these payments until the end of September. Payment amounts will be based on their 2018 tax return(s) if their 2019 income tax return is not filed or assessed by September 2020.

GST/HST credit

Individuals who are presently receiving the GST/HST credit will continue to receive these payments until the end of September. Payment amounts will be based on their 2018 tax return(s) if their 2019 income tax return is not filed or assessed by September 2020.

Registered Retirement Income Funds (RRIFs)

The minimum required withdrawal for RRIFs has been reduced by 25% for 2020.

Old Age Security Return of Income (OASRI)

Old Age Security payments to non-resident seniors have been temporarily extended even if their 2019 OASRI has not been assessed. Submit your 2019 OASRI before October 1, 2020 to continue your payments.

Sign up for Direct Deposit

Direct deposit is the quickest and most reliable way to get benefit and credit payments. Sign up through My Account or through your financial institution. This includes banks, credit unions and trust companies.
