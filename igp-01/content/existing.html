Evaluate existing content - CRA instructional guidance product

Before creating new content or updating existing content, begin by reviewing what is already on Canada.ca. Don’t assume your content is unique.

Find what already exists on your topic

When you review what exists first, you will understand the frustrations of your users. To review what exists, do a manual content inventory and audit:

Steps

  1. Understand why a user would use your content

    Beyond the corporate goal of creating the content, you will need know what value it brings to the user by determining user tasks.

  2. Be aware of keywords a user would search for when looking for your content

    Identify keywords your audience uses to find this type of content. Use these keywords to search Canada.ca to find related pages. This will give you a sense of the content holdings and what pages the user might be directed to from search, that may compete with your content.

  3. Create an inventory of related or overlaping content

    Document what content you find on the topic. This research will help you determine:

    • what relevant content already exists
    • what content is out of date and should be removed or updated (including titles, metadata, or links)
    • what content is missing and needs to be created
    • where should the new content be added to meet users’ needs
    • which government areas you need to talk to in order to improve the content experience on this topic

If you need help with content audits, talk to user researchers and other colleagues who can provide you with site data and analytics or direct you to existing data and research.

Identify redundant, outdated and trivial (ROT) content

Identify redundant content

Content may be redundant if:

  • The program/organization is not the authoritative source for the content.
  • The information is duplicated elsewhere within our pages, or on another Department's site.

Consider removing this type of content. In rare cases, it may be archived online

Identify outdated content

Content may be outdated if:

  • There are orphan pages, which means there is no way to navigate to the page online or the content no longer has an owner/author.
  • There are outdated pages. For instance, time-sensitive media advisories, irrelevant topics, discontinued programs, past events.
  • Content that is written in the wrong tense (e.g. "will be" for a date in the past).

Consider removing this type of content. In rare cases, it may be archived online

Identify trivial content

Content may be trivial if:

  • Content is not relevant or informative for the audience.
  • Web analytics show low or no page views or visits.

Consider removing this type of content. In rare cases, it may be archived online

Identify content for archiving online

You may need to keep ROT content online if:

  • There is a legislative requirement to keep it publicly available (e.g. it is a proactive disclosure).
  • It records an important decision.
  • There is a risk associated with removing it.
