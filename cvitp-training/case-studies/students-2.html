Background information

Situation

Antoine is working full time and pursuing a part-time degree at the University of Canada which granted him a scholarship.  Antoine received a T2202 from the school. He does not wish to transfer his tuition fees.

Antoine’s 2021 notice of assessment (NOA) indicates that his Canada training credit limit (CTCL) for 2022 is $250. He wants to claim this amount and he does not have any unused tuition amounts from prior years. He also wishes to claim $300 on program-related material he paid for during his part-time degree.

Takeaway points

Steps to follow

  1. Review their background information and the required slips
  2. From the Interview setup, check the box next to Employment income and employment insurance benefits (T4, T4E/RL-6) in the Employment and other benefits section. Also, check the boxes next to Pension income, other income and split pension income, COVID-19 benefits (T4A, T4A(OAS), T4A(P), T4A-RCA, T4RSP, T4RIF, T1032) in the Pension section and Student, education, textbooks, student loans, and Canada training credit in the Student section
  3. Select T4 and employment income from the left side menu, add the T4 slip and enter the information from Antoine’s T4 slip into the system
  4. Select T4A and pension income in the left side menu and click the + sign next to T4A – Pension, retirement, annuity, and other income (COVID-19 benefits), then select [105] Scholarships, bursaries, fellowship from the drop-down menu under OTHER INFORMATION (COVID-19 benefits) and in the amount field next to it, enter the amount from Antoine’s T4A slip
  5. Click Tuition, education, student loans in the left side menu and click the + sign next to T2202 Tuition and enrolment certificate* (TL11A and TL11C)(line 32300)
  6. In the Current year tuition amount section, enter the amounts from Antoine’s T2202 – Enrolment and tuition certificate into the software
  7. Click Tuition, education, student loans in the left side menu and click the + sign next to Part-time program details if you received scholarship, fellowship, and bursary income to be included at line 13010
  8. Add the amount paid for tuition shown on the T2202 and the cost of materials, then enter the result in Tuition fees and costs of part-time education program-related material paid for current year
  9. Click Tuition, education, student loans in the left side menu and click the + sign next to Canada training credit (CTC). Enter the CTCL amount from Antoine’s 2021 NOA into the field Canada training credit limit (CTCL) for 2021. Select Yes to answer the question Do you want to claim the Canada training credit in the current year?
  10. Leave the field Enter the Canada training credit you are claiming blank

Since Antoine is a part-time student, his scholarship exemption is equal to the tuition fees he paid ($3,000), plus the costs of program-related materials ($300), plus the $500 basic scholarship exemption, for a total of $3,800. Since the amount of this calculation is greater than the scholarship he received (3,000), there is no amount reported on line 13010. The amount of tuition fees that Antoine can claim on line 32300 is reduced by the amount of the Canada training credit (CTC) he is claiming this year. The $250 CTC amount is reported on line 45350 of his return.
