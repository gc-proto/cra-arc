Become a CVITP volunteer

Community Volunteer Income Tax Program (CVITP) volunteers play an essential role in helping eligible individuals with modest incomes and simple tax situations receive the benefit and credit payments they are entitled to.

As a CVITP volunteer, you can choose to either:

  • prepare tax returns as a tax preparer
  • support the clinic’s operations in a support role

Quebec residents

If you are a resident of the province of Quebec and interested in helping individuals file their taxes for free, please visit the Income Tax Assistance – Volunteer Program.

Free training

Although it’s helpful, you don’t need experience doing tax returns to volunteer. All CVITP volunteers have access to free training that includes web-based training on how to do taxes, use tax software, and determine their responsibilities. Online training can be supplemented with in-person or virtual sessions.

Types of tax clinics

Participating organizations can choose to host tax clinics in person, virtually, or both. CVITP volunteers can help taxpayers complete their tax returns through:

in-person clinics
Meeting with taxpayers face-to-face
virtual clinics
preparing tax returns remotely via videoconference, phone, or through a drop-off process

Registration period

  • The program runs year-round but clinics are busiest during tax season (late February to early May).
  • To avoid delays, register starting in late October to be ready for tax season.

Volunteer Identification Number (VIN)

The Volunteer Identification Number (VIN) is a unique and permanent, eight-digit identification number. It will remain the same for a volunteer year after year, even if there has been a break in program participation. Any volunteer approved to participate in the CVITP will be assigned a VIN and retain it for the duration of their participation in the program.

This is a feature that was implemented in mid-May 2024. Should you be approved to participate with the CVITP, you will receive an email informing you of your approval and your VIN will be included in the email. Returning volunteers will be required to enter their VIN when registering for the program.
