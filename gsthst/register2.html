When to register for and start charging the GST/HST

Effective June 17, 2024, individuals with a SIN that starts with the number 9 can now use the Business Registration Online service to register and instantly obtain a business number and a GST/HST account.

Generally, you cannot register for a GST/HST account if you provide only exempt supplies .

R13 You may not need to register for a GST/HST account number

  • The direct seller does not have a CRA approval

      Before a business can qualify as a direct seller, they have to apply for and receive approval from the CRA to use the alternate collection method. A condition of their approval is that they elect with their independent sales contractors to account for the GST/HST on their behalf. If you believe you are an independent sales contractor for a direct seller, contact them for a copy of the election. Otherwise, you may need to register for a GST/HST account number.

    You may also need to register for a GST/HST account number if you make taxable supplies in any other situation.

    Continue to use this interactive content to find out if you have to register for a GST/HST account number. When determining if you are required to register for a GST/HST account number, do not include any earnings from which an election with a direct seller applies.

  • The direct seller has a CRA approval

     You do not have to register for a GST/HST account number if your only supplies are sales of products of a direct seller. Do not continue to use this interactive content.


    Direct sellers who sell their products to independent sales contractors may apply to the CRA to use the alternate collection method (ACM). Once approved, the direct seller or an approved distributor accounts for the GST/HST on the suggested retail price of exclusive products sold to their independent sales contractors. Independent sales contractor in this situation do not have to register for the GST/HST because they do not include revenues from their sales of exclusive products in their calculation to determine if they are small suppliers.

    Learn more: GST/HST Info Sheet GI-126, Direct Selling Industry - The Alternate Collection Method for Independent Sales Contractors.

R14 You may not need to register for a GST/HST account number

  • The network seller does not have a CRA approval

    Before a business can qualify as a network seller, they have to apply for and receive approval from the CRA to use the network seller’s method. A condition of their approval is that they elect with their sales representatives so that the GST/HST would not apply to the commissions they pay to the sales representatives. If you believe you are a sales representative for a network seller, contact them for a copy of the election. Otherwise, you may need to register for a GST/HST account number.

    You may also need to register for a GST/HST account number if you make taxable supplies in any other situations.

    Continue to use this interactive content to find out if you have to register for a GST/HST account number. When determining if you are required to register for a GST/HST account number, do not include any earnings from which an election with a network seller applies.

  • The network seller has a CRA approval

     You do not have to register for a GST/HST account number if your only supply is arranging for the sale of select products of a network seller. Do not continue to use this interactive content.


    Network sellers who sell their products to consumers through sales representatives may apply to the CRA for approval to use the network sellers method (NSM). Once approved, the network seller accounts for the GST/HST when they sell their products directly to consumers through sales representatives who receive commissions for arranging the sales. The commissions paid to sales representatives for arranging for the sale of the network seller's select products would not be subject to the GST/HST and would not be used for determining whether sales representatives are small suppliers. Sales representatives in this situation do not have to register for the GST/HST.

    Learn more, including how to apply for approval to use the network sellers method: GST/HST Info Sheet GI-052, Direct Selling Industry - The Network Sellers Method for Network Sellers and Sales Representatives.

Sole proprietor, partnership or corporation

Sole proprietor, partnership or corporation

Steps

  1. If you are an employee, do not use this webpage.

  2. Determine if you are in a special situation

    • You are in a special situation

      If your only commercial activity  is the sale of real property, other than in the course of a business, you are a small supplier.

      Example

      A doctor's practice consists of making exempt supplies of health care services. The doctor decides on a one-time basis to buy land which includes a commercial building as the doctor has heard that there will be an increase in demand for this type of property in the next six months. The doctor sells the land and building for a profit after holding it for seven months.

      These transactions would be viewed as an adventure in the nature of trade rather than carrying on a business. The doctor's sale of the property is taxable and he must include GST/HST.

      The doctor is not required to register for a GST/HST account number for the purpose of reporting the GST/HST collectible on this sale, however he may decide to register voluntarily to claim ITCs.

      Learn more: 19-4-1 Commercial Real Property - Sales and Rentals

      You may still have to charge the tax on your sale of real property  if the purchaser is not registered for the GST/HST.

      Learn more: Charge and collect the tax

      You are not eligible to claim ITCs. If you register voluntarily, you may be eligible to claim ITCs for the GST/HST you had to pay for any costs related to acquiring, improving, leasing or operating the property prior to your sale.

      Learn more: Input tax credits

      You must file GST/HST returns if you charged GST/HST on the sale.

      Learn more: Complete and file a GST/HST return

      You must remit (pay) the net GST/HST payable if you charged GST/HST on the sale.

      Learn more: Remit (pay) the tax you collected

    • If you are not in a special situation, continue to: Determine if you are a small supplier.

  3. If you are a sole proprietor, a partnership or a corporation and you do not exceed the $30,000  threshold over 4 consecutive calendar quarters, you are a small supplier.

    You must continue to monitor your sales to determine when you exceed the $30,000 threshold.

    • If you are no longer a small supplier, continue to: Determine when to register, charge, file and remit.

    • $30,000 threshold over 4 consecutive calendar quarter
      $30,000 threshold over 4 consecutive calendar quarter

      You have to register for a GST/HST account number because you exceeded the $30,000 threshold over 4 consecutive calendar quarter .

      You are no longer a small supplier at the end of the month following the quarter in which you exceeded the $30,000 threshold. The effective date of registration is no later than the beginning of the month after you are no longer a small supplier.

      Example 1 – Did not exceed the $30,000 threshold amount in 4 consecutive calendar quarters

      On February 1, 2020, you started a business that sells candles. You made the following sales:

      Calculation
      Quarter Amount
      First quarter (January 1 to March 31) $2,000
      Second quarter (April 1 to June 30) $10,000
      Third quarter (July 1 to September 30) $12,000
      Fourth quarter (October 1 to December 31) $5,000
      Total $29,000

      You are a small supplier in 2020 because you did not exceed the $30,000 threshold in four consecutive calendar quarters. You are still a small supplier in the first quarter of 2021, unless you exceed the $30,000 threshold in that one quarter.

      Example 2 – Exceed the $30,000 threshold in two consecutive calendar quarters

      On November 1, 2019, you opened a retail store. You made the following taxable sales:

      Calculation
      Quarter Amount
      First quarter (October 1 to December 31) $25,000
      Second quarter (January 1 to March 31) $25,000
      Total $50,000

      In April 2020, your sales totaled $ 8,000.

      Your first sale after April was on May 1, 2020.

      • You stopped being a small supplier on April 30, 2020, because that day is the end of the month after the quarter you exceed the $30,000 threshold.
      • Your effective date of registration is no later than May 1, 2020. That day is when you made your first supply after you stopped being a small supplier.
      • You have to start charging GST/HST on your date of registration.
      • You will have to register within 29 days of your effective date of registration.
      Example 3 – Exceed the $30,000 threshold amount within the previous four consecutive calendar quarters

      On June 1, 2019, you started a business selling baseball caps. You made the following sales:

      Calculation
      Quarter Amount
      First quarter (April 1 to June 30) $2,000
      Second quarter (July 1 to September 30) $10,000
      Third quarter (October 1 to December 31) $12,000
      Fourth quarter (January 1 to March 31) $8,000
      Total $32,000

      In April 2020, your sales totaled $ 4,500.

      Your first sale after April was on May 2, 2020.

      • You stopped being a small supplier on April 30, 2020, because that day is the end of the month after the quarter you exceed the $30,000 threshold.
      • Your effective date of registration is no later than May 1, 2020. That day is when you made your first supply after you stopped being a small supplier.
      • You have to start charging the GST/HST on your date of registration.
      • You will have to register within 29 days of your effective date of registration.
    • $30,000 threshold in a single calendar quarter
      $30,000 threshold in a single calendar quarter

      You have to register for a GST/HST account number because you exceeded the $30,000 threshold in a single calendar quarter .

      You are no longer a small supplier. The effective date of registration is no later than the day of the supply that made you exceed the $30,000 threshold.

      Example – Exceed the $30,000 threshold in 1 quarter

      On January 1, 2020, you started a consulting business. You made the following revenue from your taxable services.

      • Your effective date of registration is no later than the day of the supply that made you exceed the $30,000 threshold in the quarter.
      • You have to start charging the GST/HST on your date of registration, including on the sale that made you exceed the $30,000 threshold.

Charity or public institution

Charity or public institution

What is charity or public institution

Steps

  1. Confirm if you are a branch or division

    If you are a branch or a division, you cannot register for a GST/HST account number separately.

    However, if your public service body, including a charity  or public institution  has branches or divisions, you may apply to have each branch or division with $50,000 or less in taxable supplies designated as a small supplier division.

    A public service body (PSB), including a charity or public institution, you may apply for its branches or divisions to be designated as small supplier.

    Learn more: GST31 Application by a Public Service Body to Have Branches or Divisions Treated As Eligible Small Supplier Divisions

    • If you are a branch or a division, do not continue to next step.

    • If you are not a branch or a division, continue to: Determine if you are a small supplier.

  2. Determine if you are a small supplier

    1. $250,000 gross revenue test

      If you are a charity or public institution in one of the following situation, you are a small supplier under the $250,000 gross revenue  test:

      • You are not yet operating
      • You are in your first fiscal year  of operation
      • You are in your second fiscal year of operation and your gross revenue from your first fiscal year is $250,000 or less
      • You are in your third fiscal year of operation and your gross revenue from either or both of your previous 2 fiscal years is $250,000 or less
    2. $50,000 taxable supplies test

      If you are no longer a supplier under the $250,000 gross revenue test, you are a small supplier under the $50,000 taxable supplies test if you do not exceed the $50,000 threshold over 4 consecutive calendar quarters.

    • If you are a small supplier, you do not have to register for a GST/HST account number.

      Voluntary registration

      You may choose to register voluntarily. If you register voluntarily, your effective date of registration is generally the day you request your GST/HST account number or 30 days before that day.

      Learn more: Register voluntarily for a GST/HST account number

      Claiming input tax credits (ITC)

      You may be eligible for GST/HST rebates that allow charities to recover a percentage of the GST/HST they pay on their purchases and expenses.

      Learn more: GST/HST Information for Charities

    • If you are no longer a small supplier, continue to: Determine when to register, charge, file and remit.

Taxi operator or a commercial ride-sharing driver

What is a taxi operator or commercial ride-sharing driver

A taxi business means a business carried on in Canada of transporting passengers by taxi for fares that are regulated under the laws of Canada or a province. A driver who supplies taxable commercial ride-sharing services on or after July 1, 2017, is considered to be a taxi business for GST/HST purposes.

You are usually self-employed if you are in one of the following situations:

  • You own your taxicab/motor vehicle
  • You lease a taxicab/motor vehicle from an owner for a flat fee, either on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis
  • You lease the taxicab/motor vehicle from an owner for a percentage of fares

Steps

  1. Determine if you are self-employed

    You can ask the CRA for ruling if you are not sure whether you are self-employed or an employee.

    Learn more: How to get a CPP/EI ruling.

    If you are an employee, do not continue to next step.

  2. Registering

    If you are a self-employed taxi operator or a commercial ride-sharing driver, you have to register for the GST/HST even if you are not a small supplier.

    You have to register for a GST/HST account number as of the date your business began as a taxi operator or a commercial ride-sharing driver.

    Learn more: Register for a GST/HST account number

  3. Charging

    You have to charge the GST/HST as of the date your business began.

    Learn more: Charge and collect the tax

  4. Claiming input tax credits (ITC)

    You may be eligible for to claim input tax credits for the GST/HST you pay on your purchase and expenses.

    Learn more: Input tax credits

  5. Filing

    You must file GST/HST returns.

    Learn more: Complete and file a GST/HST return

  6. Remitting (paying)

    You must remit (pay) the net GST/HST payable.

    Learn more: Remit (pay) the tax you collected

What is a non-resident business

Steps

  1. Determine if you are in a special situation

    If you are in one of the following special situation, you have to register for a GST/HST account number even if you are not a small supplier:

    • You sponsor (host) a convention  in Canada and more than 25% of the attendees are residents of Canada
    • You make taxable  sales, leases, or other supplies (including zero-rated  supplies) of admissions in Canada for a place of amusement, a seminar, an activity, or an event held in Canada
    Security deposit for a non-resident business

    Generally, you have to provide a security deposit if you register for a GST/HST account number and one of the following applies:

    • You do not have a permanent establishment  in Canada
    • You make supplies in Canada only through another person's fixed place of business

    Exception

    You have to estimate the amount of your taxable sales, leases or other supplies in Canada. Security is not required if your estimate is $100,000 annually or less and your annual net tax will be between $3,000 remittable and $3,000 refundable.

    The security deposit would be:

    • For the first year the amount of security is 50% of your estimated net tax, whether positive or negative, during the 12-month period after you register
    • For the following years, the amount of security is 50% of your actual net tax, whether positive or negative, for the last 12-month period

    The maximum security would be $1 million. The minimum is $5,000.

    Your security may be in the form of one of the following:

    • Cash
    • Certified cheque
    • Money order
    • A qualifying bond

    Cash or cash equivalents (such as a certified cheque or a money order) may be used to pay other outstanding debts to the CRA at the time the security is released. CRA does not accept non-transferable bonds such as Canada Savings Bonds.

    • If you are in a special situation, you have to register for a GST/HST account number. Do not continue to next step.

      Learn more:

      • How to register and what you need before you register: Open or manage an account – Register
      • How to make an enquiry if you are a non-resident business: Non-resident GST/HST enquiries

    • If you are not in a special situation, continue to: Determine if you are carrying on business in Canada

  2. Determine if you are carrying on business in Canada

    • If you are a non-resident business and you do not carry on business in Canada (except if you are in a special situation), you cannot register for a GST/HST account number.

    • If you are a non-resident business and you make taxable sales, leases, or other supplies (including zero-rated supplies) in Canada in the course of carrying on business activity in Canada, you have to register for a GST/HST account number if you are not a small supplier.

      Determine if you are a small supplier

      To determine if you are a small supplier (as you would for any Canadian businesses), continue to:

      • Sole proprietor, partnership or corporation
      • Charity or public institution
      • Public service body that is not a charity or public institution

      To determine if you are a small supplier if you are a digital economy business, continue to:

      • Cross-border digital products and services
      • Supply of qualifying goods
      • Platform-based short-term accommodation

Meaning of the term taxable supplies

Examples of taxable supplies are:

  • Sales of new housing
  • Sales and rentals of commercial real property
  • Sales and leases of automobiles
  • Car repairs
  • Soft drinks, candies, and potato chips
  • Hotel and other short-term accommodation
  • Clothing and footwear
  • Taxi operator or a commercial ride-sharing driver
  • Legal and accounting services
  • Franchises
  • Barber and hairstylist services

Meaning of the term zero-rated supplies (taxable at the rate of 0%)

Examples of zero-rated supplies are:

  • Basic groceries such as milk, bread, and vegetables
  • Agricultural products such as grain, raw wool, and died tobacco leaves
  • Most farm livestock
  • Most fishery products such as fish for human consumption
  • Prescription drugs and drug-dispensing services
  • Certain medical devices such as hearing aids and artificial teeth
  • Feminine hygiene products (as of July 1, 2015)
  • Exports (most goods and services for which you charge and collect the GST/HST in Canada, are zero-rated when exported)

Meaning of the terms: Direct seller or a network seller

Businesses in the direct selling industry sell their products directly to consumers through sales representatives or to independent sales contractors who, in turn, sell the products to purchasers. Their business structure is usually based on the following models:

  • Direct sellers

    Direct sellers sell their products to distributors and independent sales contractors who, in turn, sell them to purchasers.

    Direct sellers may apply for approval to use the alternate collection method (ACM), another method for accounting for the GST/HST on their sales of exclusive products.

    Under the ACM, direct sellers charge and account for the GST/HST on the suggested retail price of the exclusive products as if they had made the sales directly to purchasers.

    For more information, including how to apply for approval to use the ACM, see GST/HST Info Sheet GI-125, Direct Selling Industry - The Alternate Collection Method for Approved Direct Sellers and Approved Distributors.

  • Network sellers

    Network sellers sell their products directly to consumers through sales representatives who receive commissions for arranging the sales.

    Network sellers who meet certain conditions may apply for approval to use the network sellers method.

    For more information, including how to apply for approval to use the network sellers method, see GST/HST Info Sheet GI-052, Direct Selling Industry - The Network Sellers Method for Network Sellers and Sales Representatives.

What to include and not include

Include the total of all revenues from worldwide taxable supplies from all your businesses and those of your associates.

Do not include:

  • Supplies of financial services
  • Sales of capital property
  • Goodwill from the sale of a business

What to include and not include

Include in your gross revenue amount:

  • Income from a business, or from an adventure or concern in the nature of trade
  • Gifts and donations
  • Grants, subsidies, forgivable loans and other assistance
  • Property and investment income
  • Any amount that is a capital gain from the disposition of property for income tax purposes
  • Other revenue of any kind whatever during the year, including revenue from both taxable and exempt supplies

You also have to include the gross revenue of your organization as a whole (the legal entity).

Do not include capital loss from the disposition of property for income tax purposes.
