Find out if you need to pay GST/HST by instalments

You need to determine if you need to pay the GST/HST by instalments in your second fiscal year because special rules may apply to you if you have registered for GST/HST in the current fiscal year.

If you are an annual filer and your net tax for your previous fiscal year is $3,000 or more, you may have to make quarterly instalment payments in the current fiscal year.

What happens if your estimate is wrong

If you base your instalment payments on an estimate and the instalment payments you make are less than the amount you should have paid, the CRA will charge instalment interest on the difference.

   Filing and remitting

When you file your GST/HST return at the end of the year, deduct the instalment payments you made throughout the year.

Generally, if the instalments you paid are less than your net tax, you have to remit the difference. If the instalments you paid are more than your net tax, you can claim the difference as a refund.

You can also choose to make your instalment payments based on your estimate that your net tax will be next year. In this case, each of your instalment payments would be $1,000. We will charge instalment interest if your net tax is more than this amount.

If you base your instalment payments on your prior year actual net tax and you make those payments on time, we will not charge instalment interest, even if your net tax for the year is more than the instalments you made.

Special rules for first time filers explains how to determine if you need to pay the GST/HST by instalments in your second fiscal year.

If you are an annual filer and your net tax for your previous fiscal year is $3,000 or more, you may have to make quarterly instalment payments in the current fiscal year.

If your business has branches or divisions that file separate returns, the threshold limit of $3,000 applies to the total net tax for the whole business, including all branches and divisions.
