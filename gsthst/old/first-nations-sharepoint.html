GST/HST and First Nations peoples

Content has been updated to remove the word Indian where it is not a direct reference to the Indian Act, including improving clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

Meaning of the terms “First Nations individual” and “First Nations band”

On this webpage, the term “First Nations individual” is used to describe an individual who is registered or entitled to be registered under the Indian Act. The term “First Nations band” is used to describe the primary levels of Indigenous government (band councils and tribal councils)

We recognize that many First Nations people in Canada prefer not to describe themselves as Indians. However, we sometimes use the term “Indian” because it has a legal meaning in the Indian Act.

Generally, everyone has to pay tax in Canada, except when the customer is a First Nations individual , First Nations band  or Band-empowered entity  that is eligible for GST/HST relief because of section 87 of the Indian Act.

Steps

  1. Determine if the First Nations Goods and Services Tax (FNGST) or the First Nations Tax (FNT) applies

    Some band councils or other governing bodies of First Nations have signed self-government agreements with the Government of Canada that include withdrawal from tax relief under section 87 of the Indian Act.

    This webpage is not for you if you want to find out how the FNGST or the FNT applies. The tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act does not apply to settlement lands.

    Learn more how FNGST or the FNT applies:

    • First Nations Goods and Services Tax
    • First Nations Tax

    If FNGST or FNT does not apply, continue to Step 2 - Confirm if the business is a GST/HST registrant.

  2. Confirm if the business is a GST/HST registrant

    • If the business is not a GST/HST registrant, they generally do not charge the GST/HST.

      Learn more about who needs to register, or if a business can register voluntarily: When to register for and start charging the GST/HST

      Do not continue to next step.

    • If the business is a GST/HST registrant, they generally have to charge the GST/HST.

      Continue to Step 3 - Get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity.

  3. Get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity

    The vendor must get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity for the tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act to apply.

    Learn more from Indigenous Services Canada: About Indian status

    Customer

    Documents the vendor must obtain from the customer
    First Nations individual 

    The vendor must get proof that the individual is registered with Indigenous Services Canada from one of the following:

    • Secure Certificate of Indian Status
    • Certificate of Indian Status
    • Original Temporary Confirmation of Registration Document (TCRD) (a photocopy of this document is not acceptable)

    Learn more from Indigenous Services Canada: Is your status card valid
    First Nations band 

    The vendor must get as proof an original certificate showing one of the following:

    • Goods are being acquired by a First Nations band
    • Services are being acquired for band management activities  or for real property on a reserve
    Band-empowered entity 

    The vendor must get as proof an original certificate showing one of the following:

    • Goods are being acquired by either:
      • An unincorporated band-empowered entity
      • An incorporated band-empowered entity for band management activities  or for real property on a reserve
    • Services are being acquired for band management activities  or for real property on a reserve

    • If the customer does not provide the required documentation, the customer does not meet the conditions for the GST/HST relief.

      The vendor has to charge the GST/HST.

      Continue to: Charging the GST/HST.

    • If the customer provides the required documentation, the customer may meet the conditions for the GST/HST relief.

      Continue to Step 4 - Determine if the GST/HST applies depending on the situation.

  4. Determine if the GST/HST applies depending on the situation

    The vendor must determine if the GST/HST applies to sales made to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities in the following situations:

    Type of sale How GST/HST applies
    Goods - On a reserve

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer bought goods on a reserve .

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Goods - Off a reserve in Canada (delivered)

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer bought goods off a reserve in Canada.

    • The vendor or a company/individual contracted by the vendor (for example: postal services, shipping carriers and couriers) will deliver the goods to a reserve.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    The GST/HST applies if the customer contracts for the delivery of the goods.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 
    Goods - Off a reserve (remote store)

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer bought goods off a reserve and the vendor qualifies and operates as a remote store.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 

    What is a remote store

    Generally, a remote store refers to a store in one of the following situations:

      • The store is located more than 350 kilometres from the nearest community with a population of 5,000 or more
      • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 50% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities
      • Surface transportation is not available year-round on paved or gravelled roads linking the store to the nearest community
      • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 50% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities
      • The store is within 10 kilometres of a reserve
      • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 90% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities

    A store in a remote location but that doesn't meet the sales condition because it does not have a sales history may still qualify as a remote store based on a reasonable expectation of such sales. An example of a store without a sales history is a newly opened store.

    If the vendor qualifies and decides to operate as a remote store, each year, they have to let their tax services office know how they are operating.

    Learn more on how to contact the tax services office: Find a CRA address
    Goods - Outside Canada (delivered)

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer bought goods outside Canada.

    • The non-resident vendor or a company/individual contracted by the vendor (for example: postal services, shipping carriers and couriers) will deliver the goods to a reserve in Canada.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    However, the GST/HST on goods imported into Canada is collected by the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) even if, after importation, the property is delivered to a reserve by the vendor or company/individual, or by Canada Post. The non-resident vendor can recover the GST/HST by claiming an ITC on their GST/HST return.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 
    Intangible personal property (IPP)

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer bought IPP  that can be used only on a reserve

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    IPP is considered to be situated on a reserve where there are sufficient factors to connect it to a reserve.

    Examples - GST/HST does not apply 
    Services - Entirely on a reserve

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer paid for a service performed entirely on a reserve

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 

    Example - GST/HST applies 
    Services - Off a reserve (band management activities)

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer is a First Nations band or band-empowered entity and they paid for a service for use in band management activities

    • The service is not related to purchases of transportation, meals, short-term accommodations, or entertainment.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 
    Services - Related to transportation

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer paid for a transportation service.

    • Both the origin and destination are on a reserve.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 

    Example - GST/HST applies 
    Services - Related to real property

    The GST/HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer paid for a service performed off a reserve.

    • The service is related to real property interests on a reserve.

    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    Example - GST/HST does not apply 

    Example - GST/HST applies 
    Goods and services - In Ontario

    The 8% provincial part of the HST does not apply if all of the following apply:

    • The customer paid for qualifying goods or services off-reserve in Ontario.
    • The customer is a resident of Ontario or a resident of the Akwesasne reserve in Canada.
    • The customer presented the required documentation to the vendor.

    If all of the above apply, only the 5% federal part of the HST applies.

    Learn more about the Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate from Ontario Ministry of Finance: HST: Ontario First Nations rebate

  5. Charging the GST/HST

    • The vendor does not charge the GST/HST to the customer if all of the following apply:

      • The situation meets all conditions for GST/HST relief
      • The customer provides the required documentation to the vendor

    • The vendor has to charge the GST/HST to the customer if any of the following apply:

      • The situation does not meet all conditions for GST/HST relief
      • The customer did not provide the required documentation to the vendor
      How to recover the GST/HST

      Depending on the situation, the customer may recover the GST/HST they paid:

      GST/HST charged in error

      If the customer is charged the GST/HST in error, the customer can apply for a rebate of the GST/HST they paid that they should not have paid for property or services purchased on or delivered to a reserve.

      Learn more: Reason code 1A – Amounts paid in error for property or services purchased on or delivered to a reserve

      Eligible travel expenses incurred by a band-empowered entity

      Eligible travel expenses incurred by a band-empowered entity

      A band-empowered entity may be entitled to recover the GST/HST paid eligible travel expenses incurred or reimbursed to their employees or officials.

      Learn more: Reason code 8 – Indian band, tribal council, or band-empowered entity

      8% provincial part of the HST charged in error (Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate)

      If the vendor charged at the full 13% rate, the customer can apply for a rebate for the 8% Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate with the Ontario Ministry of Finance.

      Qualifying non-profit organization or a selected public service body

      If the customer is considered a qualifying non-profit organization  or a selected public service body , they may be entitled to recover the GST/HST paid on services.

      Learn more:

      • Partial recovery of the GST or the federal part of the HST on line 306, Form GST66, Application for GST/HST Public Service Bodies' Rebate and GST Self-Government Refund
      • Partial recovery of the provincial part of the HST, Form RC7066-SCH, Provincial Schedule - GST/HST Public Service Bodies' Rebate

      Learn more: Charge and collect the tax

  6. Reporting on the GST/HST return

    The following amounts must be reported on the GST/HST return depending on the following situation:

    • GST/HST relief applied to the sale

      If GST/HST relief is applied to the sale, the following amounts must be reported:

      • Report the sale on line 101.
      • Do not report any GST/HST charged on line 105.
      • Claim eligible ITCs for the GST/HST you paid on expenses related to the supply on line 108 (even if the sale is eligible for GST/HST relief).
    • GST/HST charged to the sale

      If the GST/HST is charged to the sale, the following amounts must be reported:

      • Report the sale on line 101.
      • Report the GST/HST charged on line 105.
      • Claim eligible ITCs for the GST/HST you paid on expenses related to the supply on line 108.
    • GST/HST relief does not apply to the sale, but eligible for the Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate

      If GST/HST relief does not apply to the sale, but eligible for the Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate, the following amounts must be reported:

      • Report the sale on line 101.
      • Report the HST at the rate of 13% on the sale on line 105.
      • Claim eligible ITCs for the GST/HST you paid on expenses related to the supply on line 108.

      • The vendor can apply for a rebate of the 8% provincial part of the HST that was credited at the point-of-sale using one of the following methods:

        • GST/HST NETFILE
        • My Business Account, select "File a return"
        • Form GST189, General Application for Rebate of GST/HST, using Reason code 23 – Ontario First Nations point-of-sale rebate
      • Report the amount included on the rebate application on line 111.

    Learn more: Complete a GST/HST return

  7. Records to keep

    The vendor must keep the required documentation that the customer provided as proof that the GST/HST relief under the Indian Act applies in case the CRA requests it.

    Information to include

    First Nations individual

    Record on the invoice or sales receipt, the 10-digit registration number. The cardholder information is displayed on the front of the card.

    If the customer presents a Temporary Confirmation of Registration Document, the 10-digit registration number and the expiration date must be included.

    First Nations band

    The following is an example of a certificate that must be kept with the invoice or sales receipt:

    This is to certify that the goods or services being acquired by [insert the name of band] are for band management activities (if applicable) or for real property on the reserve. This supply will not be subject to the goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST).



    Signature of the authorized officer: __________________________
    Date: ____________________


    Title of the signing officer: __________________________

    Band-empowered entity

    The following is an example of a certificate that must be kept with the invoice or sales receipt:

    This is to certify that the goods or services being acquired by [insert the name of band-empowered entity] are for band management activities or for real property on a reserve. This supply will not be subject to the goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST).



    Signature of the authorized officer: __________________________
    Date: ____________________


    Title of the signing officer: __________________________

    Online sales and sales made over the phone

    For online sales and sales made over the phone, the customer must send a copy later of the status card or TCRD electronically to be eligible for GST/HST relief.

    Goods delivered to a reserve

    The vendor must also keep proof that the goods were delivered to a reserve (for example: a waybill, postal receipt, or freight bill). The goods must be delivered by either the vendor or their agent.

    Vendor that qualifies as a remote store

    If the vendor chooses to operate as a remote store must keep records of their sales made to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities.

    Learn more: What records to keep

Meaning of the term First Nations individual

First Nations individual means a person who is registered under the Indian Act. A First Nations individual does not have to live or maintain a residence on a reserve. Indigenous Services Canada may issue a status card or a temporary confirmation of registration document to such a person.

Meaning of the term First Nations band

First Nations band, for GST/HST purposes, includes both a band council and a tribal council. The band council is the primary unit of an Indigenous government. A tribal council is another level of Indigenous government. It is a grouping of bands with a common interest that have joined together to provide advisory or program services for two or more bands. A tribal council's board of directors is made up of members of the band councils.
