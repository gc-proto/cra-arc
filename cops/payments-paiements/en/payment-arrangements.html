Arrange to pay your debt over time: Payments and the CRA

When you arrange to pay any existing debt over time, you are entering into a payment arrangement. A payment arrangement is an agreement you make with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). It allows you to make smaller payments over time until you have paid your entire debt including applicable interest.

Making a partial payment towards your debt

Making a partial payment to the CRA still requires you to enter into a payment arrangement.

Entering into a payment arrangement

Before you make a payment arrangement, you may need to show that you or your business has tried to pay your debt in full by borrowing money or reducing your expenses. To figure out your ability to pay, we may ask you to provide proof of your income, expenses, assets, and liabilities. You may have to do this by telephone or by completing a financial questionnaire.

Step 1 Determine how much and how frequent you can pay

Determine how much you can pay

Before you determine the amount and the frequency to be paid, the Personal income and expense worksheet is a helpful budget tool to help you determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis.

Propose a payment amount

You can then use the Payment Arrangement Calculator to calculate the payment amounts based on amount you want to pay, or the frequency on when you want to pay down your debt. The calculator also includes the prescribed CRA interest rates.

Step 2 Contact the CRA to set up your payment arrangement

There are three ways you can set up a payment arrangement.

Pre-authorized debit

(Individual and business tax only)

Paying online is one of the fastest and easiest ways to set up a payment arrangement. You can do this using Pay by pre-authorized debit through My Account or My Business Account. In most cases, you can make the arrangement on your own without speaking with the CRA.

TeleArrangement

(Individual tax only)

You can make a payment arrangement through our automated TeleArrangement service by calling 1-866-256-1147. TeleArrangement is available Monday to Friday, 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m., Eastern time. To use this service, you will need to provide:

  • your social insurance number
  • your date of birth
  • the amount entered on Line 150 from your last assessed return
Speak with an agent

If you have an individual tax debt, complete a individual tax debt call request or call 1-888-863-8657.

If you have a business tax debt, complete a:

  • GST/HST tax debt call request or call 1-877-477-5068
  • payroll tax debt call request or call 1-877-548-6016
  • corporation tax debt call request or call 1-866-291-6346

If you have a government program debt (e.g. Canada Student loans), complete a government programs debt call request or call 1-866-864-5823.

Step 3 Start to make your payments

In order to keep your payment arrangement, you will need to make a payment on the agreed upon dates. There are many ways to send a payment.

You must pay as agreed, continue to file all returns on time, and stay up to date with your tax obligations. Payment arrangements may be subject to periodic reviews.

Even if you have a payment arrangement and are making payments, the CRA is authorized to take amounts from any benefits or credits you receive when you have a debt.

Changes in your payment arrangement situation

If your situation changes and you are no longer able to pay and keep your payment arrangement, you must contact the CRA.

If you do not take action to fix your situation, the CRA may proceed with legal actions to collect the balance of your debt.

Using trust funds to help pay debt

Your business’s tax debt may include corporate income tax and trust fund (e.g. GST/HST collected, source deductions from employees, and employer CPP and EI remittences) amounts. Trust fund amounts cannot be used as cash flow. Even if you keep these amounts with your personal or business funds, the amounts are considered to be held separate and apart.
