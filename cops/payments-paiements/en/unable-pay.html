Unable to pay: Payments and the CRA

If you or your business cannot meet your debt obligations with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA), or make the minimum monthly payment for a government program, there are a few options you can take depending on your situation.

On this page

  • Contact us to discuss your situation
  • Consequences of not paying
  • Options available to assist debt repayment

Contact us to discuss your situation

You should always contact the CRA when you need help to meet your debt obligations.

The CRA has different phone numbers to call based on the type of account:

  • Personal income tax
  • Corporate income tax, GST/HST and payroll deductions
  • Canada Student Loan and Canada Apprentice Loan
  • Recovery of benefit and assistance overpayments
  • Customs debt

Consequences of not paying

If you ignore your resposibilities or refuse to co-operate to eliminate your debt, the CRA may take legal action which could result in serious financial or legal consequences.

The CRA describes the collection actions that can be taken based on the type of account:

  • Personal income tax
  • Corporate income tax, GST/HST and payroll deductions
  • Canada Student Loan and Canada Apprentice Loan
  • Recovery of benefit and assistance overpayments
  • Customs debt
