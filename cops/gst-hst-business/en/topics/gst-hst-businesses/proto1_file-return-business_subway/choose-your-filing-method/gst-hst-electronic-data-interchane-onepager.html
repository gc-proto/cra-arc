GST/HST Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)

EDI is a computer-to-computer exchange of information in a standard format. You can use EDI to file the electronic equivalent of Form GST34, Goods and Services Tax/Harmonized Sales Tax (GST/HST) Return for Registrants, Schedule B for the recapture of input tax credits, and Schedule C for the reconciliation of recaptured input tax credits. You can also pay the net tax you owe electronically through a participating Canadian financial institution.

How it works

  • You need to register with a participating financial institution.
  • You can then provide the necessary GST/HST return and payment information to them through your computer, by phone, or any other mutually agreeable method.
  • The financial institution converts the information into an electronic GST/HST return and/or payment that meets the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)'s specifications and transmits the return and/or payment to the CRA.
  • Some financial institutions may charge a fee for this service.

Benefits of using EDI

There are advantages to filing your return using EDI whether you are receiving a refund or making a payment.

  • Accuracy - you key in the information, eliminating transcription errors from further handling
  • Cash flow control - transfer of funds is guaranteed on the value date you establish with your financial institution
  • Quicker refund payments - your GST/HST refund can be deposited directly into your account within 10-15 calendar days
  • Convenience - EDI ends the need to mail a return

Eligibility

GST/HST EDI is available to GST/HST registrants across Canada, excluding those that are administered by Revenu Québec. If you are a selected listed financial institution (SLFI) that reports both the GST/HST and QST, please visit Determine which returns to complete and when to file.

Builders - transitional information

If you are a builder required to report transitional information on housing (Schedule A of the GST/HST return), you must file your return using GST/HST NETFILE.

Filing a return with a rebate amount (Common section - move to choose method page?)

You can file returns with a rebate amount on line 111 using GST/HST EDI. However, you must also mail the rebate application form(s) to the relevant tax centre on or before the due date of your return.

Recaptured input tax credits

GST/HST EDI can be used to file returns with Schedule B (recapture of input tax credits) and Schedule C (reconciliation of recaptured input tax credits). Contact your financial institution to see if they offer these filing options.

Non-residents filing GST/HST returns

Non-residents can file their GST/HST returns using EDI. However, they must complete all GST/HST amounts in Canadian dollars and remit Canadian funds to the CRA using a participating financial institution in Canada.

Retaining supporting documents (Common section - move to choose method page?)

You do not have to send receipts or supporting documents when you file your GST/HST information electronically. However, keep all supporting documents for six years, as the CRA may contact you for more information after they process your return.

You should also keep a working copy of your return for your records. It, and any other information you use to prepare your return, are subject to audit and must be retained for verification purposes.

Joint filers
(Common section - move to choose method page?)

If you are the coordinator of a closely related group of corporations (i.e. you have filed election GST303) and you file returns electronically, you must still send your letter of direction to your tax centre.
