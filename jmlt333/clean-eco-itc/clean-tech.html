Clean technology Investment Tax Credit

Intro text here

On this page

  • Overview
  • Eligibility
  • How to claim the credit

Overview

A new refundable clean technology investment tax credit (ITC) has been introduced. The credit is equal to 30% of the capital cost of eligible clean technology property that is acquired and that becomes available for use after March 27, 2023, and before 2034. The rate will be reduced to 15% in 2034 and nil after 2034.

Businesses have to meet certain specific labour requirements regarding prevailing wages and apprenticeship standards. Otherwise, the credit rate will be reduced by 10 percentage points. Exemptions apply for the clean technology ITC for acquisitions of off road zero-emission vehicles and acquisitions and installations of low carbon heat equipment.

Qualifying equipment includes certain zero-emission power generation technologies (including concentrated solar energy equipment and small modular nuclear reactors), storage equipment for zero-emission energy and non-road zero-emission vehicles. Geothermal energy systems are also generally included, unless they are used for geothermal energy projects that will co-produce oil, gas or other fossil fuels.

Eligibility

Businesses are able to fully benefit from both this credit and the Atlantic investment tax credit.

However, they cannot claim the clean technology tax credit if they claim one of the following credits for the same property:

  • clean hydrogen ITC
  • clean electricity ITC
  • clean technology manufacturing ITC
  • carbon capture, utilization and storage ITC

Businesses may claim multiple tax credits for the same project, if the project includes different types of eligible property.

How to claim the credit

Text

Corporation

A corporation claiming the investment tax credit for clean technology must file Schedule 31, Investment Tax Credit – Corporations, with the return, along with a form to be made available later. The ITC claim for clean technology is at line 155 of Schedule 31. This amount will be included on line 780 of the T2 Corporation Income Tax Return.

Partnership

A partnership that allocates the investment tax credit for clean technology to the members must advise the amount of the credit allocated to each member. The credit can only be allocated to members of a partnership that are taxable Canadian corporations. The partnership will have to file a form to be made available later and provide a T5013 slip to each member.

A corporation that is a member of a partnership must claim the amount of the credit allocated from the partnership on a form to be made available later and Schedule 31.
