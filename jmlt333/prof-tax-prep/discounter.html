Using tax discounting services

You may be looking for:

  • Filing as a tax discounting service provider

Professional tax preparers can offer discounting services and give you a discounted tax refund. This is where your refund is calculated, and immediatley part of it is paid to you before your tax return is filed.

What to expect from a discounter

A discounter preparing your tax return must:

  • Immediately pay you the discounted tax refund
  • Give you a completed and signed Form RC71, Statement of Discounting Transaction

The discounter must also give you a copy of your tax return and any related information slip(s) if you ask for them.

After the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) has processed your tax return, the discounter will receive your notice of assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment (NOR), as well as any refund. The discounter must immediately send you:

  • Form RC72, Notice of the Actual Amount of the Refund of Tax
  • The NOA or NOR received

If the refund amound is $10 or more than the refund the discounter calculated, they must pay you the full amount of any difference, excluding any refund interest.

Discounter fees

The fee a discounter can charge is the difference between your calculated refund and the refund amount paid to you. Discounters must pay you at least 85 cents for each dollar of the first $300 of an estimated refund, plus 95 cents for each remaining dollar.

The following table shows examples of refund amounts and the discounter fee that would be deducted from your refund.

The CRA conducts compliance programs under the Tax Rebate Discounting Act to make sure:

  • Your rights and interests are protected when using tax discounting services

  • Tax discounters meet their responsibilities under the law when providing tax discounting services
