Keeping documents for a personal tax return

You must keep tax documents for 6 years after filing a personal tax return if:

  • The return was filed online

    When filing with the NETFILE service, you don't need to include your tax information slips, receipts, or other documents when filing your return, but you must keep them.

  • The return was filed by paper

    If the return, form, or schedule you are completing indicates you do not have to attach supporting documents, you must still keep them.

It is important to keep your tax documents in case the CRA asks you to provide them later to support your claim.

Tax documents can include:

  • Official receipts
  • Bank statements
  • Cancelled cheques

You should also keep a copy of your return and any related notices of assessments.
