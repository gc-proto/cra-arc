Completing the tax return

Use the steps below as a guide to help you complete your tax return electronically with certified tax software.

Since each software option is different, you may need to follow the instructions in the software you selected.

If you are filing a tax return for someone else

You must be registered for Represent a Client to manage and have access to someone else's tax information.

Once registered, you will get a RepID that you can share with family or friends, so they can authorize you as their representative.

For details, refer to: Representative authorization

Check your CRA information and preferences

Before completing your tax return, make sure your details with the CRA are up-to-date.

If your circumstances have changed, you need to notify the CRA.

Change or confirm your:

  • Direct deposit information
  • Mailing address
  • Notification preferences (online mail or letter mail)

Refer to: Update your personal information with the CRA

Steps for using certified tax software

  1. Enter personal information

    Fill in personal information such as name, address, and identification number (SIN, TTN, or ITN).

  2. Fill in your CRA information automatically (optional)

    In the tax software, you can use the "Auto-fill my return" service to fill in available tax information the CRA has on file.

    If you select this option, you will be prompted to enter your CRA account user ID and password.

    For details: Auto-fill my return

  3. Get the NETFILE access code

    If you chose to use the "Auto-fill my return" service, the access code will be automatically filled in.

    If you are entering the information manually, you can find the 8-character access code on the right side of the previous tax year's notice of assessment (NOA). You will not have an access code if you are filing a tax return for the first time.

    Image of where to find the access code

    Entering the access code is not mandatory.

    If you do not provide the access code

    Tax software can still electronically send the tax return to the CRA even if you don't provide the access code.

    While the access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter it:

    • You will not be able to use any information from your most recent tax return to confirm your identity with the CRA
    • The CRA will have to use other information for authentication purposes

  4. Report income

    Use the tax information slips to report income.

    You must report all income including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, tips, and any other earnings.

    If you chose to use the "Auto-fill my return" service, tax slips and other tax related information will be automatically filled in.

    Check if all information is included

    The CRA should receive most of the tax slips and tax information from third parties by mid-March of each year.

    If information is not available in your CRA account, we may not have yet received it. If you were issued a slip with errors in your personal information, it will not be available.

    You must contact the issuer if you are missing any tax slip information.

  5. Claim deductions, credits, and expenses

    You can find and claim deductions, credits, and expenses to reduce the amount of tax you owe.

  6. Choose to view the notice in tax software (optional)

    In the tax software, you can use a service to view the notice of assessment (NOA) right after sending the return.

    To use this service, you must be registered and have access to your CRA account.

    For details: NOA in tax software

  7. Review the tax return

    It is your responsibility to review the information on the return.

    Whether you used the "Auto-fill my return" service or filled in information manually, you must make sure all the required sections are completed correctly, and the information provided is true and accurate.

    You can update some of your personal information on your tax return, including:

    • Email address
    • Marital status
    • Language of correspondence
    If you are transferring amounts to an instalment account

    If you expect a refund and want to transfer it to an instalment account, select this option when you fill in your tax return.

    The CRA will transfer the full amount of your refund directly to your instalment account. This amount will be applied toward any tax owing and will be considered received on the date your return is assessed.
