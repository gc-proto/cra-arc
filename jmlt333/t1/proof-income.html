Get a proof of income statement

You may be looking for:

  • Notice of assessments for a personal tax return (NOA and NOR)

A proof of income statement is a simple version of your tax assessment that summarizes your income and deductions for a specific year. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) can provide you with this document.

You might need to provide proof of income statement to a bank, a government, or others when you apply for:

  • A loan
  • To qualify for a benefit or subsidy
  • Other reasons that require proof of finances
Online
Sign in to My Account Registerfor CRA My Account
  • Go to the left menu and select “Tax returns”
  • Go to the column on the right, scroll down and select “Proof of income statement”

The online copy does not have the section 241 stamp.

By mail

Call the CRA's automated line to get your proof of income statement mailed to you.

Call to order your copy
Before you call

To verify your identity, you'll need

  • Social Insurance Number
  • Full name and date of birth
  • Your complete address
  • Line 15000 from your most recent assessment

If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative.

Section 241 stamp

The online copy of the proof of income statement does not have the section 241 stamp, however the information is being released at the request of an authorized individual in accordance with Section 241 of the Income Tax Act.
