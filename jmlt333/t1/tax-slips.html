Tax slips for personal income tax

Tax information slips are prepared and issued by your employer, payer, or administrator.

If you have not received your current or previous years tax slip(s), or it is missing or lost, ask your employer or the issuer of the slip for a copy.

If you do not expect to get a tax slip(s), you may be able to sign into your CRA account to get a copy, as long as the issuer sent them to the CRA.

  2. Know when you will receive your tax slip(s)

    You should have received most of your slips and receipts by the end of February.

    Depending on your situation, you may not receive your:

    • T3 and T5013 slips until the end of March
    • Receipts for RRSP and PRPP contributions made in the first 60 days of the tax year, until May

    1. If you do not expect to get a tax slip(s)

      Sign in to your Service Canada account to get your:

      • Old age security (OAS) slip - T4A(OAS)
      • Employment insurance (EI) slip - T4E
      • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) slip - T4A(P)

      You may be able to get any of the other tax information slips listed in the table above, for a current or previous year, as long as the issuer sent them to the CRA.

      If you can't get a tax slip(s) in time to file your taxes

      If you can't get a slip in time to file your taxes, you can estimate your income.

      Add up your pay stubs or financial statements to estimate the amount of income to report and any related deductions and credits you can claim. Include a note with your return stating the name and address of the issuer of the slip, the type of income, and what you are doing to get the slip.

      • If the return was filed electronically

        Keep your supporting documents even though you don't need to include them when filing. The CRA may ask to see them later.

      • If the return was filed by paper

        Attach a copy of the pay stubs or statements and your note to your paper return. Keep your original documents.
