Personal income tax

Filing a paper tax return

You may be looking for:

  • Filing online with certified tax software

You can use the tax package provided by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) to complete your tax return. The processing time for returns filed by paper is 8 weeks.

If you are filing a return on behalf of a family member or friend

If you are filing a paper return on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative to:

  • Speak with the CRA on their behalf
  • Act on behalf of them for tax matters
  • Manage and have access to their online tax information

For details: Representative authorization

Determine if you need to file a paper return

There are only a few tax situations that require you to file a paper return.

In general, you can find certified tax software to complete your tax return instead of using the paper income tax package.

In most tax situations, you can use the NETFILE service in tax software to electronically send your tax return to the CRA. If you are unsure about any restrictions that may apply to your situation, you can check to confirm you can use the NETFILE service. If you cannot use the NETFILE service, you can print your completed return and mail it to the CRA.

Check your CRA information and preferences

Make sure your details with the CRA are up-to-date.

If your circumstances have changed, you need to notify the CRA. Allow time for the CRA to process these changes before sending your tax return.

Change or confirm your:

  • Direct deposit information
  • Mailing address
  • Notification preferences (online mail or letter mail)

Find out your options to update your personal information with the CRA.

Steps for filing a paper return

  1. Get the tax package

    The paper income tax package is available to download and print, or order in alternate formats.

    You must complete:

    • Federal Income Tax and Benefit Return
    • Form 428 for your province or territory (except Quebec)
    • Federal and provincial schedules applicable to your situation

    Refer to: Get a T1 income tax package

  2. Complete the tax return

    1. Enter personal information

      Fill in personal information such as name, address, and Social Insurance Number (SIN) or other taxation number.

    2. Report income

      Use the tax information slips to report income.

      You must report all income including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, tips, and any other earnings.

      Check if all information is included

      You should receive most of your tax slips and tax information from third parties by the end of February each year.

      If your tax slip is late or lost, contact your employer or the issuer of the slip for a copy.

    3. Claim deductions, credits, and expenses

      You can find and claim deductions, credits, and expenses to reduce the amount of tax you owe.

    4. Review the tax return

      It is your responsibility to review the information on the return.

      You must make sure that all the proper fields on the return are filled in and that the information provided is true, accurate, and complete before you file your return.

      If needed, you can update some of your personal information on your tax return, including your:

      • Email address
      • Marital status
      • Language of correspondence

      If you are filing multiple returns

      If there are multiple returns being filed:

      • Place each person's return in a separate envelope
      • Place the same person's returns all in one envelope
      If you are transferring amounts to an instalment account

      If you expect a refund and want to have it transferred to an instalment account, you must attach a note to your paper return.

      The CRA will transfer the full amount of your refund directly to your instalment account. This amount will be applied toward any tax owing and will be considered received on the date your return is assessed.

      For more information, refer to required tax instalments for individuals.

  3. Send the tax return

    There are two options for sending the completed tax return to the CRA.

    • By mail

      1. Find out where to mail a paper return
      2. Mail your completed tax return to your tax centre

    • By drop-off

      1. Find out the location of CRA's drop boxes
      2. Place your return in a CRA drop box

After filing a paper return

There are 3 possible outcomes from your tax return:

  • Get a refund
  • Have a balance owing
  • Have no refund or balance owing

The CRA will send you a notice of assessment (NOA) with the outcome of your tax return. The NOA and any refund are usually sent with 8 weeks.
