Personal income tax

Get a T1 income tax package

You may be looking for:

  • Find tax software to file your taxes online
 

If you are filing a paper return, or need tax forms or schedules, you can get an income tax package online or by mail. Certain tax situations may require a specific return or form.

On this page

  • Get a 2024 tax package
  • Get a previous years' tax package
  • Order a tax package in an alternate format
  • If you need a tax package by mail
  • Confirm which tax package to use

Get a 2024 tax package

Use the income tax package if you are filing a paper return. You do not need to use the income tax package if you are filing with certified tax software.

Select the province or territory where you lived on December 31, 2024.

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • New Brunswick
  • Newfoundland and Labrador
  • Northwest Territories
  • Nova Scotia
  • Nunavut
  • Ontario
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Quebec
  • Saskatchewan
  • Yukon

Depending on your residency status, you may need to use the income tax package for non-residents and deemed residents of Canada.

If you are not sure which package may apply to you, you can confirm which tax package to use.

Documents for additional calculations

Depending on your tax situation, you may need to refer to other publications, forms, or schedules to complete additional calculations.

Refer to: Other forms and publications you may need to help complete your tax return

Order a tax package in an alternate format

You can order a tax package, forms, and publications in alternate formats such as:

  • Digital audio (mp3)
  • Electronic text (E-text)
  • Braille
  • Large print

Refer to: Order alternate formats

If you need a tax package by mail

You can request that a tax package, forms, or publications be mailed to you by using the appropriate online form.

Refer to: Order forms and publications

Confirm which tax package to use

You can confirm which tax package to use if you are filing a paper return. You do not need to use the income tax package if you are filing with certified tax software.

Do any of the following apply to your 2024 tax situation?
  • You were a newcomer to Canada
  • You left Canada permanently (emigrated)
  • You are filing a return for a person who died

NEWCOMER

Use the T1 income tax package

Use the 2024 income tax package for the province or territory where you resided on December 31, 2024.

For details about benefits and credits available to you, refer to newcomers to Canada.

DIED

Use the T1 income tax package

Use the 2024 income tax package for the province or territory where the person resided at their time of death.

For details about how to file, refer to doing taxes for someone who died.

Emigrant

Use the T1 income tax package

Use the 2024 income tax package for the province or territory where you lived on the date you left Canada.

For details about your tax obligations, refer to leaving Canada (emigrants).

Some exceptions may be based on your residential ties with Canada.

You lived outside of Canada on December 31, 2024, but kept residential ties with Canada

You may be considered a factual resident of Canada for income tax purposes.

Use the 2024 income tax package for the province or territory where you kept residential ties. You will also need to complete Form T1248 Schedule D, Information About Your Residency Status.

For details about your tax obligations when temporarily living outside of Canada, refer to factual residents.

You had no residential ties with Canada on December 31, 2024

You may be considered a deemed resident or non-resident of Canada for income tax purposes.

Use the income tax package for non-residents and deemed residents of Canada.

If you earned income in Canada

If you stayed in Canada for a period of time in 2024 and earned employment income or business income with a permanent establishment in a certain province or territory, complete the following instead:

  • 2024 income tax package for the province or territory where you stayed
  • Form T1248 Schedule D, Information About Your Residency Status

Residential ties

Significant residential ties to Canada include:

  • A home in Canada
  • A spouse or common-law partner in Canada
  • Dependants in Canada

Secondary residential ties that may be relevant include:

  • Personal property in Canada, such as a car or furniture
  • Social ties in Canada, such as memberships in Canadian recreational or religious organizations
  • Economic ties in Canada, such as Canadian bank accounts or credit cards
  • A Canadian driver's licence
  • A Canadian passport
  • Health insurance with a Canadian province or territory

For more information about residency, refer to determining your residency status.
