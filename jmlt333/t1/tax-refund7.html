Personal income tax

Tax refunds

You may be looking for:

  • Line 48400 - Refund

You will get a refund if you paid more income taxes than you owe. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) will give you a refund for the amount you overpaid.

Receiving your tax refund

The method you used to file your tax return determines how long it takes the CRA to process your refund. The timelines apply only to returns that are received on or before the due date.

The same timelines apply for refunds processed from reassessments.

  • Electronic filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your tax return through the tax software.

    • Processing time: Within 2 weeks

  • Paper filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your tax return by mail.

    • Processing time: Within 8 weeks
Why a tax refund may take longer to process

It may take longer to process a tax refund if:

  • CRA completes a review of your tax return
  • Your tax filing situation includes:
    • Multiple tax returns
    • A bankruptcy return
    • A deceased taxpayer return
    • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

Refunds from non-resident tax returns may take up to 16 weeks to process.

How you will get your refund

You will receive your tax refund based on your chosen preferences. If you have not received your refund within the processing time, you can check the status.

Direct deposit

We will deposit your refund directly into your bank account that CRA has on file.

If you need to set up or update your direct deposit

If you are not set up for direct deposit with the CRA, or need to update your banking information for future payments, refer to: Set up or update your direct deposit


Cheque

We will send your refund by mail to the address the CRA has on file.

Update your personal information to ensure you get your cheque

If your situation changes, you should notify the CRA to make sure you get your payments.

For example, if you move and do not update the CRA about your address change, your refund cheque is returned to the CRA. Any future payments will be calculated but will not be issued until your address is updated.

Refer to: Update your personal information

If you need a replacement cheque

You can request a replacement cheque if it has not been cashed and is:

  • Damaged
  • Lost
  • Stolen
  • Missing

Refer to: Cheques from the CRA

Transferring your refund to an instalment account

If you requested to have your refund transferred when you filed your return, the CRA will put the full amount in your 2025 instalment account. This amount will be applied toward any tax owing and will be considered received on the date your return is assessed. You can confirm if your instalment payment was received.

Check the status of your refund

Online

Sign into your CRA My Account to check the status of your tax refund. You can register if you do not have an account.

If your refund is not what you expected

If you think your refund amount is incorrect, review the information on your notice of assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment (NOR).

If you think your refund amount is incorrect, review the information on your notice.

Why you may not receive a refund

Generally, you will not receive a refund if your refund amount is $2 or less.

The CRA may keep all or part of your refund
  • An amount owing (or pending amount owing)
  • A garnishment order under the Family Orders and Agreements Enforcement Assistance Act
  • Certain other outstanding federal, provincial or territorial government debts, such as student loans, employment insurance (EI) and social assistance benefit overpayments, immigration loans or training allowance overpayments
  • Any outstanding GST/HST returns from a sole proprietorship or partnership

For more information, refer to: How payments are applied to offset debt

When the CRA pays you interest on your refund

The CRA will pay you compound daily interest on your 2024 tax refund, if you overpaid your taxes.

The calculation will start on the latest of the following 3 dates
  • May 30, 2024
  • The 30th day after you file your return
  • The day you overpaid your taxes

For more information, refer to: Prescribed interest rates
