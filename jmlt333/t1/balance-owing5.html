Personal income tax

Balance owing

You may be looking for:

  • Line 48500 - balance owing

You will have a balance owing if you paid less income taxes than you owe. You need to pay the amount that you owe to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) on or before April 30, 2025 to avoid paying interest and penalties.

Payment date for 2024 taxes

  • April 30, 2025: Deadline to pay your individual taxes

Review your balance owing amount

You can review your balance owing amount and explanations on your notice of assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment (NOR). Generally, you do not have to make a payment if your balance owing is $2 or less.

Refer to: Notices of assessment

Check your updated balance

Your CRA account shows the most up-to-date balance owing amount, including all payments and balances across multiple years.

The amount from your CRA accouht may be different from your notice, if you have:

  • Already paid, but your notice was issued before the payment was received
  • Unpaid balances from previous years

You can find out your tax account balance online or by phone:

Online

Sign into your CRA account to view your balance in Accounts and payments. You can register if you do not have an account.

How to pay your balance owing

You can pay your balance owing online, by mail, or in person. Do not mail cash or include cash with your return.

Find the option that works best for you:

Make a payment

If your balance owing is $10,000 or more

You should make your payment electronically. There may be a penalty if you don't pay your balance owing electronically.

If you cannot pay in full

You can have options to manage your balance:

  • Make a partial payment to reduce the interest on unpaid amounts

  • Set up a payment arrangement to give yourself more time and flexibility

    Refer to: Arrange to pay your debt over time

Interest and penalties on late payments

If you pay your balance owing after the due date, you may be charged interest on the amount until it is fully paid.

For more information: Interest and penalties on late taxes

If you do not pay

There are consequences if you don't pay the taxes you owe

For more information: If you don't pay your debt

Confirm your payment to the CRA

You can confirm that we have received the payment for the balance owing on your tax return by checking your updated balance.

If you have issues with your payment, go to: Resolve an issue with a payment

Reduce your balance owing for next year

You can reduce the amount of tax you owe to the CRA at the end of the tax year by increasing deductions from your income source.

If you receive a paycheque

Generally, most employers will deduct tax directly from your pay.You can choose to have more tax deducted from your pay by completing a TD1 form and providing it to your employer.

Refer to: Form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Returns

If you receive CPP payments

If you are 60 or older and receive Canada Pension Plan (CPP) payments, taxes are not automatically taxed, but you can request to have them deducted.

Refer to: Deducting federal income tax from your CPP payments
