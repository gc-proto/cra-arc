Personal income tax

Notices of assessment - NOA or NOR

You may be looking for:

  • Get a proof of income statement

A notice of assessment (NOA) is the summary of the calculated amounts for your personal income tax and benefits return. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) sends you a NOA after receiving your completed tax return.

The CRA will send you a notice of reassessment (NOR) if you make changes to your tax return after you have filed it. You will also receive a NOR if the CRA found errors on the tax return you filed. The NOR shows a summary of the recalculated amounts made to your tax return.

Receiving your notice after filing

You will receive your NOA or NOR after your tax return is processed. The CRA will send your notices based on your selected preferences on file.

Find out how long it takes to process

The method you use to file a tax return affects how long it takes the CRA to complete your assessment or reassessment.

  • Electronic filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your tax return through the tax software.

    • Processing time:
      • Within 2 weeks for standard processing
      • Immediately if you chose to get a NOA via tax software

  • Paper filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your tax return by mail.

    • Processing time: Within 8 weeks

Why a tax return may take longer to process

Processing a tax return

It may take longer to process a tax return and issue a notice of assessment (NOA) if:

  • You filed your return after the due date
  • CRA completes a review of your tax return
  • Your tax filing situation includes:
    • Multiple tax returns
    • A bankruptcy return
    • A deceased taxpayer return
    • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

Non-resident tax returns may take up to 16 weeks to process.

Processing adjustment requests

It may take up to 20 weeks to process changes to a tax return and issue a notice of reassessment (NOR) if your tax filing situation includes changes related to:

  • Multiple tax returns
  • Tax years beyond the normal 3-year reassessment period
  • A bankruptcy return
  • A deceased taxpayer
  • A carryback amount, such as a capital or non-capital loss
  • The elected split-pension amount
  • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

Requests filed for international and non-resident returns (including emigrant returns) may take longer to process.

For details, refer to: CRA processing times

Get a copy of a current or previous year notice

You can get a copy of your NOA or NOR either online or by mail.

Online

Sign into your CRA My Account to view or print your notices. If you do not have an account, register for one to easily access current or previous year notices.

Sign in to My Account Register
  1. Select "Tax returns" or "Mail"
  2. Select the NOA or NOR you need from the list
If you want access to someone else’s notice

To access someone else's notice, you must be an authorized representative, and they must have a CRA My Account.

Once authorized, you can sign in to Represent a Client to access their NOA or NOR.

By mail

If you would like the CRA to mail a paper copy of your notice, you will need to call us to make the request.

Call the CRA to request a paper copy
Before you call

To verify your identity, the CRA may ask for your:

  • Social insurance number
  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or to be signed in to My Account

If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative.

Understand sections of your notice

Your notice shows your name and address the CRA has on file, along with the tax centre your tax return was processed. The notice outlines your assessment and reassessment, and calculated balance.

Concurrent assessments and reassessments

If you file several consecutive-year returns at the same time, we will do a concurrent assessment. For example, if you file your 2023, 2022, and 2021 returns together, we will assess all your returns at the same time. The result appears in the account summary on the last notice of the series.

If you provide us with new information that changes your returns for several consecutive years, we will do a concurrent reassessment.

  • Notice details

    The notice details shows the:

    • Last 4 digits of your Social Insurance Number (SIN) [or TTN]
    • Tax year of the assessment or reassessment
    • Date the notice was issued

    NETFILE access code

    Your access code is used in tax software to electronically file your tax return to the CRA. If you are filing a tax return for the first time, you will not have an access code.

    The unique 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your previous year’s notice [notice of assessment (NOA)].

    While the access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter it:

    • You will not be able to use any information from your 2023 tax return to confirm your identity with the CRA
    • The CRA will have to use other information for authentication purposes
    Image of where to find the access code

  • Account summary

    The account summary shows the result of your assessed or reassessed tax return. It may also show the result from concurrent assessments or reassessments.

    There are 3 possible outcomes:

    Refund

    Your notice will show "Refund:" with the amount you will receive from the CRA.

    If you are receiving a cheque it will be mailed separately to the address on file.

    Balance owing

    Your notice will show “Amount due:” with the amount you owe to the CRA.

    No refund or balance owing

    Your notice will show "Balance: Nil". You do not get a refund or owe an amount to the CRA on this return.

  • RRSP deduction limit and available contribution room statement

    [Shows your deduction limit and available contribution room for your Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP).]

    RRSP deduction limit and available contribution room statement

    Deduction limit

    Your deduction limit is the amount of RRSP contributions you can deduct for the next year.

    Your statement also shows how we calculate your deduction limit, which is based on information you sent us with your previous tax return and information we have on file.

    Available contribution room

    The last line of the statement gives your available contribution room. This is the maximum amount you can contribute for next year.

    It is your deduction limit minus any unused RRSP contributions you reported in past years. Your unused contributions appear on your statement.

    If the total RRSP contributions (current and unused) that you claim on your return are less than your deduction limit, you have available contribution room.

    Excess contribution

    If your RRSP contributions are more than your deduction limit, you have an excess of contributions. You may have to pay tax on this amount.

    For more information on RRSP contribution and deduction rules, refer to: How contributions affect your RRSP deduction limit

    Depending on your situation, your notice may also contain your:

    Home Buyers' Plan (HBP) statement

    If you participate in the Home Buyers’ Plan (HBP), you will see your HBP statement.

    It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

    For more information, refer to: Home Buyers' Plan

    Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP) statement

    If you participate in the Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP), you will see your LLP statement.

    It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

    For more information, refer to: Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP)

If you disagree with your assessment

If you think your refund or balance owing is incorrect, or if you disagree with your assessment and want to register a formal dispute, you have 90 days from the date of your notice to register.

For details: File an income tax objection
