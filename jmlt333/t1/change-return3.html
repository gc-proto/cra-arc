Reasons for changing a return

You can change your tax return if you forgot to provide information, including:

  • Reporting income (such as a T4 slip or tips)
  • Claiming a deduction, credit, or expense (such as childcare or medical receipts)
  • Adding a tax slip (such as RRSP contribution receipt)

[Find out the options (to make changes)]

[You must wait until you receive your notice of assessment (NOA) before you can request to make changes to your personal income tax return.]

Electronically

Using your:

  • Certified tax software and the "ReFILE" service
  • CRA account and the "Change my return" feature
By mail

Using the paper form and your supporting documents

[Using your certified tax software and the "ReFILE" service]

If you are an individual changing your own tax return, or a family member or friend changing someone else's tax return, you must have used certified tax software to file the return you are requesting to change.

If you are professional service provider (such as an accountant or discounter) changing someone else's tax return, you must have used EFILE certified tax software to file the return you are requesting to change.

[Using your CRA account and the "Change my return" feature]

If you are an individual changing your own tax return, you must have a CRA My Account.

If you are a tax preparer changing someone else's tax return, you must be registered for Represent a Client (RAC) and have at least level 2 access.

By mail

Send both of the following to your tax centre:

  • A completed Form T1-ADJ, T1 Adjustment Request that provides:
    • The details of your request (including the years of the returns you want us to change)
    • Your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
    • Your address
    • A phone number where we can reach you during the day
  • Supporting documents for the entire amount, including amounts you already claimed but did not previously send documents for

Any request to change a previous return must be sent separately from your current year tax return.

How long it takes to process your request

The method you use to file your request affects how long it takes the CRA to process and make a decision.

  • Electronic filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your request for changes through the tax software or your CRA account.

    Processing time: Within 2 weeks

  • Paper filing

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your request for changes by mail.

    Processing time: Within 8 weeks


Why your request for changes may take longer to process

It may take up to 20 weeks to process changes to a tax return if your tax filing situation includes changes related to:

  • Multiple tax returns
  • Tax years beyond the normal 3-year reassessment period
  • A bankruptcy return
  • A deceased taxpayer
  • A carryback amount, such as a capital or non-capital loss
  • The elected split-pension amount
  • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

Requests filed for international and non-resident returns (including emigrant returns) may take longer to process.

For details, refer to: CRA processing times

[Outcome of your request]

Based on the CRA's review and decision, your request will either be approved or denied.

Approved request

The CRA will send you a notice of reassessment (NOR). It will show the changes to your tax return, as well as any refund.

Denied request

The CRA will send you a letter explaining why we did not make the changes you asked for, or why no changes were needed.
