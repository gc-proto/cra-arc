Reasons for changing a return

You can change your return if you need to adjust amounts or forgot to provide information, including:

  • Reporting income (such as a T4 slip or tips)
  • Claiming a deduction, credit, or expense (such as childcare or medical receipts)
    • Applications for benefits or credits cannot be completed on the tax return
  • Adding a missing tax slip (such as RRSP contribution receipt)

Do not change your return if you are updating:

  • Your personal information, including:
    • Mailing or email address
    • Name
    • Marital status
    • Direct deposit information

Refer to: Update your personal information with the CRA

Options to make your changes

You or your tax preparer must have filed the return you are requesting to change. You must wait until you receive your notice of assessment (NOA) before you can request any changes.

If you have already submitted a request for changes, you must wait until you receive a response from the CRA before any additional changes can be made.

Online

Change your return with either certified tax software or your CRA account.

Tax software

Certified tax software must have been used to send the tax return.

Using your tax software to change a return

Go to your certified tax software and look for the option to change your return using the "ReFILE" service. It may be easier for you to use the same certified tax software to change your return, however all certified software products now include the "ReFILE" service.

The service is available daily, except between 3 am and 6 am ET for maintenance and between February 1 and 16, 2025.

The "ReFILE" service includes questions and messages when the CRA:

  • Needs more information
  • Detects errors
  • Needs supporting documents to finalize your request

Changing your own return

You must have used certified tax software to file [complete and send?]the return you are requesting to change.

Changing someone else's return

You must be an authorized representative and have used certified tax software to file the return you are requesting to change.

For professional tax preparers, such as an accountant or discounter, you must have used EFILE certified tax software. If you are a family member or friend, you must have used certified tax software.

For most tax situations you can use the "ReFILE" service. If you are unsure about any restrictions that may apply to you, check to confirm which option you can use.

Your CRA account

You must have access to your CRA account.

Using your CRA account to change a return

In your CRA account, select the "Change my return" service option, and follow the instructions to make your changes.

  1. Sign in to your CRA account
  2. Select "My Account" if changing your own return; "Represent a Client" if changing someone else's
  3. Go to the left menu and select “Tax returns”
  4. Go to the column on the right, and select “Change my return”

The "Change my return" service includes questions and messages when the CRA:

  • Needs more information
  • Detects errors
  • Needs supporting documents to finalize your request

For detailed instructions, refer to: Help using the "Change my return" service

Changing someone else's return

You must be an authorized representative registered for Represent a Client (RAC) and have at least level 2 access.

For most tax situations you can use the "Change my return" service. If you are unsure about any restrictions that may apply to you, check to confirm which option you can use.

By mail

Use the paper form and your supporting documents.

Sending your adjustment request

Any request to change a previous return must be sent separately from your current year tax return.

  1. Complete Form T1-ADJ, T1 Adjustment Request to provide:
    • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
    • Tax year you want to change
    • Name and address
    • Authorization details, if applicable
    • Adjustment details of your request (including revised amounts)
    • Daytime phone number
  2. Attach supporting documents for the entire amount, including amounts you already claimed but did not previously send documents for
  3. Send both of the following to your tax centre

How long it takes to process your request

The method you use to request a change to your return affects how long it takes the CRA to process and make a decision.

  • Electronic request

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your request through the tax software or your CRA account.

    Processing time: Within 2 weeks

  • Paper request

    Processing times start after the CRA receives your request by mail.

    Processing time: Within 8 weeks


Situations that may take longer to process

It may take up to 20 weeks to process changes to a tax return if your tax filing situation includes changes related to:

  • Multiple tax returns
  • Tax years beyond the normal 3-year reassessment period
  • A bankruptcy return
  • A deceased taxpayer
  • A carryback amount, such as a capital or non-capital loss
  • The elected split-pension amount
  • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

Requests filed for international and non-resident returns (including emigrant returns) may take longer to process.

For details, refer to: CRA processing times

What to expect after your request is processed

Based on the CRA's review and decision, your request will either be approved or denied. We will send our response either electronically through your CRA online mail or by letter mail, depending on your selected preferences on file.

If approved

The CRA will send you a notice of reassessment (NOR) showing the changes to your tax return.

If denied

The CRA will send you a letter explaining why we did not make the changes you requested, or why no changes were needed.
