Offering and expanding automatic tax filing services

The CRA has introduced a number of initiatives to make it easier to file an income tax and benefit return. 

The announcements in Budget 2023 and Budget 2024 further expanded these efforts by:

  • increasing the number of eligible Canadians for SimpleFile by Phone (previously File My Return) to 2 million by 2025
  • piloting a new automatic filing service that will help lower-income and vulnerable Canadians who currently do not file their taxes receive the benefit and credit payments to which they are entitled

Current tax filing services offered by the CRA

The CRA offers several tools and services, some of which are automated, to reduce filing barriers for lower-income individuals. These include:

SimpleFile

Simplified tax filing methods offered by the CRA to eligible individuals.

SimpleFile services are offered by invitation only:

  • SimpleFile by Phone
  • SimpleFile Digital
  • SimpleFile by Paper

Indigenous credit and benefit short return

A paper-based simplified filing option available to all Indigenous communities.

Auto-fill my return (AFR)

A secure CRA service that allows individuals and authorized representatives using certified software, to automatically fill in parts of a personal tax return.

The AFR service can only use information the CRA has available at the time of the request.

Free tax clinics

Through the Community Volunteer Income Tax Program (CVITP), community organizations host free tax clinics where volunteers complete tax returns for people with a modest income and a simple tax situation.

Automatic tax filing services in the Canadian context

Many lower-income and vulnerable Canadians are missing out on valuable benefit and credit payments because they are not filing their income tax and benefit returns.

Benefit and credit payments, such as the Canada child benefit and the GST/HST credit, help make life more affordable. In order to receive, or continue to receive, benefit and credit payments, individuals have to update their information with the CRA each year by doing their taxes. Filing a tax return also confirms an individual’s eligibility for programs such as the Canadian Dental Care Plan.

The CRA has had success with its current automatic tax filing services, but more can be done to reduce burden and remove barriers to tax filing. It is important to recognize 3 key considerations when looking to enhance automatic tax filing in Canada.

  1. Taxpayer consent is mandatory before benefit and credit payments can be paid out. This means the CRA must contact individuals before producing a notice of assessment (NOA).
  2. The CRA must collect certain information to determine eligibility and payment amounts, such as their family situation and composition, rental payments, property tax paid, and self-employed income.
  3. The Canadian tax system allows individuals to make choices that may help reduce their tax burden when they file their tax return, and these choices may change the outcome of their return. This includes deciding:
    • which family member should claim certian credits and deductions
    • if and how pension income should be split
    • whether certain credits and deductions will be claimed during the current tax year, or carried forward to future years

The CRA is continuing to explore how it can further automate processes to make tax filing easier and is charting a path forward for automatic tax filing in Canada.

The path forward will be shaped by a variety of inputs, including:

  • the results of the automatic tax filing pilots
  • feedback from an automatic tax filing advisory group
  • consultations with community organizations, tax preparers, tax software professionals, and individuals

The path forward will prioritize the needs of lower-income individuals who are not filing their tax returns, with the goal of connecting more individuals to the benefit and credit payments designed to support them.
