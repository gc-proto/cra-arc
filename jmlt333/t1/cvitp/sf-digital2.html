With SimpleFile Digital, you only need to answer a series of quick questions. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) uses your answers, plus the information the CRA has on file, to complete and process an income tax and benefit return on your behalf.

Steps to use SimpleFile Digital

  1. Locate your access code

    SimpleFile Digital requires you have a Netfile Access Code (NAC). There are 2 ways locate it:

    • On your 2024 tax notice of assessment (NOA)

      Where to find the code on your NOA

      Your unique 8-character Netfile Access Code (NAC) located on the right side of your previous year’s notice of assessment (NOA)

    • On your 2026 SimpleFile Digital invitation

      Where to find the code on your invitation

      If you received a SimpleFile Digital invitation, the invitation package provides the information you need to use the SimpleFile Digital service, including the link to the service and the access code. There are no forms to fill out or calculations for you to do.

      Delivery through email notifications

      If you are registered to receive email notifications, we will send you an email when your SimpleFile invitation package is available to view in My Account.

      If you have access to My Account, but are not registered for email notifications, you can also view your invitation in My Account.

      Delivery by mail

      Unless you are registered on to receive email notifications, we will send your SimpleFile invitation package by mail to the mailing address we have on file.

    2. Get your information ready

    Before sign-in to SimpleFile Digital, you will need:

    • Your social insurance number (SIN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your mailing address
    • Your spouse’s SIN and net income, if married or common-law
    • Your access code
    • To answer a series of short questions

  2. Sign-in and file using the SimpleFile Digital web form

    The service is available:

    • 21 hours a day between 6 am and 3 am (Eastern time)
    • 7 days a week
    • February 24, 2025 to [end date]

    To access the service, launch the: SimpleFile Digital web form

    • All residents (except Quebec)

      Both the federal tax return and provincial or territory forms are filed.

    • Quebec residents

      Only a federal tax return is filed. You will still need to complete and submit a Quebec provincial income tax return with Revenu Québec.

  3. Wait for your notice of assessment

    Once your return has been assessed, you will receive a notice of assessment (NOA).
