Use your SimpleFile invitation to file your taxes

Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) is inviting eligible individuals to use the SimpleFile suite of services to file their 2024 income tax and benefit returns.

SimpleFile services are tax filing methods for eligible individuals with a lower-income income, and a simple tax situation. These services are offered by invitation only.

With SimpleFile services, you only need to answer a series of quick questions, and then the services use your answers and the information the CRA has on file, to complete and process an income tax and benefit return on your behalf. Once your return has been assessed, you will receive a notice of assessment (NOA).

  • Free
  • Fast
  • Secure

Ways to SimpleFile

To complete your income tax and benefit return, the SimpleFile services use the information in CRA's systems at the time of use of the service. Once your return has been assessed, you will receive a notice of assessment (NOA).


SimpleFile by Phone

The invitation letter provides the information you need to use the SimpleFile by Phone service, including the phone number to call.

There are no forms to fill out or calculations to do. You do not need to speak to an agent to use this service.

Eligibility

You do not need to have income to use this service. If you do have income, it must be under a certain threshold and from certain sources.

Income under a certain threshold

You must have a lower income or fixed income, based on factors that include age, province of residence, and disability status.

Income from certain sources

You must have income exclusively from the following sources:

Benefits

Old Age Security – T4A(OAS)

Guaranteed Income Supplement / Net federal supplements – T4A(OAS)

Canada or Quebec Pension Plan benefits (including disability benefits) – T4A(P)

Employment and related income supports

Employment income – T4

Employment Insurance Benefits – T4E

Workers’ compensation benefits – T5007

Social assistance payments – T5007

Canada Emergency Benefits – T4A

Investment income

Some Interest Income – T5 (box 13)

Invitations

This service applies to invitations that are sent in both:

  • February 2025
  • Summer 2025

If your personal situation has changed, you may no longer be able to use the service.

How you will receive your invitation
In your online CRA account

If you are registered to receive email notifications, your invitation letter will be available in your CRA My Account. You will be able to access and view your invitation once you are registered.

Delivery by letter mail

Unless you are registered to receive email notifications, we will send your invitation to the mailing address we have on file.

Availability

This service is available 21 hours a day between 6 am and 3 am (Eastern time), 7 days a week starting February 24, 2025

Completion time

Approximately 5 to 10 minutes

Additional information

To use the phone service, you will need to:

  • Provide some personal information
  • Answer a series of short questions using the keypad on your phone
If you are disconnected from the call

If you hang up or are disconnected from the call before the end of the session, you will have to call again.

If you think you have made an error before the end of the session, you can hang up and call back to start over.

Your information will only be saved once you have completed the call.

If you have a Personal Identification Number (PIN)

At the end of the call, you can receive an estimate of your net income, taxable income, and any refund that you may be eligible for at the end of the call. To receive this information, you will need to provide your PIN when prompted.

For information on how to create a PIN, refer to: Use a PIN when you call us

If you are a Quebec resident

The SimpleFile service options only complete the federal income tax and benefit return. You will still need to complete and submit a Quebec provincial income tax return with Revenu Québec.
