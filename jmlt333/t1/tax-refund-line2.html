Line 48400 - Refund

You may be looking for:

  • Tax refunds

After you report your income and claim your deductions, credits, and expenses, you can calculate whether you will get a refund or have an amount owing. Generally, the CRA does not refund a difference of $2 or less.

Subtract your total credits from your total payable:

  Line 43500: Total payable (total income tax that you owe for the year)
-  Line 48200: Total credits (total income tax that you paid during the year, including instalment payments and refundable credits)
=  Your result (refund or balance owing)

If the result is negative, you paid more income tax than you needed to for the year. Enter the result at line 48400 on your paper return.

If the result is positive, refer to line 48500: Balance owing.

The amount is your expected refund. Your notice of assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment (NOR) will show the final result.
