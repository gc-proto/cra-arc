Personal income tax

Using a professional tax preparer

You may be looking for:

  • Filing as an EFILE service provider

You can take your tax documents to a professional tax preparer (such as an accountant) who will prepare your tax return and electronically send it to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for a fee.

A tax preparer can be any of the following:

  • A firm
  • An organization
  • A certified individual

Find a tax preparer

Almost all Canadians are eligible to have their tax return filed electronically. Your tax preparer (EFILE service provider) will confirm based on your situation.

Filing your tax return

  1. Gather your information

    You must provide your tax preparer with:

    • Your tax slips
    • Your receipts related to claiming deductions
    • Any other documents used to prepare your return

  2. Give authorization

    There are different types of authorization you can give your tax preparer.

    Authorization to send your tax return

    You must complete and sign Form T183, Information Return for Electronic Filing of an Individual's Income Tax and Benefit Return, authorizing your tax preparer to electronically send your tax return to the CRA.

    Authorization to access your tax information

    You may authorize your tax preparer to represent you and have access to your online tax information.

  3. Keep your documents

    Keep all your tax slips, receipts, and the completed Form T183 for at least 6 years.

  4. Wait for your assessment

    After your tax preparer files your return with the CRA, you will receive your notice of assessment (NOA).
