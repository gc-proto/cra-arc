Personal income tax

Using a professional tax preparer

You may be looking for:

  • Filing as an EFILE service provider

You can take your tax documents to a professional tax preparer (such as an accountant) who will prepare your tax return and electronically send it to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for a fee.

A tax preparer can be any of the following:

  • A firm
  • An organization
  • A certified individual

Find a tax preparer

Almost all Canadians are eligible to have their tax return filed electronically. Your tax preparer (EFILE service provider) will confirm based on your situation.

Tax preparer directory

Some tax preparers offer refund discounting services. A discounter will calculate your expected tax refund and give you one up-front payment before filing your return.

Get ready to file

Make sure your details with the CRA are up-to-date and ......

Check your CRA information

Change or confirm your:

  • Direct deposit information
  • Mailing address
  • Notification preferences (online mail or letter mail)

Find out your options to update your personal information with the CRA. Allow time for the CRA to process any updates before filing your tax return.

Gather your information

You need to gather all information about your income and expenses, such as:

  • Your tax slips
  • Your receipts related to claiming deductions
  • Any other documents to support information in your return

Steps to file with a tax preparer

  1. Provide your information

    You must provide your tax preparer with the information you gathered about your income and expenses.

  2. Know your authorization options

    You will need to authorize your tax preparer to electronically send your tax return to the CRA.

    Depending on your situation, you may also choose additional authorization to have your tax preparer:

    • Speak with the CRA on your behalf
    • Act on your behalf for tax matters
    • Access your online tax information

    In this case, refer to Authorize a representative.

  3. Review your information

    Your tax preparer will prepare your return using the information you provided to them.

    Review your prepared tax return and the information your tax preparer completed on Form T183, Information Return for Electronic Filing of an Individual's Income Tax and Benefit Return

  4. Give authorization to file

    You must complete Form T183, Information Return for Electronic Filing of an Individual's Income Tax and Benefit Return.

    Fill in "Part C - Electronic filer identification" with your tax preparer's information and sign "Part F - Declaration and authorization" to authorize your tax preparer to electronically send your tax return to the CRA.

    Additional authorization (optional)

    You may also authorize your tax preparer to:

    • Speak with the CRA on your behalf
    • Act on your behalf for tax matters
    • Access your online tax information

    For details, refer to Authorize a representative.

  5. Wait for your assessment

    After your tax preparer electronically files your tax return with the CRA, you will receive your notice of assessment (NOA).

    Keep all your tax slips, receipts, and the completed Form T183 for at least 6 years.
