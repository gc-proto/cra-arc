Personal income tax

Balance owing

If you have an amount on line 48500 of your personal income tax return, you have a balance owing.

Payment date for 2024 taxes

  • April 30, 2025: Deadline to pay your individual taxes to avoid paying interest and penalties

Steps

  1. Review your balance owing amount

    [Text about signing into account and getting current amount - reference statement of account]

    If your balance owing is $2 or less

    You do not have to make a payment if you owe $2 or less.

    If your balance owing is $10,000 or more

    Payments should be made electronically if you owe $10,000 or more. There may be a penalty if you don't pay your balance owing electronically.

    If your balance owing is not what you expected

    If you think your balance owing amount is incorrect, review the information on your notice of assessment (NOA).

    For more information on NOAs, refer to: Notices of assessment

  2. Determine your options to pay

    You can pay online, by mail, or in person.

    Find the option that works for you:

    Ways to make a payment

    If you cannot pay in full

    You can make a partial payment to reduce the amount of interest you need to pay on unpaid amounts.

    You can also set up a payment arrangement to give yourself more time and flexibility to repay what you owe.

  3. Confirm your payment to the CRA

    You can confirm that we have received the payment for the balance owing on your tax return.

    For more information, refer to: Confirm a payment to the CRA

Reduce your balance owing for next year

You can reduce the amount of tax you owe to the CRA at the end of the tax year by increasing deductions from your income source.

  • If you receive a paycheque

    Generally, most employers will deduct tax directly from your pay.

    You can choose to have more tax deducted from your pay by completing a TD1 form and providing it to your employer.

    Refer to: Form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Returns

  • If you receive CPP payments

    Taxes are not automatically deducted from your CPP payments but you can make the request for it to be.

    Refer to: Deducting federal income tax from your CPP payments
