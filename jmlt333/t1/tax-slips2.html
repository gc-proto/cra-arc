Tax information slips are prepared and sent to you by your employer, payer, or administrator.

Types of tax slips you may get

When you will receive your tax slip(s)

You should have received most of your slips and receipts by the end of February.

Depending on your situation, you may not receive your:

  • T3 and T5013 slips until the end of March
  • Receipts for RRSP and PRPP contributions made in the first 60 days of the tax year, until May

Get a copy of a tax slip(s)

If you have not received your current or previous years tax slip(s), or it is missing or lost, you may be able to sign into your government accounts to get a copy.

  • Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)

    Sign into your CRA account to get any of the tax information slips listed in the table above, except for those provided by Service Canada.

    These will be available as long as the issuer sent them to the CRA.

    Sign into your Service Canada account to get your:

    • Old age security (OAS) slip - T4A(OAS)
    • Employment insurance (EI) slip - T4E
    • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) slip - T4A(P)

If you do not have a CRA account and choose not to register, you can either:

  • Ask the issuer

    Ask your employer, payer, or administrator for a copy of a tax slip(s)

  • Call the CRA

    Tax slip(s) information will be available as long as the issuer sent them to the CRA.

    Call the CRA to get a tax slip(s)
    Before you call

    To verify your identify, you will need:

    • your social insurance number
    • full name and date of birth
    • your complete address
    • details from your notice of assessment or reassessment, proof of income, or other tax document

    If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative.

If you cannot get a tax slip(s)

If you cannot get a tax slip(s) from the issuer, and if the CRA does not have the information on file, you can estimate your income in order to file your taxes on time.

Add up your pay stubs or financial statements to estimate the amount of income to report and any related deductions and credits you can claim. Include a note with your return stating the name and address of the issuer of the slip, the type of income, and what you are doing to get the slip.

  • If you are filing electronically

    Keep all documents in case the CRA asks to see them later

  • If you are filing by mail

    Attach a copy of all documents to the paper return
