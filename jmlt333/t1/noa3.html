Personal income tax

Notices of assessment - NOA or NOR

You may be looking for:

  • Get a proof of income statement

A notice of assessment (NOA) is the initial summary of the calculated amounts for your personal income tax and benefits return. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) sends you a NOA after receiving your completed tax return.

The CRA will send you a notice of reassessment (NOR) if either you or the CRA make changes to your tax return after you have filed it. The NOR shows a summary of the recalculated amounts made to your tax return.

When you will get your notice

The method you use to file your tax return affects how long it takes to get your notice. The CRA will send your documents based on the preferences you have chosen to receive your CRA mail.

Processing times

  • Electronic filing

    • Processing time: Within 2 weeks

    If you have access to your CRA account, you can choose the option to get your NOA or NOR in your tax software right away.

  • Paper filing

    • Processing time: Within 8 weeks

Filing after the due date

Your notice may take longer to process if your tax return is:

  • You filed your return after the due date
  • You have a complex tax situation (may take approximately 16 weeks to process):
    • Multiple tax returns
    • An international or non-resident, or emigrant retur
    • A bankruptcy retur
    • A deceased taxpayer return
    • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents

  • You were selected for a review of your tax return

How to get a copy of your notice

If you provide your email address on your tax return and do not change your preferences, you will not receive paper documents from the CRA by mail.

Online

If you have a CRA account, your notices will be available after your tax return has been processed.

If you provided your email address to the CRA and do not change your preferences in your CRA account, you will not receive a notice by mail.

You can sign in to CRA My Account to get:

  • Notice of assessment (NOA)
  • Notice of reassessment (NOR)
  • A notice from a previous tax year

If you want to access someone else's notice

To access a family member or friend’s NOA, complete all of the following:

  • Ensure they are registered for CRA My Account
  • Be registered in Represent a Client
    • Get your RepID
    • Refer to: Represent a Client
  • Have them authorize you
    • Give your RepID so they can authorize you as their representative
    • Refer to: Representative authorization

Once you are authorized, you will be able to access their NOA.

By mail

[If you selected the option to get paper documents from the CRA, your notice will be mailed after your return is processed. The CRA will mail it to your address on file.]

If you would like a paper version of your NOA mailed to you or do not have a CRA My Account, call the CRA to make the request.

Before you call

To verify your identity, the CRA may ask for your:

  • Social insurance number
  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or to be signed in to My Account

If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative.

Understanding your notice

Notice details

  • Social insurance number (only the last 3 digits of your SIN is shown)
  • Tax year
  • Date issued
Access code

Your access code is used to electronically file your tax return. It is not mandatory to use the code to send your return electronically.

Your 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your Notice of Assessment of the previous tax year.

Notice of assessment or Notice of reassessment

The Commissioner of Revenue outlines your assessment or reassessment result, and calculated balance. If you are getting a refund, the amount and your chosen method for payments is indicated.

If you have a balance owing, the amount you must pay and the due date is provided.

Account summary

This section shows the result of your assessed or reassessed tax return.

The amount may include outstanding balances you may owe from previous returns. If you send tax returns or provide new information that impact consecutive years, the account summary may also show the result from concurrent evaluations[?].

A refund

If you are getting a refund, the amount of your refund is displayed.

Refer to: Getting your personal income tax refund

A balance owing

If you owe income taxes, the amount you owe and your payment due date is provided. To avoid interest charges you need to pay by the payment due date.

Refer to:Paying the balance owing on your personal income tax

No refund or balance owing

If your balance is “Nil”. This means the balance on your return is $0. You do not owe any amount to the CRA and the CRA does not need to send you a refund.

You may also have a “Nil” balance if you paid your balance in full before your notice was issued.

Why your notice is showing a balance owing even after you have paid

If your notice indicates you have a balance owing, but you have already paid, it may be because your notice was issued before your payment was received.

Tax returns filed online are generally assessed so quickly that any payments sent with a return may not show up by the time you receive your notice.

You can confirm that we have received the payment for the balance owing on your tax return.

If you think your refund or balance owing is incorrect

If you think your amount is incorrect, review the information in the "Tax assessment" portion of your notice.

If you find any errors, refer to: Changing your tax return

Tax assessment

Summary

Lists the main line numbers and amounts used to calculate your refund or balance owing on your assessed tax return.

You can use these amounts to compare to the ones on your return to see where the CRA made any changes.

Any penalties and interest you owe on a balance owing is provided. If you have a balance owing from a previous assessment, it will also appear here.

Explanation of changes and other important information

This section explains in detail the changes or corrections we made to your tax return.

These changes are based on the information sent with your return and the information we have on file.

RRSP deduction limit and available contribution room statement

This section shows your deduction limit and available contribution room for your Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP).

  • Deduction limit

Your deduction limit is the amount of RRSP contributions you can deduct for the next year.

Your statement also shows how we calculate your deduction limit, which is based on information you sent us with your previous tax return and information we have on file.

  • Available contribution room

    • The last line of the statement gives your available contribution room. This is the maximum amount you can contribute for next year.

    It is your deduction limit minus any unused RRSP contributions you reported in past years. Your unused contributions appear on your statement.

    If the total RRSP contributions (current and unused) that you claim on your return are less than your deduction limit, you have available contribution room.

  • Excess contributio

    • If your RRSP contributions are more than your deduction limit, you have an excess of contributions. You may have to pay tax on this amount.

    For more information on RRSP contribution and deduction rules, refer to: How contributions affect your RRSP deduction limit

    Depending on your situation, your notice may also contain your:

    • Home Buyers' Plan (HBP) statement

      • If you participate in the Home Buyers’ Plan (HBP), you will see your HBP statement.

      It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

      For more information, refer to: Home Buyers' Plan

    • Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP) statement

      • If you participate in the Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP), you will see your LLP statement.

      It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

      For more information, refer to: Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP)

    If you disagree with your assessment

    If you disagree with your assessment and want to register a formal dispute, you have 90 days from the date of your notice to register.

    For more information, refer to: File an income tax objection
