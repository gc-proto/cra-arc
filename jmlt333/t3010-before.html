Before you file

To maintain your charitable registration, you must file a complete T3010 Return. Avoid a lost return, incorrect financial calculations and other mistakes that could cause delays.

If you haven't already, we recommend to sign up for My Business Account (MyBA) as early as possible to file your return online.

Make sure you have a complete return

A complete return must include all of the following forms and documents:

  • Form T3010, Registered Charity Information Return
    • Section C2: Describe your charitable activites, not your fundraising activities
  • Form T1235, Directors/Trustees and Like Officials Worksheet
    • Include all the information requested for each director, trustee, and like official
    • If your directors, trustees, or like officials have not left their position, you can leave the "Term-End date" field blank.
  • Form T1236, Qualified donees worksheet / Amounts provided to other organizations (if applicable)
    • Include the registration numbers for all qualified donees
  • Form T2081, Excess corporate holding worksheet for private foundations (if applicable)
  • Financial statements (even if your charity was inactive)
    • Must all have the same fiscal year-end

If you don't file your return

If you do not file your return within 6 months of the end of your fiscal year we will start the revocation process for failure to file.

We will send you a T2051A, Notice of Intention to Revoke a Charity's Registration, by registered mail. You have 45 days from the date of this notice to file your return and 90 days to file an objection.

If you do not file an objection or appeal, or if it was unsuccessful, your charitable registration will be revoked. We will send you, by registered mail, a T2051B, Notice of Revocation of a Charity's Registration, which states the effective date of revocation.

If your charity's registration is revoked, you:

  • can no longer issue official donation receipts
  • are no longer exempt from paying income tax
  • must give all of your remaining assests to an eligible donee or pay a revocation tax equal to the full value of your assets
