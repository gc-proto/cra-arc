Complete your application

Fill out the form to describe your planned project and include supporting documentation.

What form to use

  • Form T[123] - Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) pre-claim approval application

Use SR&ED definitions when describing your project

When you describe the planned work, we recommend that you use the characteristics of the pertinent terms as defined for SR&ED (for example, the definitions of uncertainties, advancement, qualified individuals, etc.).

For details: Definitions for SR&ED projects

Part 1: General information

Fill in information such as your name, business and case number.

Line 010 - Name of claimant
Line 012 - Business number
Line 014 - Total number of projects for pre-claim approval
Line 016 - Pre-claim approval case number

Part 2: Project information

You must complete this part for each project of your pre-claim approval request.

Section A: Project identification

Section A – Project identification

Provide information on the nature and scope of the planned SR&ED work.

Line 020 – Project title
Enter the title of the project. If you have a title and an identification code for the project, enter both.
Line 022 – Duration of the pre-claim approval period requested
Select the duration of the pre-claim approval period requested for the project (1, 2 or 3 years).
Line 024 – Estimated total SR&ED expenditures
Enter the total estimated SR&ED expenditures.
Line 026 – Anticipated SR&ED project start date
Enter the start date of the SR&ED project. The start of the project is defined as the point at which planned work commences to resolve the identified scientific or technological uncertainty.
Line 028 – Anticipated SR&ED project completion date
Enter the expected completion date of the SR&ED project. The completion date of the project is defined as the point at which the planned work is expected to resolve the identified uncertainty or you plan to terminate the project for any reason.
Line 030 – Field of science or technology code
From the table in Appendix 1 of the T4088 guide, select the field of science or technology that best describes the primary field of your SR&ED project. This may not necessarily be the same as the field of science or technology of the company project or in which the company carries out its regular business.

The field of science and technology you enter on this line is used for statistical and resource management purposes only and not for the purpose of determining eligibility of the work. Appendix 1 is not an exhaustive list of all eligible fields of science or technology.

Line 032 – Canadian Research and Development Classification Field of research code
[To get language from the updated T4088 guide].
Lines 034 to 036
Provide the name, position title and phone number of the person best suited to provide the supporting technical information for the planned project. This person, or another individual from the business with this knowledge, will be required to attend the meeting with the CRA.
Line 038 to 040 – Project pre-claim history
Indicate if you have previously made a pre-claim approval request for this project. Select only one. If a project is a continuation from a predecessor company, and it is the first time you are making a pre-claim approval request, select 038 (first pre-claim approval request for this project).

Section B – Project descriptions

Provide detailed technical information describing your planned SR&ED project.

Each question in section B has a strict word limit of 500 words. Therefore, your answers should focus on the technical facts and should be in the technical language and style of the personnel who will carry out the work, or who are familiar with the scientific or engineering concepts of the project. You may include more details in the supporting documents, such as your project plan.

Use existing materials and project planning documents generated during the project planning phase to extract the pertinent information to complete this section accurately and concisely.

Line 042 - What scientific or technological uncertainty do you aim to address?
In responding to this question, start by providing a brief context of your business and the planned SR&ED project.

Clearly state the objective of the project and describe the new scientific or technological knowledge or the new or improved capability you are seeking.

Describe the scientific or technological uncertainty you anticipate will require SR&ED. In other words, clearly state the uncertainty related to achieving the project objective.

Line 044 – What due diligence have you conducted to identify and validate the uncertainty described in line 042?
In answering this question, briefly explain the existing scientific or technological knowledge base and describe its shortcomings or limitations. These gaps should show why the uncertainty could not be resolved through standard methods, existing literature, known techniques, or industry expertise.

Describe the specific actions you undertook to determine that the uncertainty could not be resolved using publicly or internally available knowledge, techniques, or methods. This may include reviewing scientific literature, technical publications, patents, industry standards, vendor documentation, or consulting subject‑matter experts.

Additionally, outline any experimental work, prototypes, or trials you may have conducted using existing approaches, and explain how the outcomes demonstrated that the available knowledge is insufficient to resolve the identified uncertainty.

Line 046 – What work do you plan to perform, during the pre-claim approval period requested, to overcome the scientific or technological uncertainty described at line 042? How will you structure your investigation, including the initial hypotheses and planned experiments or analyses?
In responding to this question, clearly describe how you intend to overcome the scientific or technological uncertainty that you identified at line 042.

Outline the proposed hypothesis/solution and describe the experimentation or analysis you plan to use to test it.

Indicate whether external resources, such as contractors or specialized services (e.g., testing, analysis, or laboratory work), will be involved and their specific roles in the project.

Section C – Additional project information

Provide additional information about the context of your project. A better understanding of the context may allow the CRA to expedite your application.

Lines 050 to 060 – Who prepared the technical descriptions?
Provide information on who prepared the responses for Section B above. Select one only (line 050 or 054) and provide the relevant information.

Section D – Supporting information to be included in the application

The following information must be submitted as part of contemporaneous documents that your business has generated.

  • Project planning documents that outline the timeline, key milestones, and major activities or phases (examples include project plans, Gantt charts, work breakdown structures, schedules, or planning briefs)
  • A description of planned internal controls for tracking SR&ED work and associated expenditures, including documentation practices, and how you will maintain contemporaneous documentation throughout the project (examples include time-tracking processes, documentation protocols, internal review procedures, or controls for capturing experimental records)
  • Key supporting references and documents that demonstrate evidence of due diligence in determining that no known solution exists to address the scientific or technological uncertainty (such as scientific articles and technical papers, meeting minutes, design specifications, system architecture diagrams, photographs, time logs, or any other evidence directly related to the eligibility of the proposed project for SR&ED)
  • List of project team members and their respective areas of expertise, including potential or prospective project members

Part 3 – Certification

Lines 070 to 072 – Authorization and date
Provide the name of the individual, authorized signing officer of the corporation, or authorized partner who has certified the information on the application form and the attachments. The CRA requires that the pre-claim approval application be signed and dated.
Line 074 to 076– Name of person/firm who completed this form
If applicable, enter the name of the person/firm or representative that helped you complete this form and the business number, if different from line 010.
