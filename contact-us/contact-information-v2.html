Answers to top questions

Access a CRA account
You need a CRA security code to sign in

If you don’t have your code yet

It may take up to 10 business days to get a CRA security code by mail.

If you don’t want to wait, you may be able to use Interac® document verification service to validate your identity to get your full access immediately.

If you have received your code

If you cannot find your CRA security code, you may be able to use Interac® document verification service to validate your identity to get your full access immediately.

Even if your code has expired, you can try to sign in using Interac verification. Otherwise, you can request a new code to be sent by mail.

How to use Interac verification instead of a security code

This will give you access to your account right away.

To use Interac verification:

  1. Sign in using CRA sign-in services
  2. Select your sign-in method and follow the instructions
  3. To validate your identity, select Interac document verification service
If you lost your code and can't use Interac verification

You will need to call the CRA to receive another security code by mail.

Before you call:

  1. Have your tax documents ready to validate your identity
  2. Call 1-800-959-8281
  3. Select option 1 for online services
You can’t remember your CRA user ID or password

You can recover your CRA user ID or password online.

For My Account

To recover your ID or password:

  1. Go to Sign in to My Account
  2. Select CRA sign in
  3. Choose Forgot your user ID or Forgot your password
    • If you forgot your user ID, you will need your:
      • Social insurance number (SIN)
      • Date of birth
      • A copy of your assessed individual tax return from this year or last year
      • Answers to 4 security questions you set up during registration
    • If you forgot your password, you will need your:
      • User ID
      • Answers to 4 security questions you set up during registration
If you still can't recover your user ID or password
  1. Have your tax documents ready to validate your identity
  2. Call 1-800-959-8281
  3. Select option 1 for online services
For My Business Account

To recover your ID or password:

  1. Go to Sign in to My Business Account
  2. Select CRA sign in
  3. Choose Forgot your user ID or Forgot your password
    • If you forgot your user ID, you will need your:
      • Social insurance number (SIN)

      • Answers to 4 security questions you set up during registration

You're having trouble with a Sign-In Partner

Contact your financial institution if you have issues with your sign-in information.

To change your Sign-In Partner

If you still have access to the Sign-In Partner you previously used, you can change your Sign-In Partner at any time in your CRA account:

  1. Sign in using CRA sign-in services
  2. Select Sign-In partner
  3. Look for Switch My Sign-In Partner on the top of the Interac sign-in service page and follow the instructions
  4. After completing the switch, you will then continue to your CRA account
If you can't access your previous Sign-In Partner

You will have to register with your new Sign-In Partner:

  1. Have your tax documents ready to validate your identity
  2. Call 1-800-959-8281
  3. Select option 1 for online services

Check the status of your tax refund

How you file your personal income tax return can affect when you get your refund.

Standard processing times

The CRA aims to send you a notice of assessment, as well as any refund, within the following timelines:

  • Online filing: 2 weeks
  • Mailed paper return: 8 weeks
  • Non-resident return: 16 weeks

These timelines apply only to returns we receive on or before the due date.

Check the status of your return

You may check the status of your return online using your CRA My Account.

Sign in to My Account

For other options: Check the status of your return

Why you may not have received your refund

If you have another government debt, the CRA may have kept all or part of your refund.

How benefits and credits can be applied to your debt

If you got an automated call about your instalment payments

You may have received a reminder call from the CRA between May 27 and June 14, 2024.

This was a follow-up to the reminder we sent you in February 2024 about making quarterly instalment payments.

The next instalment payment is due June 15, 2024 (June 17, 2024, since June 15 is a Saturday).

How much you have to pay

Check the reminder we sent you in February 2024 to find out how much you should pay.

If your situation has changed or you would like to stop paying by instalments, refer to Options to calculate.

For more details about your instalment payment:

  1. Sign in to CRA My Account
  2. Go to the Accounts and payments tab
  3. Scroll to Instalments

How to make or confirm a payment

You may make your payment using your usual payment method.

Review payment options

To check online that we received your payment, go to Confirm your instalment.

For more information about tax instalment payments, go to Required tax instalments for individuals.

Find out why your benefit payment is missing

Why benefit payments stop

Your payments may stop if you did not:

  • File your tax return
  • Respond to a letter from the CRA about your situation
  • Inform the CRA of a change in:
    • Your address
    • Your banking information
    • Marital status
    • Child custody

Your payments may stop if your spouse or common-law partner did not:

  • File their tax return
  • Inform the CRA of a change in:
    • Marital status
    • Child custody

Privacy notice

The Canada Revenue Agency’s (CRA) call centres record some calls. These call recordings are used to evaluate the quality and accuracy of agents’ responses, identify call trends, and provide insight into potential areas in which training would be beneficial. Recording begins from the moment an agent answers the call and continues until the line is disconnected, including any time spent on hold.

If you do not wish for your call to be recorded, please inform the agent at the beginning of the call.

During your conversation with an agent and when using the automated self-serve options, you may be asked to provide personal information in order to validate your identity. The questions asked are in place to ensure the safety and security of your account. Without this verification step, the CRA is unable to access your account information or make changes.

Personal information is collected pursuant to the Income Tax Act and Excise Tax Act. Personal information is described in program-specific Personal Information Banks which can be found on the CRA’s Information about Programs and Information Holdings (formerly Info Source) page.

Under the Privacy Act individuals have a right of protection, access to, and correction or notation of their personal information. Individuals also have a right to file a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding our handling of their information.

Interac® document verification service

Interac document verification service is an alternative way to validate your identity. This secure method gives you immediate access to CRA sign-in services.

To use this service, you must:

  • Be 16 years of age or older
  • Have an accepted government-issued photo identification
  • Have a mobile device with a working camera

Gather your identification documents

The following original government-issued identification documents are accepted:

  • Canadian passport
  • Canadian driver's license
  • Provincial/Territorial photo ID card

We do not accept:

  • Provincial health cards
  • Documents that have been reported as lost or stolen
  • Expired or damaged identification documents
  • Photocopies of identification document
  • Pictures of yourself that were not taken in real-time
