Access a CRA account for someone else

To access a CRA account for someone else, including a spouse or family member, you must be their authorized representative and use Represent a Client in your CRA account.

By phone

Call the CRA to get help to:

  • unlock your CRA Account
  • update personal information or make payments
  • discuss your tax situation and your section 216 return
  • cancel email notifications and get a copy of your Notice of Assessment
Before you call
Have all of the following information ready to verify your identity:
  • SIN, ITN, or TTN
  • Full name and date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, or other tax document

You can also be signed in to your CRA account to access your tax information.

Calling on behalf of someone else

You can get account specific information for someone else in the following 2 situations:

  • The person is present on the call to verify their identity in English or French and can provide verbal authorization for you to speak for them

  • You are the person's authorized or legal representative and your authorization has been confirmed

    For details: Authorize a representative

Non-Resident Withholding Section

The Non-Resident Withholding Section (NRWS) maintains and monitors the accounts of Canadian payers and non-resident individuals who are required to pay non-resident withholding tax on their income from Canadian sources.
