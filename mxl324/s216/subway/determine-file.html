Determine if you should elect

As a non-resident of Canada, you can choose to send a separate return to pay less taxes. Choosing to send the CRA this return is called electing under section 216 of the Income Tax Act.

Choosing not to elect

If you do not elect under Section 216, the withholding tax of 25% on your gross rental income is considered your final tax obligation. You don’t have to file a return.

Filing a section 216 return after the end of the year

Electing under section 216 allows you to recalculate tax on your net Canadian-source rental income instead of the gross amount and get the difference refunded to you

Filing a section 216 return is beneficial if the 25% non-resident tax withheld during the year is more than the amount of tax payable on your Section 216 return.

To make this choice, complete Form T1159, Income Tax Return for Electing under Section 216 and mail it within two years after the end of the rental income year.

For more details, go to How to complete your return.

Sending Form NR6 on or before January 1

You can elect to have tax withheld during the year on your net rental income instead of the gross amount. If the CRA approves your Form NR6, you must file a Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025.

To make this choice, you (or each non-resident who is a member of a partnership) and your agent have to complete Form NR6, Undertaking to File an Income Tax Return by a Non-Resident Receiving Rent from Real or Immovable Property or Receiving a Timber Royalty, and send it to the CRA for approval on or before January 1 of each year or before the first rental payment is due.

Your agent must continue to withhold non-resident tax on the gross rental income until the CRA approves your Form NR6 in writing.

If the CRA approved your Form NR6

If you sent the CRA Form NR6 for 2024 and the CRA approved it, you must file your 2024 Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025.

You must include the income and expenses from all of your Canadian rental properties in one Section 216 return. You have to file a return even if you have no tax payable or are not expecting a refund.

If you do not fulfill your obligation, you will have to pay to the full amount of non-resident tax, as well as applicable interest charges. We calculate the required amount of tax at the statutory rate of 25% of the gross rental income, minus the amount of tax already remitted for the year.

What your agent will do after the CRA approves Form NR6

Your agent must continue to withhold non-resident tax on the gross rental income until the CRA approves your Form NR6 in writing.

After the CRA approves your Form NR6, your agent can withhold non-resident tax of 25% on your net rental income (that is the amount of rental income available after the rental expenses have been paid). Your agent must pay the tax on or before the 15th day of the month after the month the rental income is paid or credited to you. You should discuss this with your agent to make sure they withhold and remit the correct amount of non-resident tax to the CRA on your behalf.

If your agent does not withhold and remit this non-resident tax, the CRA will charge compound daily interest on the amount not withheld and remitted. The CRA may also charge a penalty.

The agent has to give you two copies of an NR4 slip showing the gross amount of rental income paid or credited to you during the year and the amount of non-resident tax withheld. The agent also has to send the CRA a completed NR4 information return as explained in Guide T4061, NR4 – Non-Resident Tax Withholding, Remitting, and Reporting.

To get help with Form NR6 or enquire about the processing status, contact the Non-Resident Withholding Section

If your Form NR6 was not approved

Your agent must continue to withhold non-resident tax on the gross rental income until the end of the year. You may choose to file a section 216 return after the end of the year.

If you sold your rental property

You must file a section 216 return if both of the following conditions apply:

  • You previously claimed Capital Cost Allowance (CCA) under Section 216 for this property.
  • You are including a recapture of CCA on your return.

To determine if you have a recapture of CCA, you may use Form T776 to calculate the recapture of CCA. For instructions, go to Recaptured capital cost allowance.

If you must file, send your Section 216 return and pay any balance owing by April 30 of the year following the sale, whether you completed Form NR6 or not.

If you have rental income from more than one rental property in Canada, you must report all of your Canadian rental income and expenses together in one Section 216 return.

For more information on the special rules that apply when disposing of your Canadian rental property, go to:

  • Disposing of or acquiring certain Canadian property
  • Information Circular IC72-17R6, Procedures Concerning the Disposition of Taxable Canadian Property by Non-Residents of Canada – Section 116
