When to file a return

The CRA will only accept your section 216 election if your return is filed by the due date.

Filing dates for 2024 taxes

  • April 30, 2025: Deadline to file if you have to include a recapture of CCA on your 2024 return
  • June 30, 2025: Deadline to file if the CRA approved NR6 form for 2024
  • December 31, 2026: Deadline to file your section 216 return for 2024

Payment due date for 2024 taxes

  • April 30, 2025: Deadline to pay your taxes

How to determine your filing due date

Generally, you have to send the CRA your section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you. This applies if your agent withheld non-resident tax of 25% on your gross rental income:

Your due date you have an approved NR6 form or if you disposed of your Canadian rental property in 2024.

Approved NR6 form for 2024

If you sent Form NR6 and the CRA approved it, you must file a Section 216 return for that year. You have to file a return even if you have no tax payable or are not expecting a refund.

You have to file your 2024 Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025, and include the income and expenses from all of your Canadian rental properties.

If you have a net rental loss, you still have to file your Section 216 return by the due date.

Disposition of a Canadian rental property in 2024

Whether or not you completed Form NR6, you must file a Section 216 return for 2024 by April 30, 2025, if both of the following conditions apply:

  • you disposed of rental property in 2024 that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for
  • you are including the recapture of CCA on your 2024 return

If you have disposed of the rental property and have to include a recapture of capital cost allowance (CCA) on your 2024 Section 216 return, any balance owing is due by April 30, 2025.

Exception to the due date

When a due date falls on a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday recognized by the CRA, your return is considered on time if the CRA receives it or if it is postmarked on or before the next business day.

Your payment is considered on time if it is received on the first business day after the due date.

For more information, go to Due dates and payment dates.

Late filing

If you do not send the CRA your section 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid. If the payer did not withhold the correct amount of non-resident tax from your rental income, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to you.

If the CRA approved your Form NR6 for the year and you do not file your section 216 return by the due date, you will be subject to non-resident tax on your gross rental income. If the correct amount of this tax was not withheld at source, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to your agent.

For details, go to Subsection 216(1) Late-filing Policy
