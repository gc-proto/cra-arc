When to file a return

The CRA will only accept your section 216 election if your return is filed by the due date.

Filing and payment dates

A section 216 election must be filed by the due date. If you have a balance owing, you must pay it by the payment due date to avoid interest charges on unpaid amounts.

Generally, you have to send the CRA your section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you.

December 31, 2026
Send the CRA your section 216 return for rental income that was paid or credited to you in 2024
June 30, 2025
Filing due date for a section 216 return with an approved NR6 form for 2024
April 30, 2025

Filing due date for a section 216 return with or without an approved NR6 form, if both of the following apply:

  • you disposed of rental property in 2024 that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for
  • you are including the recapture of CCA on your 2024 return

Note

If you have a net rental loss, you still have to file your section 216 return by the due date.

The CRA can extend the due date for filing your return in certain circumstances. For more information, contact the Canada Revenue Agency.

When a due date falls on a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday recognized by the CRA, your return is considered on time if the CRA receives it or if it is postmarked on or before the next business day. Your payment is considered on time if it is received on the first business day after the due date.

For more information, go to Due dates and payment dates.

Late filing

If you do not send the CRA yoursection 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid. If the payer did not withhold the correct amount of non-resident tax from your rental income, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to you.

If the CRA approved your Form NR6 for the year and you do not file your section 216 return by the due date, you will be subject to non-resident tax on your gross rental income. If the correct amount of this tax was not withheld at source, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to your agent.
