When to file a return

The CRA will only accept your section 216 election if your return is filed by the due date.

Filing and payment dates

A section 216 election must be filed by the due date. If you have a balance owing, you must pay it by the payment due date to avoid interest charges on unpaid amounts.

Generally, you have to send the CRA your section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you.

December 31, 2026
Send the CRA your section 216 return for rental income that was paid or credited to you in 2024
June 30, 2025
Filing due date for a section 216 return with an approved NR6 form for 2024
April 30, 2025

Filing due date for a section 216 return with or without an approved NR6 form, if both of the following apply:

  • you disposed of rental property in 2024 that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for
  • you are including the recapture of CCA on your 2024 return

Note

If you have a net rental loss, you still have to file your section 216 return by the due date.

The CRA can extend the due date for filing your return in certain circumstances. For more information, contact the Canada Revenue Agency.

When a due date falls on a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday recognized by the CRA, your return is considered on time if the CRA receives it or if it is postmarked on or before the next business day. Your payment is considered on time if it is received on the first business day after the due date.

For more information, go to Due dates and payment dates.

Late filing

If you do not send the CRA yoursection 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid. If the payer did not withhold the correct amount of non-resident tax from your rental income, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to you.

If the CRA approved your Form NR6 for the year and you do not file your section 216 return by the due date, you will be subject to non-resident tax on your gross rental income. If the correct amount of this tax was not withheld at source, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to your agent.

Before you file

Section 216 return due date

Generally, you have to send the CRA your Section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you.

If you sent the CRA Form NR6 for 2024 and the CRA approved it, you have to file your 2024 Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025, and include the income and expenses from all of your Canadian rental properties.

Whether or not you completed Form NR6, you must file a Section 216 return for 2024 by April 30, 2025, if both of the following conditions apply:

  • You disposed of rental property in 2024 that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for
  • You are including the recapture of CCA on your 2024 return

Note

If you have a net rental loss, you still have to file your Section 216 return by the due date.

The CRA can extend the due date for filing your return in certain circumstances. For more information, contact the Canada Revenue Agency.

Exceptions to due date

When a due date falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday recognized by the CRA, your return is considered on time if the CRA receives it or if it is postmarked on or before the next business day. For more information, go to Due dates and payment dates.

If you do not file your Section 216 return by the due date

If you do not send the CRA your Section 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid. If the payer did not withhold the correct amount of non-resident tax from your rental income, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to you.

If the CRA approved your Form NR6 for the year and you do not file your Section 216 return by the due date, you will be subject to non-resident tax on your gross rental income. If the correct amount of this tax was not withheld at source, the CRA will issue a non-resident tax assessment to your agent.

Example of due dates

You are a resident of Australia who rents out a property that you own in Canada. You and your agent completed Form NR6 and sent it to the CRA before January 1, 2024, and the CRA approved it.

For 2024, you received rental income and had rental expenses as follows:

Gross rental income

$20,000

Minus allowable expenses

-$15,000

Equals net rental income

$5,000

Because the CRA approved Form NR6, your agent was able to withhold and remit non-resident tax for 2024 on the net rental income (25% of $5,000) rather than on the gross rental income (25% of $20,000).

You must file a Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025. If you did not send the return by the due date, your agent will have to pay an additional $3,750 plus interest on your account. This $3,750 is the difference between the amount withheld on your net rental income (25% of $5,000 = $1,250) and the amount you would have to pay on your gross rental income (25% of $20,000 = $5,000) if you had not filed Form NR6.

Section 216 payment due date

If you have a balance owing for 2024, you must pay it on or before April 30, 2025. If your payment is late, the CRA will charge interest on the balance owing starting on May 1, 2025.

If you have disposed of the rental property and have to include a recapture of capital cost allowance (CCA) on your 2024 Section 216 return, any balance owing is due by April 30, 2025.
