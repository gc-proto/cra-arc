Who can elect

To file a valid election under section 216 of the Income Tax Act, you must meet all the following criteria :

  1. You were a non-resident of Canada for all or part of 2024
    2. Who is considered a non-resident for tax purposes?

    Generally, you are a non-resident of Canada for tax purposes if you permanently live outside Canada and do not have any residential ties with Canada.

    For more information, see:

    • Income Tax Folio S5-F1-C1Determining an Individual’s Residence Status
    • Income Tax Folio S6-F1-C1, Residence of a Trust or Estate.
  2. While you were a non-resident of Canada, you may have received the following types of income in 2024::
    • rental income from real or immovable property in Canada
    • timber royalties on a timber resource property or timber limit in Canada
    • proceeds of disposition of rental property (Canadian real or immovable property, timber resource property, or timber limit) that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for on a Section 216 return and, as a result of the disposition, you have a recapture of that CCA
  3. Your earned personal rental income as an individual or as a member of a partnership
    4. What is not considered personal rental income

    Income from a flipped property

    If you owned or held a rental property in Canada for less than 365 consecutive days before it's disposition, it is generally considered to be a flipped property. The resulting profit on the disposition is taxable as business income and not as a capital gain, unless the property was already considered inventory if the disposition occurred due to, or in anticipation of, certain life events.

    For more information about flipped property and the life-event exceptions, refer to Find out which tax package you should use.

  4. You are reporting all of your Canadian rental income, timber royalties and expenses together in one Section 216 return
  5. You are within the timeframe to file an Section 216 return
    6. What are Section 216 return due date

    Generally, you have to send the CRA your Section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you.

    If you sent the CRA Form NR6 for 2024 and the CRA approved it, you have to file your 2024 Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025, and include the income and expenses from all of your Canadian rental properties.

    Whether or not you completed Form NR6, you must file a Section 216 return for 2024 by April 30, 2025, if both of the following conditions apply:

    • You disposed of rental property in 2024 that you had previously claimed capital cost allowance (CCA) for
    • You are including the recapture of CCA on your 2024 return>

    If you do not send the CRA your Section 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid.

    For more information, go to When to file Section 216 return

Eligible income

You can only report the following Canadian-source income earned in the calendar year:

  • Rental income from all of your Canadian rental properties
  • Timber royalties

Other Canadian-source income

You cannot include the following on your section 216 return: 

  • Interest income
  • Capital gains from d
  • Business income
  • Employment income

If you have other Canadian-source income for 2024, you may have to send the CRA a separate Canadian return to report it.

If you received Canadian-source employment or business income or you realized a taxable capital gain from disposing of taxable Canadian property in 2024, see Guide T4058, Non-Residents and Income Tax.

Disposing of your rental property

If you have disposed of or are planning to dispose of your Canadian rental property, go to Disposing of or acquiring certain Canadian property or see Information Circular IC72-17R6, Procedures Concerning the Disposition of Taxable Canadian Property by Non-Residents of Canada – Section 116, for the special rules that apply.
