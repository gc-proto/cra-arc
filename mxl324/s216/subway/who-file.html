Who can elect

To file a valid election under section 216 of the Income Tax Act, you must meet all the following criteria :

  • You were a non-resident of Canada for all or part of 2024
    • Who is considered a non-resident for tax purposes?

    Generally, you are a non-resident of Canada for tax purposes if you permanently live outside Canada and do not have any residential ties with Canada.

    For more information: Determining your residency status

  • While you were a non-resident of Canada, you received one of the following types of income in 2024:
    • Rental income from real or immovable property in Canada
    • Timber royalties on a timber resource property or timber limit in Canada
    What type of income can you report on a section 216 return

    Eligible income

    You can only report the following Canadian-source income earned in the calendar year:

    • Rental income from all of your Canadian rental properties
    • Timber royalties

    Non-eligible Canadian-source income

    You cannot include the following on your section 216 return:

    • Employment income
    • Business income
    • Capital gains
    • Interest income

    If you have other Canadian-source income for 2024, you may have to send the CRA a separate Canadian return to report it. For more information, go to Guide T4058, Non-Residents and Income Tax.

  • Your earned personal rental income as an individual or as a member of a partnership
    • What is not considered personal rental income

    Income from a flipped property

    If you owned or held a rental property in Canada for less than 365 consecutive days before it's disposition, it is generally considered to be a flipped property. The resulting profit on the disposition is taxable as business income and not as a capital gain, unless the property was already considered inventory if the disposition occurred due to, or in anticipation of, certain life events.

    For more information about flipped property and the life-event exceptions, refer to Find out which tax package you should use.

  • You are reporting all of your Canadian rental income, timber royalties and expenses together in one Section 216 return
  • You are within the timeframe to file an Section 216 return
    • What are the due dated for a sSection 216 return

    Generally, you have to send the CRA your Section 216 return within two years from the end of the year that the rental income was paid or credited to you. However, your due date may be different if one of the following applies to you:

    • The CRA approved your Form NR6 for 2024: you have to file your 2024 Section 216 return on or before June 30, 2025, and include the income and expenses from all of your Canadian rental properties.
    • You disposed of your rental property (Canadian real or immovable property, timber resource property, or timber limit), and you have a recapture of CCA from a previous Section 216 return: you must file a Section 216 return for 2024 by April 30, 2025

    If you do not send the CRA your Section 216 return by the due date, your election will be invalid.

    For more information, go to When to file Section 216 return
