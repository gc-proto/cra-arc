Canadian payers (such as the tenant) or agents (such as the property manager) must withhold non-resident tax on the rental income they pay or credit to a non-resident of Canada. If you are a non-resident, the tax withheld is usually your final tax obligation to Canada on the rental income.

You can choose, however, to file a Canadian return to report your rental income from real or immovable property income by electing under section 216 of the Income Tax Act. By making this election, you pay tax on your net Canadian-source rental income instead of the gross amount and may receive a refund for all or part of the non-resident tax withheld
