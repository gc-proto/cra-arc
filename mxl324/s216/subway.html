Electing under section 216

As a non-resident of Canada, the non-resident tax withheld on your Canadian rental income is usually your final tax obligation to Canada.

However, you can choose, to file a separate Canadian tax return to report your rental income from real or immovable property income. Choosing to send this return is called electing under section 216 of the Income Tax Act. By making this election, you pay tax on your net Canadian-source rental income instead of the gross amount, to reduce the non-resident tax withheld or get a refund for the difference.
