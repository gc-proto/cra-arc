Mail

Mail is a secure tool within your CRA account to review certain correspondence items from the CRA.

The information provided here will help you understand what you can do in Mail.

View, print and save

To get the paper version of any CRA mail, you must first click on the piece of correspondence and select "PDF" before printing or saving it.

If you want to view a correspondence item later, without having to sign in to My Account, you can save it to your computer.

How to view, print and save
Options to view, print and save
Preview pane A correspondence item is a mail item. Select the correspondence item you want to view, print or save by clicking on an item in the correspondence list. The preview will appear on the right side of the page.
PDF - Portable Document Format The “PDF” button on the preview pane lets you download a correspondence item in a printer-friendly format.
Print or save The “Print/Save” button lets you print the screen you are currently viewing.
Open The “Open” button lets you fully view a correspondence item.
Action may be required The warning "Action may be required" is visible if a reply or payment is expected. Correspondence displaying this message will have buttons to reply, submit documents or make a payment based on the required action when viewed.

Sort and filter your mail

By default, the most recent correspondence items are at the top of the list.

To reset all filters and sort options, you can refresh the page.

Options to sort your mail
Sorting options
Date The date field shows the date the correspondence item was issued. You can arrange your correspondence items from newest to oldest or oldest to newest by selecting the “Date” sort option.
Status To arrange your correspondence items by status, select Read or Unread.
Subject You can sort your correspondence items by subject, either by selecting A to Z or from Z to A. A subject in bold means the status of the item is Unread.
Reference number You can sort your correspondence items by reference number, either by selecting Ascending or Descending. A reference number is a case, reference , or confirmation number assigned to your correspondence item. Not all items have a reference number.
Options to filter your mail
Filter options
Correspondence type

Only certain correspondence items that the CRA sends you are eligible to be displayed in Mail. To see a specific type of correspondence on the mail page, you can choose one of the options from the drop-down list.

Correspondence types available
  • Instalments
  • Information
  • Letters
  • Notices
  • Preassessment review letters
  • Processing review letters
  • Statements
Date range The date range filter lets you view correspondence items that were issued within a specific time period. Eligible correspondence items issued to you since February 15, 2016, will be available for up to 10 years.
Service The service filter is on the mail page only if you have mail items related to audit enquiries. Otherwise, you will not see it. This filter lets you view just your mail items that are related to audit enquiries.
Tax year The tax year filter lets you see correspondence items related to a specific tax year.
Remove a filter Use the “X” button next to the name of the filter, or use the “Clear filters” button to clear all filters at once.

Audit enquiries

Audit enquiry correspondence items are grouped by case number. 

Audit enquiry attachments

Attachments are represented by this icon: ““. After you select an item and the preview pane opens, you will see the “Attachment” button. You can select this button to download, print or save the attachment.

Access services

The “Access services” button directs you to services within the My Account Portal. When you select this button, a drop-down menu will appear with the following options:

  • Submit an audit enquiry
  • File a formal dispute
  • Request relief of penalties and interest
