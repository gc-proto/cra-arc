Digital Services Tax

Status: Not yet available

To ensure tax fairness for Canadian businesses competing in the global economy and in the absence of a multilateral convention, the Government of Canada enacted the Digital Services Tax (DST). The DST requires foreign and domestic large businesses to pay tax on the revenue earned from engaging with online users in Canada.

The DST applies at a rate of 3% on revenue earned from certain digital services that rely on the engagement, data, and content contributions of Canadian users, and on the sales or licensing of Canadian user data.

Canadian digital services revenue is calculated on a calendar year basis and is earned from:

  • Online marketplace services
  • Online advertising services
  • Social media services
  • User data

Foreign and domestic large businesses affected by the DST, including digital businesses, will have to apply to register and may have to file a return.

Who must apply to register

A foreign or domestic business must apply to register by January 31, 2025, if for any one of the 2022, 2023 or 2024 calendar years:

  • The business had Canadian digital services revenue greater than nil in the calendar year
  • The business had, or was a member of a consolidated group that had, Canadian digital services revenue greater than CAN$10 million in the calendar year
  • The business had, or was a member of a consolidated group that had, total revenue of at least €750 million during a fiscal year that ended in the prior calendar year

How to apply to register

Information on how to apply to register will be available after the coming-into-force date is fixed.

Who must file

A foreign or domestic business must file a return by June 30, 2025, if for any one of the 2022, 2023, or 2024 calendar years:

  • The business had Canadian digital services revenue greater than nil in the calendar yearr/li>
  • The business had, or was a member of a consolidated group that had, Canadian digital services revenue greater than CAN$20 million in the calendar year
  • The business had, or was a member of a consolidated group that had, total revenue of at least €750 million during a fiscal year that ended in the prior calendar year

How to file

Information on how to file a return will be provided once the coming-into-force date is fixed.

Source of the new tax

The Digital Services Tax Act (DSTA) received Royal Assent on [date].

The coming-into-force date of the DSTA will be fixed by order of the Governor in Council, but cannot be earlier than January 1, 2024. The Act will not apply to revenue earned before January 1, 2022.
