Make an election

Foreign or domestic businesses who must file a return under the Digital Services Tax Act may file a designated entity election with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

Businesses part of more than one consolidated group in a calendar year cannot make this election for that year

On this page

  • What is a designated entity election
  • What are the outcomes of a designated entity election
  • When to make a designated entity election
  • How to make a designated entity election

What is a designated entity election

A designated entity election:

  • allows you to designate another member in your consolidated group to act on your behalf for a calendar year
  • is an annual election that applies for only one calendar year
  • is jointly made by you and the designated entity (DE)
  • can be used by several members of your consolidated group to elect the same DE

Note: To elect a DE, the entity must be registered for a digital services tax (DST) program account even if the entity does not have any Canadian digital services revenue. If your intended DE is not registered for a DST program account, it must apply for registration at the time of the election. For information on how to apply for registration, go to XXXXXXX

Outcomes of the designated entity election

After a designated entity election has been made, for the general, administrative, and enforcement purposes of the Digital Services Tax Act, the DE:

  • will take actions on your behalf (deemed to be performed by you) for the calendar year, such as file the return and pay any amounts owed
  • will receive communication from the CRA for the calendar year, such as emails and notifications
  • will receive confidential information from the CRA for the calendar year, such as any outstanding balance from a prior year

If you have concerns that information related to the DST from prior years might be communicated to the DE, do not make this election.  

When to make a designated entity election

To be valid for a particular calendar year, the designated entity election must be made on or before June 30 of the following calendar year

How to make a designated entity election

There is not a prescribed form for the designated entity election. The CRA will accept a document containing the following mandatory information:

General information:

  • Name of the ultimate parent entity of your consolidated group
  • Tax identification number (TIN), if known, of the ultimate parent entity 

What is a TIN

  • In Canada: A TIN is a business number or an account number issued to a trust.
  • Outside of Canada: A TIN is a unique number used to identify an individual or entity for tax purposes.

For more information on the use of TINs outside of Canada, refer to the OECD's web page: Tax Identification Numbers

    • Calendar year to which the election applies

Information about the DE:

  • Name of the DE
  • Business number with DST program account (XXXXX XXXX DTXXXX) of the DE
  • Name of authorized officer, position or office, telephone number and extension, and email address
  • Date and signature of authorized officer (electronic signatures accepted)

Information about the group member, or members, making the election:

  • Name of the entity
  • Business number with DST program account (XXXXX XXXX DTXXXX) of the entity
  • Name of authorized officer, position or office, telephone number and extension, and email address
  • Date and signature of authorized officer (this can be done electronically)
  • A statement which acknowledges that the signatories jointly elect to designate the DE in respect of the calendar year pursuant to subsection 46(1) of the DSTA.

Do not send this document to the CRA. You must keep it in case the CRA asks to see it.

You must keep all records that are necessary to determine whether you have complied with the DSTA  for a period of eight years after the end of the calendar year to which they relate
