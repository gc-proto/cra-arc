What is a PIER review

Every year, the CRA reviews the information on the T4 slips to ensure that the following figures match:

  • Required amounts of CPP and EI calculated from the employee's pensionable and insurable earnings
  • Reported amounts of CPP and EI on the employee's T4 slips

How are the results provided

If there are discrepancies between the required and reported amounts, the results are printed on a PIER listing. If you report electronically and report an employee number on the T4 slip, you can view the employee number on the PIER listing.

The report includes:

  • PIER listing which includes the affected employees and the figures used by CRA in the calculations
  • PIER summary that shows any balance due
Examples of common errors
  • Box 28 (Exempt CPP/QPP, EI, or PPIP) of the T4 slip is not completed correctly
  • An employee's change of age has resulted in a change in pensionable status (turned 18 or 70)
  • The SIN and/or name on the T4 was entered incorrectly
  • An employee has exceeded the maximum rate of CPP and/or EI contributions but you did not stop the deductions

What is the timeline for a PIER process

Timeline Steps in the PIER process
Day 1 Initial letter (PIER package) sent to you which requests a reply within 30 calendar days
Day 45 If you do not reply or make a payment in full, an automatic assessment is raised and a notice of assessment is issued.
Day 65 We will issue amended T4s to you.

What methods we used to send you the PIER notification

The PIER will be sent to you in one of the following methods:

  • Electronically when you are registered for MyBA or RAC in the PIER overview option:
    • PIER information will be available in MyBA and RAC for the last 5 years
    • You can view, download, and reply to the PIER letter in MyBA or RAC
  • Mail if you are not registered for the electronic services

How to respond to a PIER

You are not required to respond to the PIER if you agree with our calculations and you will be remitting (paying) the exact amount shown on the PIER.

What if you disagree with the PIER

You are required to respond to the PIER if you do not agree with our calculations. You must respond by returning the PIER with any corrected information and an explanation. The response can be sent using:

  • MyBA or RAC
  • Fax number or the address provided on the PIER

If you do not reply or make a payment in full by the reply date noted on the PIER, an automatic assessment is raised and a notice of assessment (NOA) is issued to you. The NOA will include applicable penalties or interest, or both.

In either situation, do not send amended T4 slips. We will issue any amended slip to you.

What are the payment methods

If you are using MyBA, you will have a Proceed to pay option in the PIER Overview section. This will allow you to do either of the following:

  • Pay the PIER deficiencies in full without replying
  • Pay any remaining deficiencies after you provided a response to their PIER

This payment option is not available in RAC.

What if adjustments are required on T4 slips

If adjustments are required on T4 slips due to a PIER review, we will prepare two copies of the amended T4 slip and forward them to you. One copy for your records and one copy to be sent to your employee.
