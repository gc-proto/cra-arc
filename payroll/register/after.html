What to expect from the CRA

Reference number

You will receive a reference number after you submit your online registration form. You can use this reference number to ask about the status of your registration.

You may be contacted by the CRA by phone to collect or validate information. When we contact you, we will identify ourselves using information from your registration request. We will also give you instructions that will allow you to validate that you are speaking to a CRA agent.

Business number

You will receive a business number once we review and validate the information you provided. We may contact you by phone or send you a letter with your business number.

You will also be emailed a registration package. This package includes general information about registration, additional forms you may need. We will also send you a separate e-mail with links to tax guides and general tax information.

Remittance forms

You will receive a remittance form in the mail after you register your account. This can be used for your next remittance. If you do not receive a form in time for your next remittance, send in your remittance by the due date.

New employers are considered regular remitters. Send your remittance monthly unless we tell you to remit using a different frequency.

Learn more: Remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions.

When to open a separate payroll account

You can request a separate RP account under your existing business number (BN) for many reasons, including administrative convenience. For example, to request a separate RP account be opened under your BN is under your existing for employees participating in a wage-loss replacement plan for short-term disability.

You must apply for the Reduced EI premium rate through Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC). Once approved by ESDC, you must let us know which account will be assigned as the Reduced EI premium rate account.

Once the additional RP account is created, you must contact ESDC to have the reduced rate applied to the account.

Learn more: How to reduce the EI premium rate if you provide your employees with a short-term disability plan.
