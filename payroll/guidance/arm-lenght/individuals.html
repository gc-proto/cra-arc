Two individuals

You must determine the relationship with the two individuals to determine if they are related to each other.


Confirm if individuals are connected by

Blood relationship

The following individuals are related to each other by blood:

  • A parent and a child (or other descendant, such as a grandchild or a great-grandchild)
  • A brother and a sister

Blood relatives do not include more distant relatives such as aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews or cousins.

Example
Description

Zuly and Silvia are sisters. Zuly is married to Juan and Silvia is married to Yuri. Zuly and Silvia are related because they are sisters. Zuly and Juan are related by marriage. Silvia and Yuri are related by marriage.

Result

As a result of their marriages, Yuri is related to Zuly and Juan is related to Silvia, but Juan and Yuri are not related to each other.

Marriage

Individuals are related to each other if they are legally married to each other. They are also related to anyone connected by blood relationship (parents and siblings) to their spouse.

If the marriage is dissolved because of divorce or death of a spouse, the individual ceases to be connected by marriage.

Depending on the situation, the person may be related by adoption to any children of their spouse, continue to: Adoption.

Example
Description

Jack and Jill were married on June 15, 2016. Later, their marriage broke down and they were legally divorced on October 1, 2019.

Result

For the period from the date of marriage until the date of their legal divorce, Jack and Jill were related to each other as well as their respective parents-in-law and brothers/sisters-in-law.

Common-law partnership

Individuals are related to each other if they are in a common-law partnership with each other. The CRA considers that 2 individuals are in a common-law partnership in one of the following situations:

  • They live together in a conjugal relationship and have done so for a continuous period of at least 12 months
  • They live together in a conjugal relationship and have a child together (this includes through adoption), in which case the 12-month period does not apply

They are also related to anyone connected by blood relationship (parents and siblings) to their common-law partner.

If there is a breakdown in the relationship, they are considered to continue to live in a common-law partnership until they have lived separate and apart for 90 consecutive days.

Depending on the situation, the person may be related by adoption to any children of their common-law partner, depending on the circumstances. Adoption.

Example
Description

Jack and Jill had a child together. They began living together in a conjugal relationship 1 month before their child was born.

Result

They are common-law partners as of the day their child is born. If they did not have a child together, they would not be common-law partners until they lived together for 12 months.

Adoption

Individuals are related to each other through adoption if one individual is the adopted child of the other.

In addition to their parents, the child is also related to their siblings, grandparents and great-grandparents.

Adoption includes legal adoption and de facto adoption.

The following factors are considered when determining whether a de facto adoption exists:

  • Actual control and custody
  • An exercise of parental care and responsibility on a continuing basis
  • Dependency
  • Proximity to each other
Example
Description

Jack and Jill are related to 2 of their children by blood (Mark and Tom) and one of their children by adoption (Eleanor).

Result

Eleanor is related to her siblings (Mark and Tom) and her four grandparents (the parents of Jack and Jill).

  • If the persons are related, they are not dealing at arm's length with each other.

    Related employers/employees

    In an employer and employee situation, related individuals may be deemed to deal at arm's length if it is reasonable to conclude that the employer and worker would have entered a similar contract if they had been dealing at arm's length giving consideration to:

    • Remuneration
    • Work hours
    • The terms and conditions of employment
    • The nature and importance of the work performed

  • If the persons are not related, they may deal at arm's length with each other.
