Incorporated employee and payer relationship: Understand the tax obligations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language, including additional examples and legislative references.

If you are not sure if the worker is an incorporated employee, continue to: Determine if the worker is an incorporated employee

Understanding the tax obligations based on the individual:

  • Payer - Payments made to a PSB
  • PSB
Payer

Understanding the payer's tax obligations:

  • Payroll obligations
  • Income tax obligations
  • GST/HST obligations
  • Other tax obligations

Payroll obligations

Generally, any person must open a payroll account, calculate payroll deductions and contributions, remit (pay) deductions and contributions, and file information returns if they make payments to a self-employed worker.

Depending on who the payment is made to, the payer may have withhold and remit the income tax, EI, CPP or GST/HST on payments made related to the provision of services to a PSB.

Generally, if the payer makes a payment for a service outside of an employment relationship or tenure of office, the payer must fill out a T4A slip. This includes payments of fees for services between businesses, including sole proprietors and corporations. Depending on the situation, the payer may need to fill out a T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 instead.

Learn more: Payments of fees for services

Income tax obligations

The payer must report and pay tax on all income earned from taxable activities. The income is the net earnings from employment, property, investments and benefits. To report and pay tax, the payer must calculate the business income and expenses using one of the following form when filing their income tax return:

  • T1 return

    Form T2125, Statement of Business or Professional Activities

    Learn more: Lines 13499 to 14300 – Self-employment income

  • T2 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: Completing your corporation income tax return

  • T5013 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: T5013 Partnership Information Return filing requirements

GST/HST obligations

Generally, any person must open a GST/HST account, collect, file and remit (pay) the GST/HST if they provide goods or services and earn more than $30,000 over 4 calendar quarters.

Learn more: Collect, file, and remit (pay) the GST/HST

Other tax obligations

Depending on the type of industry, the payer may have other tax obligations, for example:

  • Import-export account (RM), for businesses that imports goods or sells goods or services abroad
  • Information returns account (RZ), for businesses that files information returns, such as tax-free savings account (TFSA), T5, T5013, and more
  • Registered charity account (RR), for businesses that are a charity organization
  • Underused housing tax account (RU), for corporations that files a Underused Housing Tax return for each of their properties in Canada.
  • Fuel charge account (CT), for businesses that need to include a fuel charge on products
  • Excise duties account (RD), for businesses that need to charge excise duty on products
  • Excise tax and special levies account (RE), for businesses that need to charge excise tax on products
  • Air travelers security charge account (RG), for air carriers that charge the air travellers security charge
  • Insurance premium tax account (RN), for brokers and agents who place contracts of insurance
  • Luxury tax account (LT), for businesses that need to charge luxury tax on the sale or importation of select luxury items
PSB

Understanding the PSB's tax obligations:

  • Income tax obligations
  • GST/HST obligations
  • Tax obligations for employees of the PSB
  • Other tax obligations

Income tax obligations

PSB who are residents of Canada are required to report their worldwide income from all sources.

If the PSB is non-resident, they are subject to Canadian income tax on most Canadian-sourced income paid or credited to them during the year unless all or part of that income is exempt under a tax treaty.

When filing the T2 return, the PSB uses the amounts from the slip to:

  • Only claim the following eligible expenses on Schedule 125, Income Statement Information:
    • Salary and wages the corporation pays to their employees performing the services of a PSB
    • Any benefit or allowance the corporation provides to their employees performing the services of a PSB
    • Certain expenses of the corporation associated with selling property or negotiating contracts
    • Legal expenses incurred by the corporation in collecting amounts owing
  • Report the PSB income on line 520 of Schedule 7, Aggregate Investment Income and Income Eligible for the Small Business Deduction (SBD) because they are not eligible for the SBD
  • Does not claim the SBD on line 430 of the T2 return because they are not eligible for the SBD
  • Report the PSB income on line 432 of the T2 return because they are not eligible for the general tax reduction
  • Report the PSB income on line 555 of the T2 return because they are subject to an additional 5%

Learn more: Completing your corporation income tax return

Receiving the slip

Generally, the payer must provide the PSB with a T4A slip which includes any fees or other amounts paid for services. Depending on the situation, the payer may provide the PSB with a T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 instead.

GST/HST obligations

Generally, any person must open a GST/HST account, collect, file and remit (pay) the GST/HST if they provide goods or services and earn more than $30,000 over 4 calendar quarters.

Tax obligations for employees of the PSB

The tax obligations for employees of the PSB are the same as regular employers.
