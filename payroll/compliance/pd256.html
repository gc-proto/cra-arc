What is a PD256 letter

The CRA may amend previously filed slips to correct a reporting error or may add slips when a slip was not originally filed. A PD256 letter is sent to you if the CRA needs to provide you with amended or additional T4, T4A, or T4RSP slips.

When is a PD256 issued

Before you receive the PD256 letter, the CRA will either have issued to you a:

  • Pensionable and insurable earnings review (PIER)

    Learn more: Pensionable and insurable earnings review (PIER)

  • Letter of explanation

What to do if you received a PD256 letter

The employer or payer must distribute the additional or amended slips to the workers concerned. Learn more about the obligation to distribute tax slips: Distribute the slips.

The employer should also keep a copy of the slip for their records, or update their records with the information from these additional or amended slips.

Learn more: Distribute the slips

Impact on taxes and benefits for workers

As a result of these corrections, changes to the worker’s previously filed personal income tax and benefit returns will be required.

If the worker receives an amended or additional slip from their employer or a payer, the worker must determine if:

  • The CRA reassessed their return
  • They must send a request to reassess their previously filed return

Learn more: Reassessments after payroll compliance reviews

What if you disagree with the slips attached to the PD256 letter

If you disagree with the reporting on the slips issued or amended by CRA attached to the PD256 letter, you may submit amended slips with a letter explaining why you disagree.
