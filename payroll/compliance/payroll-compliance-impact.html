Reassessments after payroll compliance reviews

References to workers

The term “worker” is used on this page to refer to a current employee, a former employee, a retired employee or a self-employed worker.

Following a payroll compliance review, a payroll trust accounts examination or an employer compliance audit, T4 or T4A slips may be corrected to reflect accurate income, withholdings, or other tax-related information of workers. This is done to make sure that employers or payers are fully compliant with their tax obligations for the period under review.

As a result of these corrections, changes to the worker’s previously filed personal income tax and benefit returns will be required.

The default method to receive correspondence is now online. The CRA recommends signing into My Account, access Notification Preferences in your Profile to ensure your email address is up to date. This allows the CRA to notify you when important changes are made on your account and when you have mail to view in My Account.

How the process works

  1. CRA provides additional or amended T4 or T4A slips to the employer or payer

    After a payroll compliance review, a payroll trust accounts examination or an employer compliance audit, the CRA will provide additional or amended T4 or T4A slips if the review found the employer or payer failed to properly:

    • Report income or payroll deductions

      Learn more: Fill out the slips and summaries

    • Report taxable benefits

      Learn more: Determine if a benefit is taxable

    • Deduct CPP contributions

      Learn more: About the deduction of Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contribution

    • Deduct EI premiums

      Learn more: About the deduction of Employment Insurance (EI) premiums

    • Determine the employment status of their workers

      Learn more: Determine the relationship with the employer or the payer

    Common corrections to T4 or T4A slips

    The CRA may amend the previously filed T4 or T4A slips to correct the reporting by the employer or payer by increasing or reducing the amount reported on the slip.

    The CRA may also add T4 or T4A slips when a slip was not originally filed or when the employment status has been determined to be different from how it was reported on the original slip.

    Changes are most commonly made to the following boxes or codes:

    • T4 slips

      • Total employment income (box 14)
      • CPP contributions (box 16)
      • CPP2 contributions (box 16A)
      • EI premiums (box 18)
      • Income tax deducted (box 22)
      • Insurable and pensionable earnings (box 24 and box 26)
      • Taxable benefits (codes 30, 32, 33, 34, 36, 38, 40 or 90)

    • T4A slips

      • Self-employed commissions (box 020)
      • Income tax deducted (box 022)
      • Other income (code 028)
      • Fees for services (code 048)

  2. Employer or payer distributes additional or amended T4 or T4A slips to the workers

    The employer or payer must distribute the additional or amended T4 or T4A slips to the workers concerned.

    Learn more about the obligation to distribute tax slips: Distribute the slips

    Impact on taxes and benefits for workers

    Changes to T4 or T4A slips can have a direct impact on a worker’s previously filed returns.

    Examples of impacts on taxes and benefits

    The following are examples of changes to the T4 or T4A slips that can have a direct impact on previously filed personal income tax and benefit returns:

    Increased or decreased income

    • If the changes show that the worker earned more than previously reported, their tax liability will increase, meaning they could owe more when their return is reassessed.
    • If the changes show that the worker earned less than previously reported, their tax liability will decrease, meaning they could owe less in taxes or be entitled to a larger tax refund when their return is reassessed.

    Adjustments to payroll deductions

    • If the correct amount of CPP contributions or EI premiums withheld were not originally reported, the changes will make sure the worker receives credit for the payroll deductions actually withheld.
    • If the correct amount of CPP contributions or EI premiums were not originally withheld, the changes will make sure the correct payroll deductions are now reported, increasing the payroll deductions reported on the personal return.
    • If too much was initially withheld, the correction to the pensionable and insurable earnings could mean a lower balance owing or a larger refund as a result of a CPP or EI overpayment, depending on the circumstances.

    Change in employment status

    In addition to income and source deduction reporting corrections, changes to the T4 or T4A slip may have been made to correct how the worker’s employment status was reported:

    • If the review determined that the worker was incorrectly treated as a self-employed worker instead of as an employee (or the reverse), this will result in their T4A slip being replaced with a T4 (or the reverse).

      It will also impact whether CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax deductions should have been withheld from their earnings.

    • If the worker’s employment status was changed, this could change their tax obligations, eligibility for benefits, and even the source of income tax deductions they are entitled to claim. The worker may have to send a request to reassess their return to correct how they were claiming deductions on their return (either as business expenses or employment-related expenses).

    Tax credits and benefits

    The amounts reported on the T4 or T4A slip affects eligibility for certain tax credits and government benefits (for example: Canada Child Benefit, GST/HST credits).

    If the worker receives any government benefits, they should verify whether the revised income will impact these benefits. In some cases, it could lead to a reduction or an increase in benefits.

    What happens if the employer or payer does not distribute the T4 or T4A slips

    If the employer or payer does not distribute the T4 or T4A slips to the worker, the worker can get a copy from the CRA.

    Learn more on how the worker can get a copy of the slip if they did not receive it: Get a copy of your slips

  3. CRA makes a reassessment or worker sends a request to reassess their return

    Depending on the payroll compliance program, the CRA will make a reassessment to the worker’s previously filed personal income tax and benefit return or the worker will need to send a request to reassess their return.

    Even if the CRA reassesses the return, the worker may need to send a request to reassess their return to correct amounts entered on any other line of the return impacted by this reassessment. For example, to make corrections to amounts claimed as employment expenses or business expenses on their previously filed return.

    Common lines of the return impacted by a reassessment

    When a reassessment is done, the following are the most common lines of the personal income tax and benefit return that may be impacted:

    Line 10100 – Employment income
    This line reports the total employment income from the T4 slips received by the worker. If there are adjustments to box 14 of the T4 slip, the income here will be updated accordingly.
    Line 10400 – Other employment income
    This line reports amounts usually included on a T4A or T4PS slip. If there are other sources of employment income that should have been included on a T4 slip and were originally reported here by the worker, they will be removed from this line.
    Line 15000 – Total income
    This is the total income you report on the tax return. Any adjustments in the employment income or other sources of income will flow into this line.
    Line 22215 - Deductions for enhanced CPP contributions on employment income
    If there were errors in calculating enhanced CPP contributions, this line will reflect the correct deduction.
    Line 23600 – Net income
    Adjustments to the total income (from Line 15000) or deductions will affect this line, which shows the net income after deductions.
    Line 30000 – Basic personal amount (federal tax credit)
    Changes to net income may adjust the tax credit for the Basic Personal Amount.
    Line 30800 – CPP contributions
    If there were errors in calculating the CPP contributions (for example: due to an incorrect determination of employment status or incorrect income reporting), this line will reflect the corrected amount.
    Line 31200 – EI premiums
    If there were errors in calculating the EI premiums (for example: due to an incorrect determination of employment status or incorrect income reporting), this line will reflect the corrected amount.
    Line 42000 – Net federal tax
    If the taxable income changes, this will impact the amount of federal tax you owe. Any reassessment will adjust this line accordingly.
    Line 42800 – Provincial or territorial tax
    Depending on the province or territory, the provincial tax could also be affected by changes to the income or deductions.
    Lines 48400 and 48500 – Refund or balance owing
    After the reassessment, this line will reflect either a refund or balance owing based on the changes to the personal income tax and benefit return and taxes paid.

    If the worker receives an amended or additional slip from their employer or a payer, the worker must determine if:

    • The CRA reassessed their return
    • They must send a request to reassess their previously filed return

    Confirm if the CRA reassessed the worker’s return

    To confirm if the CRA has reassessed the previously filed return, the worker can view the status of a notice of reassessment (NOR) using the CRA online services when they click “Tax returns” in the Navigation menu.

    The “Notices of assessment and reassessment” table provides general information about the tax returns. The table provides detailed information when they click on the linked options under the columns “Status of return” and “Notice of Assessment or Reassessment”.

    If the “Status of return” column shows reassessed, the worker should review this notice to make sure the revised amounts match the changes made on the slips received from the employer or payer.

    The CRA recommends waiting 60 days after the employer or payer has distributed the additional or amended T4 or T4A slips provided by the CRA to the worker before sending a request to reassess a return. This is to make sure that the CRA does not already have a reassessment in progress.
