Assisted payroll compliance reviews - Trust accounts examinations

The assisted payroll compliance review offers you support as a business to help you (as an employer) understand your payroll deducting, remitting, and reporting obligations and how to meet them.

The examination process focuses on specific payroll issues, providing you with an opportunity to voluntarily correct errors before facing penalty and interest charges.

This opportunity does not apply to your GST/HST program account if non-compliance issues are found during an examination. For example, if you are a GST/HST registrant and you have outstanding GST/HST returns.

What are the benefits of the payroll assisted compliance examination

The goal is to assist and guide you in complying with payroll tax laws.

The examination focuses on key payroll aspects such as correct payroll deductions (income tax, CPP, EI), accurate reporting on employee benefits, and proper T4 slip issuance. It ensures you are correctly remitting payroll deductions.

The CRA officer works with you to identify payroll discrepancies and provides feedback to help correct any issues. It is a cooperative process aimed at compliance improvement.

  • This is your opportunity for corrections, it may help you avoid costly penalties by catching issues early. For example, if errors are identified, you are given the chance to correct them within a reasonable time, without facing significant penalties or interest charges. This could include adjusting payroll records or filing corrected slips.
  • The CRA officer will provide educational support and online resources for best practices in payroll administration. They may provide guidance to help you become compliant and maintain compliance in the future.

  1. Your account is being reviewed by a Trust Accounts Examination Officer

    Timeline: Day 1

    The CRA will contact you (or your authorized representative) to inform you that your payroll program account  is being reviewed by a Trust Accounts Examination Officer.

    How the CRA may review your account

    The CRA may review your account based on various factors, including but not limited to the following:

    • You have not previously been reviewed for a payroll examination
    • Your payroll account was opened for less than 5 years
    • You do not have related corporations or entities
    • You have less than $ 1 million in salaries

  4. Results of the examination

    Target timeline: Generally, your results are given the same day if in-person or within 15 days if by mail

    1. Common changes required for your T4 or T4A returns after an examination

      The following is a list of the most common changes to your T4 or T4A returns (but not limited to) the CRA officer can identify that need to be made once they finished reviewing your books and records:

      • Amend or add T4 slips

        The CRA officer may identify that you need to amend your T4 slips to correct the reporting by increasing or reducing the amount reported on the slip.

        They may also identify that T4 slips need to be added where a slip was not originally filed or when the employment status has been determined to be different from how it was reported on the original slip.

        Changes are most commonly made to the following boxes or codes:

        • Total employment income (box 14)
        • Income tax deducted (box 22)
        • CPP contributions (box 16)
        • EI premiums (box 18)
        • Insurable and pensionable earnings (box 24 and box 26)
        • Taxable benefits (codes 30, 32, 33, 34, 36, 38, 40 or 90 )

      • Amend or add T4A slips

        The CRA officer may identify that you need to amend your T4A slips to correct the reporting by increasing or reducing the amount reported on the slip.

        They may also identify that you need to add T4A slips where a slip was not originally filed or when the employment status has been determined to be different from how it was reported on the original slip.

        Changes are most commonly made to the following boxes or codes:

        • Self-employed commissions (box 020)
        • Income tax deducted (box 022)
        • Other income (code 028)

    2. If the CRA officer has identified payroll discrepancies, they will work with you (or your authorized representative) to help correct them. This is your opportunity for corrections. It may help you avoid penalty and interest charges.

      When to make corrections

      Once the review of the books and records has been completed, the CRA officer will discuss the results with you. They will provide 30 days, from the day of this discussion, for you to make all the required corrections.

      What if you need more time

      If you cannot make the corrections requested by the due date, call the CRA officer within 30 days of the due date to let them know you need more time. The CRA officer may provide an extension to self-correct any discrepancies found if the extension is requested because you are trying to correct all issues identified by the CRA officer.

      To determine the due date, use the: Date calculator

      If you either:

      Make all corrections

      You may be eligible for proactive penalty and interest relief if:

      • You provided all the required books and records
      • You make all corrections requested by the due date
      • You continue to meet all your tax obligations
      Do not make all corrections

      The CRA officer will make the adjustments required and will assess your tax payable based on the information they have if you do not make all corrections.

      Penalties will apply to amounts the CRA assesses. Interest accumulates on overdue balances.
