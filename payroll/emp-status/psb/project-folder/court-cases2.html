  • Title: Mazraani v. Minister of National Revenue

    • Result: Employee
    • Court: Tax Court of Canada
    • Industry: Finance and insurance
    Summary
    • Act: Employment Insurance Act
    • Reference: 2016 TCC 65
    • File number(s): 2013-3484(EI)

    Worker was an employee and worker was not operating a personal service business.

    Summary of court case

    Intent (factor 1)
    Self-employed worker
    Control (factor 2)
    • Worker does not have freedom to determine how work should be done
    • Worker does not have the right to refuse work from the payer
    • Payer sets hours and schedule for the worker
    • Worker is monitored or supervised by the payer
    • Worker works at the payer's workplace
    • Payer determines what work is to be done
    • Payer imposes a dress code or requires a uniform
    • Payer provides the worker with training
    Ownership of tools (factor 3)
    Payer owns the most important tools or equipment
    Subcontracting of work (factor 4)
    Worker cannot delegate or subcontract work
    Risk (factor 5)
    Worker is not personally at risk of loss
    Reimbursement (factor 5)
    Worker is not reimbursed for work-related travel expenses
