Payments to members of a religious order who have taken a vow of perpetual poverty

This new content has been developed for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may have employees who are members of a religious order who have taken a vow of poverty. Depending on the situation, if your employee meets all conditions, the income earned (including taxable benefits) by your employee will not be subject to CPP, EI or income tax withholding.

You have a legal obligation to report all income and deductions on a T4 slip regardless of whether the income is taxable or exempt.

Steps

  1. Determine if your employee is considered a member of a religious order

    • If your employee is not considered a member of a religious order, do not continue to the next step.

      Refer to: Determine the tax treatment of payments other than regular employment income.

    • If your employee is considered a member of a religious order, continue to: Step 2 - Determine if your employee has taken a vow of poverty.

  2. Determine if your employee has taken a vow of poverty

    You need to determine if your employee who is a member of a religious order has taken a vow of poverty.

    A vow of poverty is a pledge or promise of a member of a religious order to renounce temporal goods to follow God.

    The member of the religious order commits to giving their entire earned income over to the religious order of which they are a member.

    The vow is referred to as:

    • Under the Income Tax Act and the Canada Pension Plan, a vow of perpetual poverty
    • Under the Employment Insurance Act, a vow of poverty

    Both a permanent and a temporary vow of poverty are considered a vow of poverty.

    • If your employee has not taken a vow of poverty (for example: your employee does not give their entire earned income over to the religious order), do not continue to next step.

      Learn more about relevant taxable benefits: Housing and utilities - Provided to a member of the clergy, a regular minister or a member of a religious order or Travel expenses

    • If your employee has taken a vow of poverty, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if you need to withhold source deductions.

  3. Determine if you need to withhold source deductions

    If your employee is considered a member of a religious order (step 1) who has taken a vow of poverty (step 2), your employee must provide you with the required records before you can stop the withholding source deductions.

    • Income paid directly to the religious order

      If your employee provided you with a letter or other documentation from the religious order confirming that they are both a member of the order and that they have taken a vow of poverty, do not withhold the following deductions:

      • Income tax (do not withhold)
      • CPP (do not withhold)
      • EI (do not withhold)
    • Income paid to your employee - Letter of authority from the CRA provided

      If your employee provided you with a letter of authority from the CRA, do not withhold the following deductions:

      • Income tax (do not withhold)
      • CPP (do not withhold)
      • EI (do not withhold)
      How your employee can request a letter of authority from the CRA

      Your employee can request a letter of authority by completing Form T1213 Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source, and sending to the CRA at the address provided on this form.

      If they have not previously claimed the deduction on line 25600 of their personal income tax return, they may be asked to provide a letter from the religious order confirming that they are a member and have taken a vow of poverty.

    • Income paid to your employee - Letter of authority from the CRA not provided

      If your employee did not provided you with a letter of authority from the CRA, withhold the following deductions:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI

    If your employee was under a vow of poverty for only part of the year, this only applies to the part of the year when they were under the vow. For the period where they were not under the vow, withhold income tax, CPP and EI.

    Learn more: How to calculate - Calculate payroll deductions and contributions

  4. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    If your employee is considered a member of a religious order who has taken a vow of poverty (step 1), you must report the following on a T4 slip:

    • Income paid directly to the religious order or income paid to your employee who provided you with a letter of authority from the CRA

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 28 - Exempt, both CPP/QPP and EI must be checked
      • Any applicable codes for taxable benefits

    • Income paid to your employee who did not provide you with a letter of authority from the CRA

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Any applicable codes for taxable benefits

    If your employee was under a vow of poverty for only part of the year, report the income using the above only for the part of the year when they were under the vow. For the part of the year when they were not under the vow, report the income as you would for your other employees.

    Learn how to report the payment: T4 slip - Information for employers.
