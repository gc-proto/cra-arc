Payments and earnings related to members of a religious order who has taken a vow of perpetual poverty

This new content has been developed for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of advance payments.

You may have employees who are members of a religious order who have taken a vow of perpetual poverty. Depending on the situation, if your employee meets all conditions, the income earned (including taxable benefits) of your employee will not be subject to CPP, EI or income tax withholding.

You have a legal obligation to report all income on a slip regardless the income is taxable or exempt.

Steps

  1. Determine if your employee is considered a member of the clergy

    • If your employee does not meet both tests, your employee is not eligible for the clergy residence deduction. Do not continue to the next step.

      Refer to the information for most employees: Determine the tax treatment of payments other than regular employment income.

    • If your employee meets both tests, your employee is considered a member of the clergy or a religious order.

      Continue to: Step 2 - Determine if your employee has taken a vow of perpetual poverty .

  2. Determine if your employee has taken a vow of perpetual poverty

    You need to determine if your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order decided to take a vow of perpetual poverty. This is a pledge or promise of a person to give their income to the religious order of which they are a member.

    The vow may be referred to as one of the following:

    • Vow of perpetual poverty, this means it permanent (for life or for an indefinite period of time)
    • Vow of poverty, this means it is temporary (for a short period of time)

    Learn more: IT86R- Vow of perpetual poverty

    • If your employee has not take a vow of property, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more about taxable benefits: Housing and utilities - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order or Travel expenses - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order

    • If your employee has taken a vow of property, continue to: Step 3 - Determine which deductions you need to withhold.

  3. Determine which deductions you need to withhold

    If your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order decided to take a vow of perpetual poverty (step 2), your employee must provide you with documents before you can determine which deductions you need to withhold.

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 2, you must withhold the following deductions depending on the documents you received:

    • You paid the income directly

      If you paid the income directly to your employee, do not include the housing or utilities that is equal to the clergy residence deduction in your employee's income in one of the following situation:

      • Your employee completes parts A and C of Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, and requests that you complete part B.
      • Your employee tells you in writing that they intend to claim the deduction, including the amount of the deduction that will be claimed.

      You can reduce the income used to calculate the CPP contributions and income tax by the amount indicated on line 11 of the Form or the amount indicated on the letter.

    • You did not pay the income directly

      If your employee owns or rents their residence and they pays for utilities or you provide an allowance, do not include the housing or utilities that is equal to the clergy residence deduction in your employee's income in one of the following situation:

      • Your employee completes parts A and C of Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, and requests that you complete part B.
      • Your employee completes Form T1213 Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source
      • Your employee sends Forms T1213 and T1223 to the CRA at the address provided on Form T1213
      • Your employee provides you with the CRA letter of authority they received.

      You can only reduce the income used to calculate the CPP contributions and income tax by the amount indicated on the letter of authority.

    • If your employee provides you with the documents, you can apply the clergy residence reduction. The benefit or allowance is excluded from your employee’s income. You must report the excluded benefit or allowance on a slip.

      Continue to: Step 7 - Report the benefit or allowance on a slip.

    • If your employee does not provide the documents, do not apply the reduction. The benefit or allowance must be included in your employee’s income. The benefit or allowance is taxable.

      If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA, only the value over that is more than a reasonable amount for housing or utilities must be included in the employee’s income.

      Continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

  4. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    If your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order decided to take a vow of perpetual poverty, you must report the following on a T4 slip:

    • Box 14 - Employment income
    • Box 28 - Exempt, both CPP/QPP and EI must be checked
