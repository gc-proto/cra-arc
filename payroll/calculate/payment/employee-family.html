Employee who is hired as a family member or a related person

Content to be covered: Hiring a family member or a related person

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You may have employees who are family members or are considered related to you. Depending on the facts of the working relationship, they may not be considered to be in insurable employment and you may not be required to withhold EI premiums from their employment income.

Steps

  1. Determine if your employee is considered related to you

    You (as the employer) may have employees that are considered related to you if one of the following situation apply:

    • Confirm if individuals are connected

      Blood relationship

      The following individuals are related to each other by blood:

      • A parent and a child (or other descendant, such as a grandchild or a great-grandchild)
      • A brother and a sister

      Blood relatives do not include more distant relatives such as aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews or cousins.

      Marriage

      Individuals are related to each other if they are legally married to each other. They are also related to anyone connected by blood relationship (parents and siblings) to their spouse.

      If the marriage is dissolved because of divorce or death of a spouse, the individual ceases to be connected by marriage.

      Common-law partnership

      Individuals are related to each other if they are in a common-law partnership with each other. The CRA considers that 2 individuals are in a common-law partnership in one of the following situations:

      • They live together in a conjugal relationship and have done so for a continuous period of at least 12 months
      • They live together in a conjugal relationship and have a child together (this includes through adoption), in which case the 12-month period does not apply

      They are also related to anyone connected by blood relationship (parents and siblings) to their common-law partner.

      If there is a breakdown in the relationship, they are considered to continue to live in a common-law partnership until they have lived separate and apart for 90 consecutive days.

      Adoption

      Individuals are related to each other through adoption if one individual is the adopted child of the other.

      In addition to their parents, the child is also related to their siblings, grandparents and great-grandparents.

      Adoption includes legal adoption and de facto adoption.

      The following factors are considered when determining whether a de facto adoption exists:

      • Actual control and custody
      • An exercise of parental care and responsibility on a continuing basis
      • Dependency
      • Proximity to each other

    • Confirm if persons are considered related to a corporation

      A person (or related group of person) and a corporation

      The following person who controls  or related group of person who control  (de jure control) a corporation are related to each other if one of the following applies:

      • A person controls a corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Amy owns 60% of the voting shares in AM Corp
        • Mike owns the remaining 40%
        • Since Amy owns more than 50% of the voting shares, Amy controls AM Corp
        Result

        Amy is related to AM Corp and therefore does not deal at arm's length with AM Corp. If Mike is not related to Amy or otherwise related to AM Corp by other means, Mike may deal at arm's length with AM Corp.

      • A person is a member of a related group that controls the corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Warren, Fred and Mike each own 1/3 of the voting shares of WFM Corp
        • Warren and Fred are common-law partners
        • Since Warren and Fred are related to each other and together they own 66.7% (more than 50%) of the voting shares, they are a related group that controls WFM Corp
        Result

        Warren and Fred do not deal at arm's length with WFM Corp. Assuming Mike is not related to either Warren or Fred and does not control WFM Corp by other means, Mike deals at arm's length with WFM Corp.

      • A person is related to a related group of persons who controls the corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Warren, Fred and Mike each own 1/3 of the voting shares of WFM Corp
        • Warren and Fred are common-law partners
        • Since Warren and Fred are related to each other and together they own more than 66.7% of the voting shares, they are a related group that controls WFM Corp
        • Fred's brother Neil is a supplier for WFM Corp
        • Neil is related to Warren and Fred and therefore to WFM Crop
        Result

        Neil does not deal at arm's length with WFM Corp.

      • A person is related to another person who controls the corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Amy owns 60% of the voting shares in AM Corp
        • Mike owns the remaining 40%
        • Since Amy owns more than 50% of the voting shares, Amy controls AM Corp
        • Amy is married to Alan
        Result

        As a result Alan is related to Amy and AM Corp. If Alan (or a related party) does business with AM Corp, he does not deal at arm's length with AM Corp.

      Two corporations

      The following person who controls  or related group of person who control  (de jure control) a corporation are related to each other if one of the following applies:

      • Both corporations are controlled by the same person or group of persons

        Example
        Description
        • Bob owns 100% of the shares of A Corp
        • Bob owns 51% of the voting shares of B Corp
        • Bob controls both corporations since he owns more than 50% of the voting shares in each
        Result

        A Corp and B Corp are related to each other.

      • The person that controls one corporation is related to the person that controls the other corporation

        Example
        Description

        The voting shares of A Corp and B Corp are owned as follows:

        A Corp % B Corp %
        Bob 60% Phillip 51%
        Henry 20% Rachel 39%
        Sadie 20% Vicky 10%
        • Phillip is married to Bob's sister
        • As brothers-in-law, Bob and Phillip are related to each other and Rachel is related to each of them
        • Bob controls A Corp since he owns more than 50% of the voting shares
        • Phillip controls B Corp since he owns more than 50% of the voting shares
        • The person (Bob) who controls A Corp is related to the person (Phillip) who controls B Corp
        Result

        A Corp is related to B Corp

      • Each member of an unrelated group that controls one corporation is related to at least one member of an unrelated group that controls the other corporation

        Example
        Description

        The voting shares of A Corp and B Corp are owned as follows:

        A Corp % B Corp %
        Bob 33.3% Sadie 33.3%
        Henry 33.3% Rachel 33.3%
        Phillip 33.3% Vicky 33.3%
        • Bob, Henry and Phillip are not related to each other which means A Corp is not controlled by a related group
        • Sadie, Rachel and Vicky are not related to each other which means B Corp is not controlled by a related group
        • Bob is married to Sadie
        • Henry is married to Rachel
        • Phillip is married to Vicky
        Result

        Each member of A Corp is related to at least one member of B Corp. This means that A Corp is related to B Corp.

      • The person who controls one corporation is related to one member of a related group that controls the other corporation

        Example
        Description

        The voting shares of A Corp and B Corp are owned as follows:

        A Corp % B Corp %
        Bob 60% Phillip 33.3%
        Henry 20% Rachel 33.3%
        Sadie 20% Vicky 33.3%
        • Bob and Rachel are related to each other
        • Rachel and Phillip are married
        • Bob controls A Corp since he owns more than 50% of the voting shares
        • Since Phillip and Rachel are related to each other and together they own more than 50% of the voting shares of B Corp, they are a related group that controls B Corp
        • The person (Bob) who controls A Corp is related to a person (Rachel) who is part of a related group that controls B Corp
        Result

        A Corp is related to B Corp

      • Any member of a related group that controls one corporation is related to each member of an unrelated group that controls the other corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Rachel has 2 sons, Phillip and Henry
        • Phillip has a daughter, Sadie
        • Henry has a daughter, Vicky
        • Rachel, Phillip and Henry each own 1/3 of the voting shares in A Corp
        • Vicky and Sadie each own 50% of the shares of B Corp
        Relationship
        • Rachel, Phillip and Henry are all related to each other since Rachel is their mother
        • Vicky and Sadie are not related to each other because they are cousins
        • Rachel is related to Vicky and Sadie since she is their grandmother
        Result

        A Corp and B Corp are related to each other, since:

        • A Corp is controlled by a related group
        • A member (Rachel) of the related group that controls A Corp is related to each member (Vicky and Sadie) of an unrelated group that controls B Corp

      • The person who controls one corporation is related to every member of the unrelated group that controls the other corporation

        Example
        Description

        The voting shares of A Corp and B Corp are owned as follows:

        A Corp % B Corp %
        Bob 60% Phillip 33.3%
        Henry 20% Rachel 33.3%
        Sadie 20% Vicky 33.3%
        • Bob is married to Rachel
        • Bob and Phillip are brothers
        • Phillip, Rachel and Vicky are not related to each other
        • Bob controls A Corp since he owns more than 50% of the voting shares
        • B Corp is not controlled by a related group
        • Since Bob is related to both Phillip and Rachel and together they own more than 50% of the voting shares of B Corp, they are an unrelated group that controls B Corp
        • The person (Bob) who controls A Corp is related to each person (Phillip and Rachel) who is part of an unrelated group that controls B Corp
        Result

        A Corp is related to B Corp

      • Each corporation is related to the same third corporation

        Example
        Description
        • Cecilia and Rita are sisters
        • Cecilia is the sole shareholder of Ceci Co
        • Rita is the sole shareholder of Rita Co
        • Cecilia has a daughter, Tatiana
        • Tatiana is the sole shareholder of Tatiana Co
        • Ceci Co is related to Rita Co (sisters)
        • Ceci Co is also related to Tatiana Co (mother and daughter)
        Result

        If not for this provision, Rita Co and Tatiana Co would not be related, since an aunt is not related to her neice. However, since Ceci Co is related to both Rita Co and Tatiana Co, Rita Co and Tatiana Co are deemed to be related to each other.

    • Confirm if a person and a partnership are considered related

      A person and a partnership

      The following partner who control a partnership  are related to each other if one of the following applies:

      • A partner controls a partnership

        Example
        Description

        Dylan, Leonard, Donovan and Sarah form a partnership. They are not related to each other and each has an equal 25% share in the profits.

        Under the partnership agreement, Sarah manages the business and has full decision making authority. Since Sarah controls the partnership, she does not deal at arm's length with the partnership.

        Result
        • Income Tax Act

          For income tax purposes, Dylan, Leonard and Donovan deal at arm's length with the partnership based on control, though they may not deal at arm's length based on other factors.

        • Excise Tax Act

          For GST/HST purposes, all partners are related to the partnership. They do not deal at arm's length with the partnership.

      • A partnership controls a corporation

        Example
        Description

        Dylan and Leonard form DyLo partnership. Dylan, Leonard and DyLo partnership are shareholders in a corporation, DL Corp. Dylan and Leonard are not related to each other.

        The voting shares in DL Corp are distributed as follows:

        • DyLo partnership - 60%
        • Dylan - 20%
        • Leonard - 20%
        Result
        • Income Tax Act
          • DyLo Partnership does not deal at arm's length with DL Corp.
          • Based on control, Dylan and Leonard deal at arm's length with DL Corp, though they may not deal at arm's length based on other factors.
        • Excise Tax Act
          • DyLo Partnership is related to DL Corp. DyLo Partnership does not deal at arm's length with DL Corp.
          • Dylan and Leonard are:
            • Related to DyLo Partnership
            • Not part of a related group that controls the corporation
            • Not related to DL Corp

      • A related group of partners controls a partnership

        Example
        Description

        Dylan, Leonard, Donovan and Sarah form a partnership. Leonard, Donovan and Sarah are siblings (related). Dylan is their cousin which they means they are not related. Each has an equal 25% share in the profits.

        Result

        Since they are a related group that controls the partnership, Leonard, Donovan and Sarah do not deal at arm's length with the partnership.

        • Income Tax Act

          For income tax purposes, Dylan deals at arm's length with the partnership based on control, though he may not deal at arm's length based on other factors.

        • Excise Tax Act

          For GST/HST purpsoes, all partners are related to the partnership. They do not deal at arm's length with the partnership.

    • Confirm if a person and a trust are considered related

      A person and a trust

      A trust is a person. The rules for determining if a trust is related to another person are the same as for any other persons.

      Types of trust and explanation
      Bare trust
      A bare trust exists where a trustee is merely vested with the legal title to property and has no other duty to perform or responsibilities to carry out as trustee, in relation to the property vested in the trust.
      Explanation

      A trust that is considered to be a bare trust acts as agent for the beneficiary. The CRA considers the beneficiary is the person dealing with other parties with respect to the trust property.

      Explanation

      The sole duty of a bare trustee is to convey legal title to the trust property on demand, on the instructions of the beneficial owner (beneficiary). The bare trustee does not have any independent power, discretion or responsibility pertaining to the trust property. The beneficiary retains the right to control and direct the trustee in all matters relating to the trust property.

      Example
      Description

      123 Property Trust holds property for A Corp (the beneficiary). The beneficiary directs the trustee in all matters relating to the property. The trustee does not have any independent power, discretion or responsibility pertaining to the property.

      It is likely that 123 Property Trust is a bare trust. The benficiary instructs 123 Property Trust to transfer the property to B Corp. A Corp and B Corp are related to each other.

      Because A Corp and B Corp are related to each other, the transfer of the property is a non-arms-length transaction.

      Had the role of the trustee been different, for example, if the trustee had discretionary power or responsibility to manage the property, the trust would not be a bare trust. In that case, if 123 Property Trust was not related to B Corp, the transaction would be at arm's length.

      Testamentary trust
      A testamentary trust is a trust or estate that is generally created on and as result of the death of the person.
      Personal trust

      A personal trust (for 2016 and subsequent tax years) means:

      • A trust that arose on and as a consequence of the individual's death if it meets the conditions to be a graduated rate estate
      • A trust in which no beneficial interest was acquired for consideration payable to the trust or to any person or partnership that has made a contribution to the trust

      Explanation

      A personal trust is a specified personal trust if it is not described in paragraphs 108(1)(a) to (e.1).

      A beneficiary does not deal at arm's length with the trust from which it acquired property in accordance with the terms of the will of the deceased.

      Additionally, 2 or more specified personal trusts are deemed not to deal at arm's length in either of the following situations:

      • The same person is a beneficiary of each trust
      • A person who is a beneficiary of one trust does not deal at arm's length with a person who is a beneficiary of the other trust(s)
      Inter-vivos trust

      An inter-vivos trust is any trust, other than a testamentary trust.

      List of inter-vivos trusts

      List of inter-vivos trusts:

      • An amateur athlete trust
      • An employee life and health trust
      • An employee trust
      • A prescribed master trust
      • A trust governed by a deferred profit sharing plan
      • An employee benefit plan
      • An employees profit sharing plan
      • A foreign retirement arrangement
      • A pooled registered pension plan
      • A registered disability savings plan
      • A registered education savings plan
      • A registered pension plan
      • A registered retirement income fund
      • A registered retirement savings plan
      • A registered supplementary unemployment benefit plan
      • A tax-free savings account
      • A related segregated fund trust
      • A retirement compensation arrangement trust
      • A trust whose direct beneficiaries are one of the aforementioned trusts
      • A health and welfare trust
      • A trust governed by an eligible funeral arrangement
      • A cemetery care trust
      • A trust deemed in respect of a communal religious organization

      You must deduct CPP, EI and income tax as you would for your other employees.

  2. Determine if you are considered to be dealing at arm’s length

    If your employee is related to you (individually or as a corporation) as their employer, they may not be in insurable employment. However, the related employee can be in insurable employment if you would have negotiated a similar contract with a person with whom you deal at arm’s length .

    How to determine if you would have negotiated a similar contract

    All the facts about your working relationship must be reviewed to see if someone else would have been hired under a similar contract of employment.

    Generally, the CRA reviews the following 4 factors when looking at the working relationship:

    • Remuneration paid
    • Terms and conditions of employment, such as the hours of work
    • Duration of work performed
    • Nature and importance of the work performed by your employee

    Learn more: Not dealing at arm's length for purposes of the Employment Insurance Act (EIA)

Person or related group of person controls a partnership

  • Income Tax Act

    For income tax purposes, partners are not normally related to a partnership and partnerships are not related to other persons.

    Control determines if partners or partnerships deal at arm's length with each other or another person.

    A partner can control a partnership if one of the following apply:

    • Ownership of a controlling interest, by having more than a 50% share in the profits of the partnership
