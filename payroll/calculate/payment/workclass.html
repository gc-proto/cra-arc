Factors to consider

You need to consider multiple factors to determine if the worker is an employee or self-employed.

  1. Contract arrangement

    1. At the time of contracting, how did the parties intend to characterize the relationship?

      • Employer-employee
      • Indipendent contractor

    2. How long has the worker been working for the hirer (or is expected to work)?

      • Short period (for example, 1 month)
      • Long period

    Tips

    Even if the worker and the hirer intend to characterize the relationship as employer-employee, the worker might be characterized as an independent contractor for tax purposes. Other factors needs to be considered.

    You need to look at the intent of the worker and the hirer when they entered into the working arrangement. Combined with other factors, if there is a contract for services, it may be an indication that the worker is an independent contractor.

    Specific to PSB: Whether or not the service provider must carry out the work personally or if they can hire or subcontract the work (not being able to subcontract relevant work may indicate an employment relationship)

  2. Control

    1. Is the worker monitored or supervised by the hirer?

      • Yes
      • No

    2. Who determines what work is to be done by the worker?

      • Worker
      • Hirer
      • Both

    3. Does the worker have the freedom to turn down work from the hirer?

      • Yes
      • No

    4. Does the worker set his or her own hours and schedule?

      • Yes
      • No

    5. Does the worker have the freedom to determine how the work should be done?

      • Yes
      • No

    6. Is the worker free to work for other people or companies?

      • Yes
      • No

    7. Can the worker delegate or subcontract work without notifying the hirer?

      • Yes
      • No

    8. Does the hirer impose a dress code or require the worker to wear a uniform?

      • Yes
      • No

    Tips

    Control is the ability, authority, or right of a hirer to exercise control over a worker concerning the manner in which the work is done and what work will be done. Combined with other factors, if the control factors suggests that the worker has a high degree of control, it may be an indication that the worker is an independent contractor.

    Specific to PSB: The degree of control exercised by the payer over the service provider’s duties (more control may indicate an employment relationship)

    Specific to PSB: Providing similar services to other clients also indicates self-employment

  3. Ownership

    1. Who owns the most important tools that the worker uses while performing services for the hirer?

      • Worker
      • Hirer
      • Both

    Tips

    The ownership of tools and equipment required to accomplish the work is another important factor that needs to be considered.

    Specific to PSB: Whether the payer uses their own tools to perform the services (providing tools to a service provider may suggest an employment relationship)

  4. Risk

    1. Other than the loss of work, is the worker personally at risk of loss? (e.g., liability, additional cost of re-doing work that fails to meet client satisfaction, etc.)

      • Yes
      • No

    2. Is the worker reimbursed for work-related travel expenses?

      • Yes
      • No

    Tips

    The degree of financial risk taken by the worker is also a factor. Combined with other factors, if the worker has substantial risk of loss, it may be an indication that the worker is an independent contractor.

    Specific to PSB: Whether or not the service provider has an investment in their business, which includes paying for costs before billing for services

  5. Integration

    1. Where does the worker do most of his or her work?

      • Hirer's workplace
      • Mobile locations
      • Worker's home or office

    2. Did the hirer provide the worker with training?

      • Yes
      • No

What if you do not know if the worker is an employee or self-employed

You can ask for a CPP/EI ruling. If you are not sure if the worker is an employee or self-employed, you can request a CPP/EI ruling.
