Payments from a supplementary unemployment benefit plan (SUBP)

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirements.

You may provide a supplementary unemployment benefit plan (SUBP) to your employees that makes payments to them to top up their EI benefits during unemployment during a temporary or indefinite layoff. Depending on whether the plan qualifies as a SUBP under the Income Tax Act (ITA) or is registered with Service Canada, your withholding and reporting obligations will change.

Steps

  1. Determine if the plan qualifies as a SUBP plan

    A SUBP plan is a plan established by you, as an employer, or group of participating employers, to top up their employee’s employment insurance (EI) benefits during a period of unemployment due to a temporary layoff for:

    • Health-related benefits
    • Maternity, parental, compassionate care and family caregiver leave
    • Sickness, accident, or disability
    • Temporary stoppage of work
    • Training

    These plans can be either registered or unregistered with:

    • Service Canada, if the plan meets the requirements of article 37 of the Employment Insurance Regulations (EIR)
    • The Minister of National Revenue (Canada Revenue Agency), if the plan satisfies the conditions for registration under section 145 of the Income Tax Act (ITA)

    Learn more about the conditions to register and the process to do so: Supplemental Unemployment Benefit Program and IC72-5R2 - Registered Supplementary Unemployment Benefit Plans - Canada.ca

  2. Determine if you have to withhold CPP contributions

    If the plan qualifies as a SUBP under the ITA, you do not withhold CPP contributions from payments made under the SUBP.

    If the plan does not qualify as a SUBP under the ITA, but is registered with Service Canada, you must withhold CPP contributions from payments made under the SUBP.

    If the plan does not qualify as a SUBP under the ITA and is not registered with Service Canada, you must withhold CPP contributions from payments made under the SUBP.

  3. Determine if you have to withhold EI premiums

    If the plan qualifies as a SUBP under the ITA, you do not withhold EI premiums from payments made under the SUBP.

    If the plan does not qualify as a SUBP under the ITA, but is registered with Service Canada, you do not withhold EI premiums from payments made under the SUBP if they were paid to an employee because of a temporary stoppage of work, training, sickness, accident, injury, or quarantine.

    If the plan does not qualify as a SUBP under the ITA, but is either registered or not with Service Canada, you do not withhold EI premiums from payments made under the SUBP if the payments were made to an employee for maternity, parental (including adoption), compassionate care or family caregiver leave, if both of the following two conditions are met:

    • The total amount of your payment and your employee's EI weekly benefits combined do not exceed your employee's normal weekly gross salary
    • Your payment does not reduce any other accumulated employment benefits of your employee, such as banked sick leave, vacation leave credits, or retiring allowance

    In any other situation where the plan does not qualify as a SUBP under the ITA, you must withhold EI premiums from payments made under the SUBP.

  4. Determine if you have to withhold income tax deductions

    For all payments under a SUBP, regardless of whether the plan would qualify to be registered under the ITA or if it was registered with Service Canada, you must withhold income tax deductions.

  5. Report the payments on a slip

    You must report payments made under a SUBP on a T4 or a T4A slip depending on whether the plan qualifies as a SUBP under the ITA:

    • Plan qualifies as a SUBP - T4A slip

      You must report the following amounts on a T4A slip:

      • Code 152 - SUBP qualified under the Income Tax Act

      Learn more: T4A slip - Information for employers

    • Plan does not qualify as a SUBP - T4 slip

      You must report the following payments on a T4 slip:

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings

      Learn more: T4 slip - Information for employers
