Payments to workers of placement and employment agencies

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of these payments.

Placement or employment agencies may hire workers either as their own employees, to work for clients of the agency or as self-employed workers.

Depending on the situation, your withholding and reporting responsibilities as the agency or as a client of the agency will be different.

What is a placement or employment agency

For the purposes of CPP and EI, a placement or employment agency includes any person or organization that is engaged in the business of:

  • Placing individuals in employment
  • Placing individuals for performance of services
  • Securing employment for individuals

Steps

  1. Determine the employment status of the employment agency worker

    Workers hired by a placement or employment agency can either be:

    • Employees (even if the employee is working at a client of the agency’s premises)
    • Worker placed by the agency in an employment under the direction and control of a client of the agency (whether the agency pays the worker or not)
    • Self-employed workers

    • If the worker is an employee of the placement agency and is not placed under the direction and control of a client of the agency, do not continue to the next step.

      You, as the agency, must deduct CPP, EI and income tax deductions as you would for your other employees. You also have to report the payment on a T4 slip.

      Learn more: How to calculate

      If you are not sure if the worker is an employee or self-employed for CPP/EI purposes

      You need to consider multiple factors to determine if the worker is an employee or is self-employed. You can use the CRA guidance to help you gather the facts based on your situation.

      Learn more: Employee or Self-employed

    • If the employment agency worker is in any other situation, continue to Step 2 - Determine if you need to report the payment on a T4 slip.

  2. Determine if you need to report the payment on a T4 slip

    Generally, you (as the payer, either the employment agency or the client of the agency) must report payments you make to the worker on a T4 slip.

    Under the CRA administrative policy, you must report the payments on a T4 slip if one of the following apply:

    • You deducted CPP, EI or income tax from the payment.
    • The total of all payments in the calendar year were more than $500.

  3. Determine if the amounts paid are pensionable and insurable

    1. Determine if the amounts paid are pensionable

      The amounts paid to a worker placed by an agency are pensionable if all of the following conditions apply:

      • The employment is performed in Canada
      • The agency’s contract with the client is restricted to providing the worker
      • The agency received a fee, reward or other remuneration for placing that worker in that employment
      • The worker is performing services for the client of the agency
      • The terms and conditions under which the services are performed are similar to a contract of service (for example, an employer-employee relationship)
      • The worker is paid by either the agency or by the client of the agency

      • If the worker’s employment does not meet all conditions above, the amounts paid are not pensionable.

      • If the worker’s employment meets all conditions above, the amounts paid are pensionable.

    2. Determine if the amounts paid are insurable

      The amounts paid to a worker placed by an agency are insurable if all of the following conditions apply:

      • The employment is performed in Canada
      • The agency’s contract with the client is restricted to providing the worker
      • The worker is placed in that employment by the agency to perform services for and under the direction and control of the client of the agency
      • The worker is remunerated by the agency for the performance of those services

      • If the worker’s employment does not meet all conditions above, the amounts paid are not insurable.

      • If the worker’s employment meets all conditions above, the amounts paid are insurable.

  4. Withhold deductions from the payment

    If an employment agency places a worker under the direction and control of a client of the agency, the following deductions must be withheld depending on who paid the worker:

    • Paid by the agency

      The employment agency is responsible to:

      • Income tax - Do not withhold, the worker is responsible for their own income tax
      • CPP contributions - Withhold
      • EI premiums - Withhold

    • Paid by the client of the agency

      The client of the agency is responsible to:

      • Income tax - Withhold, calculate income tax deductions using the bonus or irregular payments method
      • CPP contributions - Withhold
      • EI premiums - Do not withhold

    • Paid to the self-employed worker

      • Self-employed employment agency workers are responsible for remitting CPP contributions, EI and income tax in the same way as other self-employed individuals.

    • If the worker is a self-employed employment agency worker, do not continue to the next step.

      Generally, if you provide payments to a self-employed worker for their services, you need to report the amounts on a T4A slip.

      Learn more depending on the worker’s situation:

      • Payments to First Nations workers
      • Payments of fees for services
    • If the payment is not made to a self-employed employment agency worker, continue to Step 5 - Report the payment on a T4 slip.

  5. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    Depending on the employment agency worker’s situation, you must report the following amounts on a T4 slip:

    • Box 29 - Employment code 11
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Box 56 - PPIP insurable earnings if individual worked in the province of Quebec
    • Code 81 - Placement or employment agency worker – Gross income

    Learn more: T4 slip - Information for employers
