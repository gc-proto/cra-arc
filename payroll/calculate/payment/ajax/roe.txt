<p>You <strong>have</strong> to report on the <abbr title="record of employment">ROE</abbr> any amounts that are considered <strong>employment income</strong>.</p>
<p>Generally, you have to provide the <abbr title="record of employment">ROE</abbr> to your employee within <strong>5 calendar days</strong> after the end of the pay period in which your employee’s interruption of earnings occurs to issue it.</p> 

<p>The <abbr title="record of employment">ROE</abbr> helps determine the employee’s eligibility for any Employment Insurance (EI) benefits.</p>

<p>Learn more: <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/programs/ei/ei-list/reports/roe-guide.html">ESDC | Employers: How to complete the record of employment (ROE) form</a>.</p>
