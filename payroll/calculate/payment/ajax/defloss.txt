<dl class="dl-horizontal">
<dt>Damages</dt> <dd><div id="damages"><p>An amount paid as a result of a judgment or of a settlement in compensation for a loss, or a moral or physical injury. In wrongful dismissal actions, when the worker is not reinstated, the amount allocated is "damages" since under such circumstances, the amount is generally paid to compensate a loss of reputation and income, rather than to put the person back in the same position as if nothing had taken place during the period the person was not working. </p> </div></dd>

<dt>Payment for long service </dt> <dd><div id="lservice"><p>An amount paid by the employer to an employee that is related to the time the employee spent in that employment. </p> </div>
</dd>

<dt>Payment for loss of office or employment </dt> <dd><div id="loss"><p>An amount paid by an employer to an employee for the only reason that the employer has terminated the contract of employment (it is important to note that such a payment would not be made in the case of a temporary lay-off). </p> </div>
</dd>

<dt>Payment for long service </dt> <dd><div id="lservice"><p>An amount paid by the employer to an employee that is related to the time the employee spent in that employment. </p> </div>
</dd>

<dt>Pay in lieu of notice </dt> <dd><div id="lnotice"><p>An amount paid by the employer to an employee in cases where an employee is dismissed or laid off without the necessary time notification prescribed by the applicable statute; the amount represents the earnings that the employee would have earned if the employer had given him or her the required notice prior to dismissal or lay-off. </p></div>
</dd>

<dt>Retiring allowance</dt> <dd><div id="retiring"><p>As defined in <strong>subsection 1(1) <abbr title="Insurable Earnings and Collection of Premiums Regulations">IECPR</abbr>, a "retiring allowance" means an amount received by a person.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>(a) on or after retirement of the person from an office or employment in recognition of the person's long service, or</li>
<li>(b) in respect of a loss of an office or employment of the person, whether or not received as, on account or in lieu of payment of, damages or pursuant to an order or judgment of a competent tribunal.</li>
</ul></div>
</dd>

<dt>Severance pay  </dt> <dd><div id="severance"><p>This term usually encompasses more than one type of payment made by an employer when the contract of employment of the employee is terminated; for this reason, it is important to try to determine the exact nature of the various payments. </p></div>
</dd>
