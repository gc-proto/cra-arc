Employees whose province of employment is Quebec, including if employees are transferred in another province or territory

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You may have employees whose province of employment is Quebec. If you have a place of business in Quebec and in another province or territory, you may want transfer your employee.

Depending on your situation, your withholding and reporting obligations will be different.

Determine your withholding and reporting obligations:

  • CPP contributions
  • EI premiums
  • Income tax
  • GST/HST
CPP contributions

Steps

The CPP and QPP have different contribution rates. If your employee moves between provinces, their contributions must be reconciled to avoid underpayment or overpayment.

  1. Determine if you need to withhold CPP

    If you have employees whose province of employment is Quebec, regardless of your employee's province or territory of residence, you have to deduct QPP contributions instead of CPP contributions.

    Learn more: Québec Pension Plan Contributions | Revenu Québec

  2. Calculate the CPP contributions

    If you transfer an employee from Quebec to another province or territory, you must take into account the QPP contributions you deducted from that employee throughout the year when calculating the maximum CPP contributions to deduct.

    CPP/QPP reconciliation formula

    The following formula reconciles the amounts contributed to the CPP and QPP to make sure that enough contributions to the CPP are withheld and future benefits are not affected:

    Step 1 - Determine the conversion factor

    • CPP contribution rate
    • divide by QPP insurance rates and amounts 
    • equals This is the conversion factor

    Step 2 - Calculate the CPP equivalent of QPP contributions

    • QPP contributions withheld from your employees pay periods
    • multiply by Amount of the conversion factor
    • equals This amount is the CPP equivalent to include in the annual maximum CPP contributions for the year
    Example - Calculation with CPP contributions

    The employer has establishments in both Quebec and Newfoundland. After the first 2 pay periods in March 20X5, the employer transfers their employee Melodie to their Newfoundland establishment. She had $400 in QPP contributions withheld from her 2 pay periods as an employee with a province of employment of Quebec.

    To determine the amount of CPP contributions to withhold, the employer applies the following formula to determine the amount to take into account in making CPP contributions:

    Step 1 - Determine the conversion factor

    • 5.95% is the CPP contribution rate in March 20X5
    • divide by 6.40% is the QPP contribution rate in March 20X5
    • equals 0.9296875 is the conversion factor
    Step 2 - Calculate the QPP contribution based on the conversion factor
    • $400 is the QPP contributions withheld from Melodie's 2 pay periods
    • multiply by 0.9296875 is the amount of the conversion factor
    • equals $371.88 is the CPP equivalent to include in the annual maximum CPP contributions for the year
    Example - Calculation with CPP2 contributions

    The employer has establishments in both Quebec and Newfoundland. The employer transferred their employee Kamal to their Newfoundland establishment after she had made the annual maximum QPP contribution and began to make QPP2 contributions. She had $40 in QPP2 contributions withheld from her additional pensionable earnings as an employee with a province of employment of Quebec.

    To determine the amount of CPP2 contributions to withhold, the employer applies the following formula to determine the amount to take into account in making CPP2 contributions:

    Step 1 - Determine the conversion factor

    • 4% is the CPP2 contribution rate in March 20X5
    • divide by 4% is the QPP2 contribution rate in March 20X5
    • equals 1 is the conversion factor
    Step 2 - Calculate the QPP2 contribution based on the conversion factor
    • $40 is the QPP2 contributions withheld from Kamal's 2 pay periods
    • multiply by 1 is the amount of the conversion factor
    • equals $40 is the CPP2 equivalent to include in the annual maximum CPP2 contributions for the year

  3. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    In addition to deducting CPP/QPP contributions, you will also have to prepare at least two T4 slips:

    • One for your employee’s earnings with a province of employment of Quebec
    • One for their earnings with the other province of employment

    It is important that you calculate and report the proper deductions and insurable/pensionable earnings on each T4 slip.

EI premiums

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to withhold EI

    If you have employees whose province of employment is Quebec, regardless of your employee's province or territory of residence, you have to deduct a reduced EI premium using the Quebec EI premium rates and maximums in addition to the Québec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) premiums.

    Learn more: Québec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) Premiums | Revenu Québec

  2. Determine if your employee reached the maximum premiums

    You may have establishments in Quebec and in another province or territory of Canada. If you transfer an employee between Quebec and another province or territory of Canada, you must:

    • Withhold EI based on either the Federal EI rate or the Quebec EI rate depending on your employee’s province of employment.
    • Determine if your employee reached the maximum premium for the year in their employment with you based on the provinces or territories where the first of insurable earnings is paid.

  3. Calculate the EI premiums

    If you transfer an employee from Quebec to another province or territory, do not do not reconcile or adjust EI premiums based on what was deducted in the other province. Each employer (or each province of employment) must deduct EI premiums independently.

    • Total insurable income for the pay period (step 2)
    • multiply by EI rate 
    • equals This amount is the EI premiums to deduct for the pay period
    Calculation example

    Joseph's total insurable income for the period is $550. Joseph's province of employment is Manitoba.

    • $550 is Joseph's total insurable income for the pay period
    • multiply by 1.64% is the 2025 EI premium rate
    • equals $9.02 is the amount of EI premiums to deduct for the pay period

  4. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    You may have an establishment in Quebec and in another province or territory.

    How to report on T4 slips

    In addition to deducting EI/QPIP premiums, you will also have to prepare at least two T4 slips:

    • One for your employee’s earnings with a province of employment of Quebec
    • One for their earnings with the other province of employment

    It is important that you calculate and report the proper deductions and insurable/pensionable earnings on each T4 slip.

Income tax

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to withhold income tax

    If you pay your employee remuneration or other income and their province of employment is Quebec, as well as deducting federal income tax, you will also have to deduct Quebec provincial income tax and remit the provincial tax to Revenu Quebec.

    Learn more: Source Deductions of Québec Income Tax | Revenu Québec (revenuquebec.ca)

  2. Report the payment on a T4 slip

GST/HST

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to remit GST/HST

    In Quebec, Revenu Québec administers the GST/HST unless you are a person that is a selected listed financial institution (SLFI) for GST/HST or QST purposes or both.

    If you pay your employee remuneration or other income and their province of employment is Quebec, as well as deducting federal income tax, you will have to remit the GST/HST to Revenu Quebec.

    Learn more if the physical location of your business is in Quebec: GST/HST and QST | Revenu Québec

  2. Report the payment on a T4 slip
