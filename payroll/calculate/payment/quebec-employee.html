Employees whose province of employment is Quebec

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You may have employees whose province of employment is Quebec. This includes situations where you transfer your employee between one of your establishments in Quebec and one in another province or territory.

Depending on your situation, your withholding and reporting obligations will be different.

Steps

  1. Confirm your employee’s province of employment (POE)

    Learn more: Determine the province of employment (POE)

  2. Determine if you need to deduct CPP or QPP

    1. You must deduct one of the following from the pensionable earnings you pay to your employee based on their province of employment (POE) regardless of your employee’s province or territory of residence:

      •   Quebec

        If the POE is Quebec, you must deduct using the QPP contribution rate 

      •    Any other province or territory

        If the POE is any other province or territory, you must deduct using the CPP contribution rate

      •        If you transfer an employee from one of your establishments in another province or territory to Quebec, refer to: Employee Transferred From an Establishment Subject to the Canada Pension Plan to an Establishment Subject to the Québec Pension Plan | Revenu Québec

        If the POE of your employee does not change during the year, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if you need to deduct using the federal or Quebec EI premium rate.

      •         If you transfer an employee from one of your establishments in Quebec to one in another province or territory, you must take into account the QPP contributions you deducted from that employee throughout the year when calculating the maximum CPP contributions to deduct.

        Continue to: Step 2b - Calculate the CPP contributions using the reconciliation formula.

    2. Calculate the CPP contributions using the reconciliation formula

      You must use the following formula before the end of the year to reconcile the amounts contributed to the CPP and QPP to make sure that enough contributions to the CPP are withheld and future benefits are not affected:

      1. Determine the conversion factor

      • CPP contribution rate
      • divide by QPP contribution rate 
      • equals This is the conversion factor

      2. Calculate the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum CPP contributions

      • QPP contributions withheld from your employee’s pensionable earnings
      • multiply by Conversion factor
      • equals This is the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum CPP contributions for the year
      Example - Calculation with CPP contributions

      The employer has establishments in both Quebec and Newfoundland. After the first 2 pay periods in March 20X5, the employer transfers their employee Melodie to their Newfoundland establishment. She had $400 in QPP contributions withheld from her 2 pay periods as an employee with a province of employment of Quebec.

      To determine the amount of CPP contributions to withhold, the employer applies the following formula to determine the amount to include when determining the employee’s maximum CPP contributions:

      1. Determine the conversion factor

      • 5.95% is the CPP contribution rate in March 20X5
      • divide by 6.40% is the QPP contribution rate in March 20X5
      • equals 0.9296875 is the conversion factor

      2. Calculate the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum QPP contributions for the year

      • $400 is the QPP contributions withheld from Melodie's 2 pay periods
      • multiply by 0.9296875 is the amount of the conversion factor
      • equals $371.88 is the amount to include when determining the employee’s maximum CPP contributions for the year
      Example - Calculation with CPP2 contributions

      The employer has establishments in both Quebec and Newfoundland. The employer transferred their employee Kamal to their Newfoundland establishment after she had made the annual maximum QPP contribution and began to make QPP2 contributions. She had $40 in QPP2 contributions withheld from her additional pensionable earnings as an employee with a province of employment of Quebec.

      To determine the amount of CPP2 contributions to withhold, the employer applies the following formula to determine the amount to include when determining the employee’s maximum CPP2 contributions:

      1. Determine the conversion factor

      • 4% is the CPP2 contribution rate in March 20X5
      • divide by 4% is the QPP2 contribution rate in March 20X5
      • equals 1 is the conversion factor

      2. Calculate the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum QPP2 contributions for the year

      • $40 is the QPP2 contributions withheld from Kamal's 2 pay periods
      • multiply by 1 is the amount of the conversion factor
      • equals $40 is the amount to include when determining the employee’s maximum CPP2 contributions for the year

      What happens if you do not use the reconciliation formula

      If you do not use the reconciliation formula before the end of the year, you may overdeduct QPP or under-deduct CPP contributions. If you under deducted CPP, you are responsible for remitting both the employer's and employee's shares.

      If there are discrepancies between the required from the employee's pensionable earnings and reported amounts on the T4 slips, your account may be selected for a Pensionable and insurable earnings review (PIER).

  3. Determine if you need to deduct using the federal or Quebec EI premium rate

    1. You must deduct using one of the following EI premiums rate from the insurable earnings you pay to your employee based on their province of employment (POE) regardless of your employee’s province or territory of residence:

      •   Quebec

        If the POE is Quebec, you must deduct using the Quebec EI premium rate Quebec, in addition to the Québec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) premiums

        Learn more: Québec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) Premiums | Revenu Québec

      •    Any other province or territory

        If the POE is any other province or territory, you must deduct using the Federal EI premium rate

      • If the POE of your employee does not change during the year, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if you need to withhold income tax.

      •         If you transfer an employee from one of your establishments in Quebec to one in another province or territory, you must take into account the Quebec EI premiums you deducted from that employee throughout the year when calculating the maximum Federal EI premiums to deduct.

        Continue to: Step 3b - Calculate the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum EI premiums.

    2. Calculate the amount to include when determining your employee’s maximum EI premiums

      You may have establishments in Quebec and in another province or territory of Canada. If you transfer an employee between Quebec and another province or territory of Canada, you must:

      • Withhold EI based on either the Federal EI rate or the Quebec EI rate depending on your employee’s province of employment.
      • Determine if your employee reached the maximum premium for the year in their employment with you based on the provinces or territories where the first of insurable earnings is paid.

      Calculate the EI premiums

      If you transfer an employee from Quebec to another province or territory, do not do not reconcile or adjust EI premiums based on what was deducted in the other province. Each employer (or each province of employment) must deduct EI premiums independently.

      • Total insurable income for the pay period (step 2)
      • multiply by EI rate 
      • equals This amount is the EI premiums to deduct for the pay period
      Calculation example

      Joseph's total insurable income for the period is $550. Joseph's province of employment is Manitoba.

      • $550 is Joseph's total insurable income for the pay period
      • multiply by 1.64% is the 2025 EI premium rate
      • equals $9.02 is the amount of EI premiums to deduct for the pay period

  4. Determine if you need to withhold income tax

    If you pay your employee remuneration or other income and their province of employment is Quebec, as well as deducting federal income tax, you will also have to deduct Quebec provincial income tax and remit the provincial tax to Revenu Quebec.

    Learn more: Source Deductions of Québec Income Tax | Revenu Québec (revenuquebec.ca)

  5. Report the payment on T4 slips

    You must report your employee’s earnings and the proper CPP or QPP deductions for each POE on each T4 slip.

    Learn more: T4 slip - Information for employers
