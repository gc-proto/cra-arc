Payments to hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering workers

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of payments to hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering workers.

You may hire a worker for hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering workers.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the worker is an employee or self-employed

    If you pay the worker for hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering workers, the worker can either be anemployee or a self-employed worker.

    • If the worker is a self-employed worker, do not continue to the next step.

      You do not withhold CPP, EI or income tax deductions.

      Generally, if you provide payments to a self-employed worker for their services and you are not considered their employer, you need to report the amounts on a T4A slip.

      Learn more: Payments of fees for services

      If you are not sure if the worker is an employee or self-employed for CPP/EI purposes

      You need to consider multiple factors to determine if the worker is an employee or is self-employed. You can use the CRA guidance to help you gather the facts based on your situation.

      Learn more about the factors the CRA considers when determining a worker’s employment status: Employee or Self-employed

    • If the worker is an employee, continue to Step 2 - Determine if the employment is pensionable and insurable.

  2. Determine if the employment is pensionable and insurable

    The employment is pensionable or insurable, or both, if all of the following apply:

      • CPP purposes

        The employment is in Canada and at the location of the circus, fair, parade, carnival, exposition, exhibition, or other similar activity.

      • EI purposes

        The employment is in Canada and in connection with the circus, fair, parade, carnival, exposition, exhibition, or other similar activity. However, it does not have to be at the location of the event.

    • Your employee works for you regularly

    • Your employee works for you for 7 or more days in a calendar year

    • If the employment meets all conditions above, the employment is pensionable or insurable from the first day the employment becomes regular or from the first day of work.

    • If the employment does not meet all conditions above, the employment is not pensionable or insurable.

      Where the employment is not pensionable, your employee can elect to pay CPP contributions on these earnings using Form CPT20, Election to Pay Canada Pension Plan Contributions when filing their personal income tax return.

  3. Determine which deductions you need to withhold

    You must withhold the following deductions on amounts paid to your employee for hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering activities:

    • Pensionable and insurable

      • Income tax
      • CPP contributions
      • EI premiums

    • Not pensionable and insurable

      • Income tax
      • CPP contributions (do not withhold)
      • EI premiums (do not withhold)

    The amounts you withhold must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Learn more: How to calculate

  4. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    You must report the following on amounts paid to your employee for hunting, trapping, forestry, logging or lumbering activities on a T4 slip:

    • Pensionable and insurable

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings

    • Not pensionable and insurable

      • Box 14 - Employment income

    Learn more: How to report - T4 slip - Information for employers
