Employer restructuring, succession of employers and amalgamations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You (as the employer) may have changes to the structure of your business, either an employer restructuring, succession of employers or an amalgamation. Depending on the nature of the change, your withholding, remitting and reporting obligations may be different.

Determine your withholding and reporting obligations based on the following changes to your business structure:

  • Employer restructuring or succession of employers
  • Amalgamation
Employer restructuring or succession of employers

Steps

  1. Confirm the nature of the change to your business is a restructuring or succession of employer

    An employer restructuring or succession of employer occurs where an employer, with the agreement of the former employer or by operation of law, immediately succeeds another employer as a result of any of the following:

    • Formation of a corporation
    • Dissolution of a corporation
    • Acquisition of all or part of a business
    What if you are not sure whether a restructuring or a succession of employers applies to you

    If you are not sure whether the conditions for an employer restructuring or succession of employers have been met, you can ask the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for a ruling.

    Learn more: How to get a CPP/EI ruling

    Learn more: Employer restructuring/Succession of employers - CPP/EI explained

    • If the change to your business is not an employer restructuring or succession of employers, do not continue to the next step.

    • If the change to your business is an employer restructuring or succession of employers, continue to: Step 2 - Determine the impact of restructuring or succession of employer on the payroll (RP) program accounts.

  2. Determine the impact of restructuring or succession of employer on the payroll (RP) program accounts

    When restructuring or succession of employer occurs:

    • Previous employer

      The business number (BN) of the previous employer must be closed, including all of their CRA program accounts.

    • Successor employer

      The successor employer cannot continue to use the BN of the previous employer. They will either:

      • Continue using their payroll account number
      • Register for a new BN, including new program accounts for the business

    Learn more on how to close or open a business number using our online services: Sign in to your CRA account

  3. Determine how to calculate the CPP contributions and EI premiums deductions

    • Previous employer

      The previous employer must deduct CPP contributions and EI premiums up to the day before the change.

    • Successor employer

      If all of the conditions are met for change to be considered an employer restructuring or a succession of employers, the successor employer has the option to consider the employment of the employee to be continuous for the purpose of CPP contributions and EI premiums deductions.

      • Not continuous

        If the successor employer does not choose to consider the employment of the employees to be continuous, the successor employer must deduct CPP contributions and EI premiums without taking into account what the previous employer paid. This is the case even if the employee has contributed the maximum amount during the previous employment.

        • The CRA will credit the excess CPP contributions and EI premiums to the employee when they file their income tax and benefit return.

        • The successor employer cannot request a refund for the employer's share of CPP contributions and EI premiums.

      • Continuous

        If the successor employer chooses to consider the employment of the employees to be continuous, the successor employer continues to deduct CPP contributions and EI premiums as a continuation of the previous employer’s deductions, taking into account what the previous employer deducted when determining the annual maximum for each.

        Employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the previous employer

        If the employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the previous employer, the successor employer is not required to make any further deductions.

  4. Determine how to calculate the income tax deductions

    The previous and successor employers must deduct income tax from the remuneration or other income they have paid to their employees regardless of whether the employees have other employers in the year.

    • Previous employer

      The previous employer must deduct income tax up to the day before the change.

    • Successor employer

      The successor employer must deduct income tax from the date of the change to year-end.

      The successor employer can ask employees for a TD1 form if they do not have the records of the previous employer.

      If the employee has already claimed personal tax credit amounts in the year on Form TD1 with another employer, they cannot claim them again when starting employment with their employer.

  5. Remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions

    The previous and successor employers must remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions for the actual CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax amounts they deducted.

    • Previous employer

      The previous employer must provide a final remittance of CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax up to the day before the change within 7 calendar days of the end of the restructuring or succession of employers.

    • Successor employer

      The successor employer must remit CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax from the date of the change to year-end.

    Learn more on which method to use to remit based on the situation: When to remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions

  6. Report on a T4 slip

    The previous and successor employers must report on the T4 slips the actual income paid and the actual CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax amounts they deducted.

    • Previous employer

      The previous employer must file T4 slips up to the day before the change.

    • Successor employer

      The successor employer must file T4 slips from the date of the change to year-end.

      The successor employer will only report the insurable earnings using box 24 and pensionable earnings using box 26 remaining up to the annual maximum. This is to make sure that the calculations of the CPP contributions and EI premiums deducted match the pensionable and insurable income amounts on the T4 slip. It may also prevent unnecessary Pensionable and insurable earnings review (PIER).

      Employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the previous employer

      If the employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the previous employer, the successor employer must enter $0 in boxes 16, 16A, 17, 17A, 18, 24, and 26.

    Example

    A succession of employers occurs between employer A and employer B in the 2026 calendar year. Their employee, whose province of employment was Ontario, earned $50,000 with the previous employer and $40,000 with the successor employer. The employer received a CPP/EI Rulings decision confirming that a valid succession of employers had occurred.

Amalgamation

Steps

  1. Confirm the nature of the change to your business is an amalgamation

    An amalgamation occurs when two or more corporations, known as predecessor corporations, combine their businesses to form a new successor corporation.

    • Predecessor corporation is a business that existed independently prior to the date of amalgamation.
    • Successor corporation is a business that is formed as a result of an amalgamation.
      • The successor corporation is considered a continuation of all the predecessor corporations.
      • The successor corporation acquires all of the assets and liabilities of the predecessor corporations.

    • If the change to your business is not an amalgamation, do not continue to the next step.

    • If the change to your business is an amalgamation, continue to: Step 2 - Determine the impact of amalgamations on the payroll (RP) program accounts.

  2. Determine the impact of amalgamations on the payroll (RP) program accounts

    When an amalgamation occurs, the information from the predecessors RP program accounts is moved to the successor RP program account.

    If the predecessor or the successor closes a predecessor RP account rather than requesting a transfer, it is treated as a closed account and the payroll account of the successor is treated as a new account. In this situation, the data is not transferred.

    • Keep the predecessor BN

      The successor corporation can keep the business number (BN) of one of the predecessor corporation (unless one of the corporation is a non-resident).

    • Register for a new BN

      The successor corporation can register for a new BN and CRA accounts for the newly created corporation

    Required documents to confirm the amalgamation

    To avoid processing delays, the successor corporation must send all of the following required documents:

    • Letter to confirm if the successor corporation wants to keep the predecessor BN, including the CRA number to keep or wants to register for a new BN.

      • The certificate of amalgamation from either Corporations Canada and the provincial and territorial governments
      • A court order

    Based on this information, the CRA will make the necessary changes to the predecessor BN including the payroll account. If required, the CRA will register a new BN and CRA accounts for the newly created corporation.

    How to send the required documents

    The successor employer must send the required documents online, by fax or by mail.

    Online - For faster service, send the required documents using our online services

    Send the required documents using our online services:

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Click “Submit documents” in the Correspondence menu and then click “Submit documents” at the bottom of the page
    3. Click “Start” and answer “No” to the question “Do you have a case or reference number?”
    4. Select “Payroll notices, statements, and vouchers” from the Topic menu
    5. Select “Other payroll accounts notice(s)”
    6. Select the “Program account number” associated to the documents you are submitting and click “Next”
    7. Click “+ Attachments” to attach your required documents and use the “Browse” button to choose the file(s) you want to attach from your device (if more than one file is being added, each must have a unique name)
    8. Enter that the successor corporation is confirming if they want to keep the BN or register for a new one, that you are providing proof of amalgamation, add that the request is for "Employer Services" in the blank field “Provide a brief description of your document” and click “Upload file(s)”
    9. If you want to attach more documents, click “+Attachments” again and repeat steps 2-3 and when all your documents are added, click “Next”
    10. Review and click “Submit”

    Keep the confirmation page for your records. You cannot use the confirmation number on this page to send additional documents.

  3. Determine how to calculate the CPP contributions and EI premiums deductions

    When an amalgamation occurs, the successor corporation is not considered a new employer for CPP or EI purposes.

    • Predecessor corporation

      The predecessor corporation must deduct CPP contributions and EI premiums up to the day before the change.

    • Successor corporation

      The successor corporation continues to deduct CPP contributions and EI premiums as a continuation of the predecessor corporation’s deductions, taking into account what the predecessor corporation deducted when determining the annual maximum for each.

      Employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the predecessor corporation

      If the employee reached their maximum CPP contributions and EI premiums with the predecessor corporation, the successor corporation is not required to make any further deductions.

      What if the predecessor corporation had a reduced EI premium rate

      Reduced EI premium rate

      The successor corporation must re-apply for the reduced EI premium rate with Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) after an amalgamation.

      ESDC will inform them if it is approved and confirm the reduced EI premium rate to use. If the rate is different than the predecessor's EI premium rate, it is effective from the date of amalgamation.

      Until ESDC completes their review, the reduced EI premium rate RP account will maintain its existing remittance rates.

      Learn more on how and when to apply for a EI premium reduction through ESDC, including what you have to send with your application: ESDC | EI Premium Reduction Program: For employers

  4. Determine how to calculate the income tax deductions

    The predecessor and successor corporation must deduct income tax from the remuneration or other income they have paid to their employees regardless of whether the employees have other employers in the year.

    • Predecessor corporation

      The predecessor corporation must deduct income tax up to the day before the change.

    • Successor corporation

      The successor corporation must deduct income tax from the date of the change to year-end.

      The successor corporation can ask employees for a TD1 form if they do not have the records of the predecessor corporation.

      If the employee has already claimed personal tax credit amounts in the year on Form TD1 with another employer, they cannot claim them again when starting employment with their employer.

  5. Remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions

    When an amalgamation occurs, the successor corporation:

    • Old BN

      The successor corporation must continue to remit CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax according to the remitter type of the successor corporation’s BN.

    • New BN

      • Predecessor corporation

        The predecessor corporation must provide a final remittance of CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax up to the day before the change within 7 calendar days of the amalgamation.

      • Successor corporation

        The successor corporation must remit CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax from the date of the change to year-end.

    Learn more on which method to use to remit based on the situation: When to remit (pay) payroll deductions and contributions

  6. Report on a T4 slip

    The due dates for filing returns will depend on whether the predecessor's remittance balance is transferred to the payroll account of the successor company.

    • Balance is not transferred to the successor's business number

      If the remittance balance is not transferred to the successor's payroll account:

      • The predecessor has to file returns for the period up to the day before the amalgamation within 30 days of the account closure
      • The successor has to file the returns from the amalgamation date until the end of the year by the regular due dates

    • Balance is transferred to the successor's business number

      The predecessor company does not have to file returns for the period leading up to the amalgamation and the successor company files the returns for the entire year by the regular due dates.

    Learn more on the method to use to file based on the information return type: How to file information returns (slips and summaries)
