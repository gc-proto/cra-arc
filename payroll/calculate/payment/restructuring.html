Employer who is restructuring

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You (as the employer) may restructure your business. Depending on your situation, your withholding, remitting and reporting obligations is different.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine the employer status

    An employer, with the agreement of the former employer or by operation of law, may immediately succeeds another employer as a result of any of the following:

    • Formation of a corporation
    • Dissolution of a corporation
    • Acquisition of all or part of a business
    What if you are not sure if you are considered the succession of the employer

    If you are not sure whether CPP contributions or EI premiums, or both, are payable in a particular restructuring or succession of employers situation, you can ask the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for a ruling.

    Learn more: How to get a CPP/EI ruling

    Learn more: Employer restructuring/Succession of employers - CPP/EI explained

    • If all of the conditions are met for the succession to apply, the successor employer has the option to consider the employment of the employee to be continuous.

    • If all of the conditions are not met for the succession to apply, the employees are considered new hires.

  2. Determine how to calculate the withholdings

    The calculation of the withholding depends on whether:

    • CPP contributins and EI premiums

      • Succession applies

        If the successor employer chooses to consider the employment of the employee to be continuous, CPP and EI deductions made by the former employer are treated as having been made by the successor employer. The successor employer may need to ask employees for a TD1 form if they do not have the records of the previous employer.

        If the successor employer does not choose to consider the former employer’s deductions, they must withhold CPP contributions and EI premiums until your employees reaches the maximum without taking into account deductions made by previous employer.

      • Succession does not apply

        The employer must withhold CPP contributions and EI premiums until your employees reaches the maximum without taking into account deductions made by previous employer or another employer.

    • Income tax

      The successor and the previous employer must deduct income tax from the remuneration or other income they have paid to their employees regardless of whether they have other employers in the year.

      If the employee has already claimed personal tax credit amounts in the year on Form TD1 with another employer, they cannot claim them again when starting employment with their employer.

    What if the previous employer withheld deductions but did not remit

    The trustee (or previous employer) may be legally required to hold amounts in trust and remit. If a trustee continues to operate the employer’s business, they must get a new BN and continue to deduct and remit.

    Learn more: How to calculate

  3. Determine who is required to file the T4 slip

    You must file the T4 slip depending on whether the remittance balance was:

    • Moved

      If the remittance balance was moved to your BN, you are required to file T4 slips for the full year.

    • Not moved

      If the remittance balance was not moved, the:

      • Previousemployer is required to file T4 slips up to the day before the transfer
      • Successoremployer is required to file T4 slips from transfer date to year-end

  4. Report on a T4 slip

    The previous and successor employers must report on the T4 slips for the actual CPP contributions, EI premiums and income tax amounts they deducted.

    The You must report on a T4 slip depending on whether the employer was:

    • Successor employer

    • Previous employer

      The successor employer will only enter the insurable income using box 24 and pensionable income using box 26 remaining up to the annual maximum to make sure the calculations of the CPP and EI deducted match the pensionable and insurable income amounts.

    • Box 14 - Employment income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
