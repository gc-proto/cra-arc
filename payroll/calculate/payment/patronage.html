Patronage allocations (dividends)

This new content has been developed for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of patronage allocations.

You may provide patronage allocations (dividends) to a recipient. Depending whether the recipient is a Canadian resident or a non-resident of Canada, your income tax withholding and reporting will be different.

What are patronage allocations

Patronage payments include all of the following:

  • Payments to members of a co-operative calculated in proportion to a members’ patronage during the year
  • Certificates of indebtedness
  • Amounts credited towards the balance a recipient can owe the payer of the patronage
  • Shares of a corporation that an individual receives because of a patronage payment

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip

    You must report the total of the patronage allocations paid to a Canadian resident on a T4A slip or a non-resident of Canada on a NR4 slip in the year the patronage allocations are received.

  2. Withhold payroll deductions from the patronage payment

    You must withhold the following deductions from the payment:

    • Income tax or Part XIII tax - Withhold
    • EI - Do not withhold
    • CPP - Do not withhold

  3. How to calculate the tax deductions

    You have to calculate the the tax using one of the following tax rate depending on the recipient’s residency status:

    • Canadian resident

      You must calculate using the 15% income tax rate.

      Learn how to calculate income tax deductions for patronage allocations (dividents) using the bonus or irregular payments method

    • Non-resident of Canada

      You must calculate using the 25% Part XIII tax rate, unless a tax treaty between Canada and that country reduces this rate

      Learn how to calculate income tax deductions for patronage allocations (dividents) using the Non-resident tax calculator

  4. Report the payment on a slip

    You must report payments on a T4A or NR4 slip depending on the recipient’s residency status:

    • Canadian resident

      You must report the following amounts on a T4A slip:

      • Box 022 - Income tax deducted
      • Code 030 - Patronage allocations

      Learn how to report the payment: T4A slip – Information for payers

    • Non-resident of Canada

      You must report the following amounts on a NR4 slip:

      • Box 16 or 26 - Gross income
      • Box 17 or 27 - Non-resident tax withheld
      • Box 18 or 28 - Exemption code

      Learn how to report the payment: NR4 slip – Statement of amounts paid or credited to non-residents of Canada
