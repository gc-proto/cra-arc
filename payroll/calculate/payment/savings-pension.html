Payments from savings plans, pension or superannuation plans

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirements.

You may pay pension or superannuation payments or payments from a Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP) or a Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) to a recipient. Generally, you must withhold income tax deductions and report these payments on a T4A slip.

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip

    If you make a pension or superannuation, RESP or RDSP payment to a recipient, you must report the payments on a T4A slip for a Canadian resident or an NR4 for a non-resident.

  2. Determine which deductions you need to withhold from the payment

    If you make a pension or superannuation, RESP or RDSP payment to a recipient, you must withhold the following deductions:

    • Income tax, continue to the next step for information about your specific income tax withholding obligations
    • CPP (do not withhold)
    • EI (do not withhold)

    Learn more: Calculate income tax deductions

  3. Withhold and report the payment on a slip

    If you make a pension or superannuation, RESP or RDSP payment to a recipient, your income tax withholding and reporting requirements will depend on the nature of the payment:

    Situations

    Situation: Pension and superannuation payments paid to a Canadian resident

    If the payment is made to a Canadian resident, you must:

    • Withhold income tax from the payment
    • Report the payment on a T4A slip using box 016 and any other relevant reporting codes

    Learn more: T4A slip - Information for payers

    Situation: Pension and superannuation payments paid to a non-resident

    If the payment is made to a non-resident, you must:

    • Withhold tax from the payment, unless your employee completed Form NR5, Application by a Non-Resident of Canada for a Reduction in the Amount of Non-Resident Tax Required to be Withheld for the Tax Year and you received written authorization from the CRA to reduce the amount of tax to withhold (this authorization only lasts for a five year period)
    • Report the payment on an NR4 slip using income code 39 for periodic payments or code 40 for lump-sum payments in box 14 or 24
    • Report exemption code J on the NR4 slip if a tax reduction was approved

    Learn more: NR4 slip - Canada.ca and Pensions and similar payments

    Situation: Accumulated income payments made from an RESP

    If the payment was an Accumulated income payment (AIP), you must:

    • Withhold both income tax and an additional tax of 20% (12% for residents of Quebec) calculated using T1172, Additional Tax on Accumulated Income Payments from RESPs, unless all of the following apply:
      • The recipient provided you with a completed Form T1171, Tax Withholding Waiver on Accumulated Income Payments from RESPs
      • The AIPs are transferred directly to the recipient's RRSP, PRPP, or SPP or their spouse or common-law partner's RRSP or SPP
      • The recipient's RRSP deduction limit allows them to deduct the contribution in the year it is made
    • Report the payment on a T4A slip using code 040
    • Report AIPs you made to someone else because the subscriber, the subscriber's spouse or common-law partner was deceased using code 122 and code 040

    Learn more: T4A slip - Information for payers

    What is an Accumulated income payment (AIP)

    Accumulated income payments (AIPs) are amounts, usually paid to the subscriber, of the income earned from an RESP. An AIP does not include any of the following:

    • Payments of Educational assistance payments (EAP)
    • Payments to a designated educational institution in Canada
    • Refunds of contributions to the subscriber or to the beneficiary
    • Transfers to another RESP
    • Repayments under the Canada Education Savings Act or under a designated provincial program
    Situation: Educational assistance payments made from an RESP

    If the payment was an Educational assistance payment (EAP) you made to a Canadian resident:

    • Do not withhold tax from the payment unless the payment was made to a beneficiary who had become a non-resident
    • Report the payment on a T4A slip using box 042

    Learn more: T4A slip - Information for payers

    If the payment was an EAP made to someone who had become a non-resident, you must:

    • Withhold Part XIII tax at a rate of 25% or income tax if they were deemed to be employed at Canada
    • Report the payment on an NR4 slip using code 03 in box 14 or 24 or on a T4A slip using box 042 if they were deemed to be employed in Canada

    Learn more: T4A slip - Information for payers and NR4 slip - Canada.ca and Registered Education Savings Plan payments, transferring and rolling over Registered Education Savings Plan property

    What is an Educational assistance payment (EAP)

    Educational assistance payments (EAPs) are amounts paid to a beneficiary (a student) from an RESP to help finance the cost of post-secondary education. An EAP consists of the Canada education savings grant, the Canada Learning Bond, amounts paid under a designated provincial program and the earnings on the money saved in the RESP.

    Situation: RDSP payments to a Canadian resident

    If the payment was the proceeds from rollover amounts as well as the grant, bond and investment income earned in the RDSP paid to a Canadian resident, you must:

    • Withhold income tax using lump-sum withholding rates once the taxable portion exceeds the basic personal amount and disability amount of the beneficiary
    • Report taxable amounts on a T4A slip using box 131

    Learn more: T4A slip - Information for payers

    Situation: RDSP payments to a non-residents

    If the payment from an RDSP was made to a non-resident, you must:

    • Withhold Part XIII tax at a rate of 25%
    • Report taxable amounts on an NR4 slip using income code 63 in box 14 or 24

    Learn more: NR4 slip - Canada.ca and What is a registered disabililty savings plan (RDSP)
