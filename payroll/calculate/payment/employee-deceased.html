Payments related to the death of your employee

Content to be covered: Payments made after death and Death benefits

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirements.

You may pay amounts related to salary and wages to your employee after their death or death benefits to recognize their service. Your withholding and reporting requirements will depend on whether the amount was owed to your employee by you before their death or a death benefit.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. 1. Determine if the payment was a death benefit

    A death benefit is the gross amount of any payment that meets both of the following conditions:

    • it is made on or after the death of the employee
    • it is in recognition of an employee’s service in an office or employment

    Note: A death benefit can be paid to a surviving spouse or common-law partner, heir, or estate.

    Qualify as death benefits
    • payments that continue for a long time on a periodic basis, or for the lifetime of the recipient, if they were made under an employer's fund or plan that is distinctly separate from certain plans such as a superannuation or pension fund or plan, a salary deferral arrangement, or a retirement compensation arrangement
    • payments to an employee who died prior to retirement that represent a settlement of accumulated sick leave credits to which the employee was entitled under the terms of the office or employment
    Not qualify as death benefits
    • a payment received out of a superannuation or pension fund or plan, and in general, payments received out of a salary deferral arrangement, or a retirement compensation arrangement upon or after the death of an officer or employee
    • where an employee dies prior to retirement, a payment in respect of accumulated vacation leave
    • a death benefit paid under the Canada or Québec Pension Plan
    • a payment representing deferred employment income that would have been taxable in the employee's hands under subsection 6(3) if the employee had not died
    • a payment representing overtime pay that would have been taxable to the employee under subsection 5(1) if the employee had received the amount before death

    • XXX

    Non-taxable situation

    If the benefit is derived from an employee benefit plan, the benefit is not taxable if one of the following apply:

    • The benefit is a death benefit or an amount that would be a death benefit but for the deduction provided in the definition of the term in subsection 248(1)
      • The benefit is a return of amounts contributed to the plan by the person or a deceased employee of whom the person is an heir or legal representative
      • The amounts in question was not deducted from the person’s or your employee’s income for any taxation year
      • The benefit is a superannuation or pension
      • It is for services rendered in a period throughout which your employee was not resident in Canada

    If the benefit is a designated employee benefit (for example: a group sickness or accident insurance plan, a group term life insurance plan, or a private health services plan, continue to: Insurance plans.

    Taxable situation

    If the benefit does not meet all the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

  3. Withhold and report the payment on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions:

    • Withhold: Option 1

      • Income tax (threashold for a death benefit)
      • CPP (do not withhold)
      • EI (do not withhold)

    • Remit: Option 2

      • GST/HST (do not remit)

    For a death benefit, the income tax is deducted only on the amount that exceeds $10,000 using the withholding rates for lump-sum payments.

    The payment is not pensionable or insurable because the amount is not paid in respect of pensionable or insurable employment. The amounts must be included in the year they were received or enjoyed.


    Learn how to calculate deductions and the GST/HST to remit on benefits: How to calculate - Calculate payroll deductions and contributions

  4. Report the benefit on a slip

    You must report payments on a T4 or T4A slip depending on the nature of the payment and when it was paid:

    • Payments made after death

      You must report the following amounts in the name of your employee on a T4 slip:

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Box 56 - PPIP insurable earnings if your employee worked in the province of Quebec
      • Code 40 - Other information

      Learn how to report the payment: T4 slip - Information for employers

    • Death benefit

      You must report the following amounts in the name of the recipient on a T4A slip:

      • Box 016 - Pension or superannuation
      • Box 018 - Lump-sum payments
      • Code 106 - Death benefits

      Learn how to report the payment: T4A slip – Information for payers
