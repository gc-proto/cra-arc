Vacation pay and public holidays payments

This new content has been developed for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of vacation pay and public holidays payments.

Content to be covered: Vacation pay and public holidays

You may provide vacation pay and public holiday payments to your employee.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip

    You must report the payment as employment income on your employee’s T4 slip in the year the vacation pay or the holiday payments are received.

    You may provide vacation pay to your employee using the following payment methods:

    • Your employee takes holidays and you pay them while they are on vacation leave

    • Your employee takes holidays and you do not pay them while they are on vacation leave, this is because you pay them vacation pay (at the labour standards rate) on a continuous basis throughout the year

    • Your employee does not take holidays and you provide compensation instead, this is because you pay them in the form of pay (over and above their regular salary) instead of time off work

  2. Withhold payroll deductions

    You must withhold the following deductions:

    • Income tax
    • CPP contributions
    • EI premiums

    If your employee does not take holidays, calculate the:

    • Income tax using the bonus or irregular payments method
    • EI premiums using the regular method
    • CPP contributions using the bonus or irregular payments method

  3. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    You must report the following on the T4 slip:

    • Box 14 - Employment income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings

    Learn how to report the payment: T4 slip - Information for employers
