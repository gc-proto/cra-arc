Payments of stipends, fees or remuneration related to tenure of office (elected or appointed officials)

You may be looking for:

  • Payments of fees for services

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement or to the CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you pay stipends, fees (for example, director's or trustee's fees) or remuneration to a person to provide a service in an employment relationship or tenure of office, you need to fill out a T4 slip. This includes payments made to a non-resident elected or appointed official for services.

Common examples of elected or appointed officials
  • Directors
  • Mayors
  • Municipal councillors
  • School commissioners
  • Chiefs of Indian bands
  • Band councillors
  • Executors
  • Liquidators
  • Administrators of estates
  • Members of a board of referee at the Commission of Canada Employment Insurance
  • Any other position when a person is elected or appointed to that office

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip

    If you make payments for services, you will need to report the payment if all of the following apply:

    • The payment was made to one of your employee or to a person in tenure of office
    • The individual is elected by popular vote or appointed
    • The individual is entitled to a fixed or ascertainable stipend or remuneration

    The payment is considered as employment income when the payment is received.

    What if you do not know if a worker is an employee or is self-employed

    If you or an individual working for you is not sure of the worker's employment status (employee or self-employed), either one of you can request a CPP/EI ruling to determine the status and whether the employment or the earnings are pensionable, insurable or both.

    Learn more:

    • How to get a CPP/EI ruling
    • Guide RC4110 - Employee or Self-employed?
    • CPP/EI Explained

  2. Withhold and report the payment on a slip

    If the payment is considered employment income, you must withhold the following deductions and report on a T4 slip depending whether the amount is paid with or without a salary:

    The amounts you withhold must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    • Step 2a - Payment made without the salary
    • Step 2b - Payment made with the salary

    1. Payment made without the salary

      • Withhold CPP deductions (prorated over the number of times the payments are made during the year)
      • Do not withhold EI deductions
      • Withhold income tax deductions only if the payment will exceed the TD1 claim amount
      • Report the payment on a T4 slip in box 14 - Employment Income (may include other income)

      Learn more on how to calculate using the regular payments method: How to calculate and how to report: T4 slip - Information for employers

    2. Payment made with the salary

      • Withhold CPP deductions calculated on both the salary and the payment
      • Withhold income tax deductions calculated on both the salary and the payment
      • Withhold EI deductions on the salary portion only
      • Report the payment on a T4 slip in box 14 - Employment Income (may include other income)

      Learn more on how to calculate using the bonus or irregular payments method: How to calculate and how to report: T4 slip - Information for employers
