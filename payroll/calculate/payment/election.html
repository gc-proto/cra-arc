Payments to election workers

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness, and plain language. No changes were made to the CRA's treatment of payments to election workers.

You (as a federal, provinical or territorial government body, a municipality or a school board in connection with a referendum or election to public office) may employ a worker in connection with a referendum or an election to public office.

Your CPP and EI withholding obligations will depend on whether the worker is a regular employee of the government boday and the number of hours they work for you during the year.

Steps

  1. Determine if the employment is pensionable

    The employment of an employee in connection with a referendum or election to public office is not pensionable if both of the following conditions apply:

    • Your employee is not a regular employee of the government body
    • Your employee works for less than 35 hours in a calendar year (the 35 hours do not have to be consecutive, but they must be in the same calendar year and must be with the same employer)

    • If your employee’s employment meets both of the conditions above, the employment is not pensionable.

      Where the employment is not pensionable, your employee can elect to pay CPP contributions on these earnings using Form CPT20, Election to Pay Canada Pension Plan Contributions when filing their personal income tax return.

    • If your employee’s employment does not meet the conditions above, the employment is pensionable. If the employee works 35 hours or more, their employment is pensionable from the first hour of work.

  2. Determine if the employment is insurable

    The employment of an employee in connection with a referendum or election to public office is not insurable if both of the following conditions apply:

    • Your employee is not a regular employee of the government body
    • Your employee works for less than 35 hours in a calendar year (the 35 hours do not have to be consecutive, but they must be in the same calendar year and must be with the same employer)

    • If your employee’s employment meets both of the conditions above, the employment is not insurable.

    • If your employee’s employment does not meet the conditions above, the employment is insurable. If the employee works 35 hours or more, the employment is insurable from the first hour of work.

  3. Determine which deductions you need to withhold

    You must withhold the following deductions from amounts paid to your employee in connection with a referendum or an election to public office:

    • Income tax
    • CPP contributions - If employment is pensionable (Step 1)
    • EI premiums - If the employment is insurable (Step 2)

    Learn more: How to calculate

  4. Report the payment on a T4 slip

    You must report the following on amounts paid to your employee in connection with a referendum or election to public office on a T4 slip:

    • Box 14 - Employment income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings, if the employment is insurable (Step 2)
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings, if employment is pensionable (Step 1)

    Learn more: How to report - T4 slip - Information for employers
