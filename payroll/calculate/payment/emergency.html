Payments to emergency services volunteers

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You (as a government, municipality or public authority) may pay volunteers as emergency services volunteers for their services. A person cannot be a volunteer if their regular line of work is the same as volunteer activities.

Depending on the situation, the volunteer may qualify for an exemption on amounts paid up to $1,000 if all conditions are met.

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a T4 slip

    Generally, you (as a government, municipality or public authority) must report payments you make to an emergency services volunteer on a T4 slip.

    Under the CRA administrative policy, you must report the payments on a T4 slip if one of the following apply:

    • You deducted CPP, EI or income tax from the payment.
    • The total of all payments in the calendar year were more than $500.

  2. Determine if the volunteer qualifies for the tax exemption

    Under the Income Tax Act, if you pay an emergency services volunteer for their services, the volunteer qualifies for the tax exemption of up to $1,000 if:

    • You are a government, municipality, or public authority.

    • The volunteer is one of the following:

      • Volunteer firefighters
      • Volunteer ambulance technicians
      • Emergency service volunteers who help in the search and rescue of individuals or in other emergency situations and disasters
    • The volunteer was not employed in the year by the same public authority for the same or similar duties (for example: a full-time firefighter who, from time to time, acts as a volunteer firefighter or rescue worker for their employer does not qualify for the exemption).
    • The amount paid for the duties that the volunteer performed is a nominal amount compared with what it would have cost in the same circumstances to have the same duties performed by a regular full-time or part-time individual.

    • If the emergency services volunteer does not meet all conditions above, the volunteer does not qualify for the tax exemption.

    • If the emergency services volunteer meets all conditions above, the volunteer qualifies for the tax exemption of up to $1,000.

  3. Determine if the amounts paid are pensionable and insurable

    The amounts paid to an emergency services volunteer are pensionable and insurable if all of the following apply:

    • The emergency services volunteer receives an hourly wage, salary, or other fixed amount of remuneration.
    • The emergency services volunteer adheres to a regular work schedule.
    • The emergency services volunteer is available and obligated to intervene when an emergency (such as a fire) occurs during the schedule you (as the employer) set. However, if they must be available during the fixed work scheduled but are not obligated to intervene when the emergency occurs, the amounts paid are not pensionable and insurable.

    Learn more if you are not sure if the volunteer is considered a regular employee or a volunteer: CPP/EI explained, Rescue operations-Abating a disaster

    • If the emergency services volunteer does not meet all conditions above, the amounts paid are not pensionable or insurable.

    • If the emergency services volunteer meets all conditions above, the amounts paid are pensionable or insurable.

  4. Determine which deductions you need to withhold from the amounts paid

    If you pay an individual who performs the duties of certain emergency services volunteers on a volunteer basis, you must withhold the following deductions:

    • Income tax

      Withhold income tax depending on if the individual qualifies for the exemption (step 2):

      • Exemption applies
        Only withhold income tax on amounts paid over $1,000
      • No exemption
        Withhold income tax on all amounts paid

    • CPP contributions

      Withhold CPP contributions depending on if the individual qualifies for the exemption (step 2) and if the income is pensionable (step 3):

      • Pensionable and exemption applies
        Only withhold CPP contributions on amounts paid over $1,000
      • Pensionable and no exemption
        Withhold CPP contributions on all amounts paid
      • Not pensionable
        Do not withhold CPP contributions from the amounts paid

    • EI premiums

      Withhold EI premiums depending on if the income is insurable (step3):

      • Insurable
        Withhold EI premiums on all amounts paid
      • Not insurable
        Do not withhold EI premiums on the amounts paid

  5. Report the amounts paid on a T4 slip

    If you pay an individual who performs the duties of certain emergency services volunteers on a volunteer basis, you must report the following amounts on a T4 slip depending if the individual qualifies for the exemption (step 2) or not:

    You must report the following amounts on a T4 slip depending if the emergency services volunteer qualifies for the exemption (step 2) or not:

    • Exemption applies

      • Box 14 - Employment Income (amounts paid over $1,000)
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings (pensionable earnings and amounts paid over $1,000)
      • Code 87 (tax exempt amounts paid up to $1,000)

    • No exemption

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
