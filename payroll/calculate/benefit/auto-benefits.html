Steps

  1. Determine if a motor vehicle is an automobile

    When you make an automobile available to an employee for personal use, then your employee must pay income tax on the benefit related to the personal use of the vehicle. There are different ways of calculating the taxable benefit for a motor vehicle that is an automobile than for a motor vehicle that is not an automobile.

    Type of vehicles

    Type: Car with seating for the driver and up to four passengers
    The motor vehicle is not an automobile if any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • You bought the car to use it primarily as a taxi
    • You bought the car to sell, rent, or lease in a sales, rental or leasing business
    		Any other situation
    Type: Vehicle designed or adapted for transporting up to eight passengers
    The motor vehicle is not an automobile if any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • You bought the vehicle to use it primarily as a taxi
    • You bought the vehicle to use it primarily in a business of transporting passengers
    • You bought the vehicle to sell, rent, or lease in a sales, rental or leasing business
    		Any other situation
    Type: Emergency response vehicle (police/fire/medical)
    The emergency response vehicle is not an automobile if any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • The vehicle is an ambulance
    • The vehicle is clearly marked as a police or fire emergency response vehicle
    • The vehicle is clearly marked as an emergency medical response vehicle that you use to carry emergency medical equipment and one or more emergency medical attendants or paramedics
    		Any other situation
    Type: Pick-up truck
    The pick-up truck is not an automobile if any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • You used it at a remote work location or at a special work site primarily to transport goods or equipment in the course of business
    • The vehicle can seat no more than the driver and two passengers and you used it primarily to transport goods or equipment in the course of business
    • you used it exclusively to transport goods or equipment in the course of business

    at least 30 kilometres away from any community having a population of at least 40,000     		Any other situation
    Type: Van, minivan, sport utility or similar vehicle
    The motor vehicle is not an automobile if, in the year you bought or leased it, any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • The vehicle can seat no more than the driver and two passengers and you used it primarily to transport goods or equipment in the course of business
    • you used it exclusively to transport goods or equipment in the course of business
    		Any other situation
    Type: Hearse or another vehicle used in a funeral business to transport passengers
    The motor vehicle is not an automobile if, in the year you bought or leased it, any of the following apply: The motor vehicle is an automobile
    • If the vehicle is a hearse, you used it primarily in a funeral business
    • If the vehicle is not a hearse, you bought it to use a funeral business to transport passengers
    		Any other situation

  2. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    The vehicle is a:

    Automobile

    ...

    Motor vehicle

    You may require your employees to use a motor vehicle to perform their duties of office or employment. You may provide the motor vehicle to the employee or your employee may use their own. When you provide your employees with an automobile or a motor vehicle, the benefit may be taxable if they use the vehicle other than for business use.

    .

    Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Examples of personal use or business use

    • Personal use: Option 1

      • Travel between the employee's home and the employer's place of business, including returning to work after normal hours

    • Business use: Option 2

      • Errands for an office party
      • Travel between the employer's place of business and a client's establishment
      • Travel between the employee's home and a client's establishment, if required by the employer or with the employer's permission

    Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI

      Remit:

      • GST/HST - Reimbursements
      • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

  4. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 40 - Other Information

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 40 - Other Information
