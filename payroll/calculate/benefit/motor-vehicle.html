Motor vehicle provided by the employer

You may be looking for:

  • Automobile or motor vehicle benefits – Allowances or reimbursements provided to an employee for the use of their own vehicle

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a motor vehicle to your employee or a shareholder to use in performing their duties of office or employment. Generally, the benefit from personal driving of the employer provided motor vehicle is a taxable benefit.

  • Employer
    Includes a person related to the employer
  • Employee and shareholder
    Includes a person related to the employee or shareholder
  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the vehicle is a motor vehicle

    The Income Tax Act defines motor vehicles and automobiles differently for tax purposes.

    Answer a few questions to determine if the vehicle is a motor vehicle or an automobile.

    Launch vehicle validator

    vehicle validator

    Motor vehicle and automobile definitions
    • If the vehicle is an automobile, do not continue to next step. Refer to Automobile provided by the employer.
    • If the vehicle is a motor vehicle, continue to Step 2 - Determine if you made the motor vehicle available to the employee.

  2. Determine if you made the motor vehicle available to the employee

    • If you did not make the motor vehicle available to your employee, there is no taxable benefit to calculate. Do not continue to next step.
    • If you made the motor vehicle available to your employee, continue to Step 3 - Determine if there was personal driving of the motor vehicle.

  3. Determine if there was personal driving of the motor vehicle

    • If there is no personal driving, the use of the employer-provided motor vehicle is not a taxable benefit. Do not continue to next step.
    • If there is personal driving, the use of the employer-provided motor vehicle may be a taxable benefit. Continue to Step 4 - Determine if special considerations apply to the motor vehicle benefit.

  4. Determine if special considerations apply to the motor vehicle benefit

    • If the the use of the employer-provided motor vehicle is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the use of the employer-provided motor vehicle is not in one of the above special situation, the personal driving of the motor vehicle may be a taxable benefit. Continue to Step 5 - Determine if the motor vehicle benefit is taxable.

  5. Determine if the motor vehicle benefit is taxable

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the Income Tax Act, if you make a vehicle available  to your employee (step 2), the benefit is not taxable if all of the following (step 3) apply:

    • There is no personal driving 
    • Use of the vehicle is only in the course of their employment duties
    • The vehicle was returned to the premises of the employer at the end of the work day

    Taxable situation

    If you make a vehicle available  to your employee and they use the vehicle for both business and personal driving , the personal driving is taxable.

    You need to calculate the value of the benefit from your employee's personal driving of the motor vehicle.

    What is considered made available to the employee

    What is considered business and personal driving

    • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations. Do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to Step 6 - Review the required information (logbook) your employee must have kept.

  6. Review the required information (logbook) your employee must have kept

    • If no record is kept, you cannot calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      Continue to Step 8 - Calculate the value of the motor vehicle benefit to be included on the T4 slip.

    • If the employee kept the required records and you received a copy, you may be able to calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      Continue to Step 7 - Determine if the motor vehicle home at night policy applies to the benefit.

  7. Determine if the motor vehicle home at night policy applies to the benefit

    • If the motor vehicle home at night policy does not meet all conditions, you cannot calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      Continue to Step 8 - Calculate the value of the motor vehicle benefit to be included on the T4 slip.

    • If the motor vehicle home at night policy meets all above conditions, you can calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      Continue to Step 8: You want to calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

  8. Calculate the value of the motor vehicle benefit to be included on the T4 slip

    Use one of the following to calculate the value of the benefit related to the personal driving  of the motor vehicle:

    • Fixed rate calculation: if you meet all conditions for the motor vehicle home at night policy under step 7. This calculation is simple.
    • Reasonable allowances rates calculation: no conditions to be met under the CRA administrative policy. This calculation is simple.
    • Reasonable estimate the fair market value (FMV): no conditions to be met under the Income Tax Act. This calculation is complex.

    Situations

    Situation: You want to calculate using the fixed rate calculation

    Under the CRA administrative policy, you can use the fixed rate calculation if all the following apply:

    • Only personal driving was between home and work
    • Motor vehicle home at night policy meets all conditions (step 7)

    The fixed rates ($0.33 for 2023 (or $0.29 for 2022)) per kilometre of personal driving to calculate the motor vehicle taxable benefit:

    • Total number of kilometres travelled for personal driving
    • multiply by Fixed rate
    • equals This is the amount for the motor vehicle benefit
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you in the year for the motor vehicle benefit
    • equals This is the total value of the motor vehicle benefit which is a taxable benefit
    Calculation example

    In 2022, Matthew was required to use a motor vehicle provided by his employer in connection with his employment in Ontario. In reviewing his travel logbook, you found that he drove 10,000 km for personal driving. This personal driving was exclusively between home and work. The benefit met all other conditions of the motor vehicle home at night administrative policy. Matthew reimbursed you $300 for the personal driving of the motor vehicle.

    • 10,000 km is the total number of kilometres Matthew travelled for personal driving
    • multiply by $0.29 is the fixed rate for 2022
    • equals $2,900 is the amount for the motor vehicle benefit
    • minus $300 is the amount Matthew reimbursed for the motor vehicle benefit
    • equals $2,600 is the total value of the motor vehicle benefit which is a taxable benefit
    Situation: You want to calculate using the reasonable allowances rates calculation

    Under the CRA’s administrative policy, you can use the reasonable allowance rate  to calculate the motor vehicle taxable benefit.

    • Total number of kilometres travelled for personal driving
    • multiply by Reasonable allowance rate - First 5,000 kilometres
    • multiply by Reasonable allowance rate - Additional kilometres
    • equals This is the amount for the motor vehicle benefit
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you in the year for the motor vehicle benefit
    • equals This is the total value of the motor vehicle benefit which is a taxable benefit
    Calculation example

    In 2022, Matthew was required to use a motor vehicle provided and owned by his employer in connection with his employment in Ontario. In reviewing his travel logbook, you found that he drove 10,000 km for personal driving. Matthew reimbursed you $300 for the personal driving of the motor vehicle His personal driving of the motor vehicle was not limited to driving between home and work. His employer chose to calculate using the reasonable rates method to determine the fair market value of the personal driving of the motor vehicle.

    • 10,000 km is the total number of kilometres Matthew travelled for personal driving
    • multiply by $0.61 is the fixed rate for 2022 on the first 5,000 kilometres
    • multiply by $0.55 is the fixed rate for 2022 on the additional kilometres
    • equals $5,800 ($3,050 + $2,750) is the amount for the motor vehicle benefit
    • minus $300 is the amount Matthew reimbursed for the motor vehicle benefit
    • equals $5,500 is the total value of the motor vehicle benefit which is a taxable benefit
    Situation: You want to reasonably estimate the FMV

    This is based on all amounts that the employee would normally have had to pay for a comparable vehicle in an arm's-length transaction, such as leasing expenses and operating costs.

    The FMV is the highest price that can be obtained on an open market for a motor vehicle, where the buyer and seller consent to the transaction, are well-informed and are dealing at arm's length.

    The following calculation is provided as a guideline but other factors or expenses may need to be considered. This is calculation may be done on a case-by-case basis.

    Step A - Estimated FMV

    • This is the result A: estimated FMV for the cost of the motor vehicle (compparable if owned or leased) (do not include the GST/HST)

    Step B - Use and availability

    • Total number of days when the motor vehicle was made available to the employee
    • divide by 30 days
    • equals Result B - Use and availability (rounded to the nearest whole number if it is greater than one)

    Step C - Value of the motor vehicle benefit

    • Result B - Use and availability
    • multiply by Result A - Estimated FMV
    • equals This is the value of the motor vehicle benefit
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you for the motor vehicle benefit
    • equals This is the value of the motor vehicle benefit which is a taxable benefit

    The benefit for personal driving of a motor vehicle is a non-cash benefit.

  10. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • T4 - Employee: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings (for cash benefit only)
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 34 - Personal use of employer's automobile or motor vehicle

    • T4A - Person or partnership that was a shareholder: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Code 028 - Other income
