Board and lodging expenses

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide an allowance, a reimbursement or pay on behalf of your employees for the cost of housing (for example, house, condominium, apartment or room) or utilities (for example, electricity, natural gas, water, telephone or internet).

On this page

Steps

  1. Determine if the traveller is considered in a special situation

    • Do not continue to next step if the traveller is in one of the following special situation:
      • Benefits provided to an employee in prescribed zones
      • Benefits provided to an employee in remote locations
      • Benefits provided to an employee in special work sites
    • If no special situation apply, continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable

  2. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    Generally, housing and utilities you provide or pay on behalf of your employees are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Situations

    Situation: You provide an allowance or you reimburse your employee who is a participant or member of a sports team or recreational program for board and lodging expenses

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, if you paid an allowance or reimburse your employee who is a participant or member of a sports team or recreational program for board and lodging expenses, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • You are a registered charity or a non-profit organization
    • Participation or membership with the team or program is restricted to persons under 21 years of age
    • Allowance is for a participant or member who has to live away from their ordinary place of residence
    • Allowance is not attributable to any services, such as coaching or refereeing, that your employee provides to the team or program
    • Amount is equal or less then $377 (for 2021) per month

    Taxable situation

    If the allowance or reimbursement you provide to your employee for board and lodging expenses do not meet all of the conditions above, it is a taxable benefit.

  4. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI

      Remit:

      • GST/HST - Reimbursements
      • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

  5. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 40 - Other Information

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 40 - Other Information
