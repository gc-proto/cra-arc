Housing, utilities or travel assistance - Provided to an employee working in a prescribed zone

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you provide a benefit or an allowance for housing, utilities or travel assistance to your employee, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

Depending on your situation, the travel assistance  benefit or allowance you provide to your employee in a prescribed zone , may not be taxable. Where the benefit for housing or utilities is provided to an employee who works or lives in a prescribed zone, the value of the benefit will be based either on its fair market value (FMV)  or the ceiling amount  if the place does not have a developed rental market.

What is travel assistance

If you provide travel assistance by any mode of transportation you own or charter to your employees that is not for business travel, they receive a taxable benefit. The value of that benefit is based on the fair market value of the transportation provided.

When your employees travel other than by air, the benefit for travel assistance may include:

  • Automobile expenses
  • Meals
  • Hotel and motel accommodations
  • Camping fees
  • Taxi fares
  • Road and ferry tolls

You can provide travel assistance benefits in many ways, such as:

  • Travel allowance before the trip, such as a certain amount per hour, or on some other periodic basis
  • Lump-sum payments to your employee before or after the trip is taken
What are the ceiling amounts for housing or utilities
What is the fair market value (FMV)

Generally, if you provide meals and short-term accommodations to your employee, it is subject to GST/HST. If the benefit is taxable, include the GST/HST in the value of the benefit.

Steps

  1. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance

    If you provide a benefit or an allowance for housing, utilities or travel assistance  in one of the following situations, special conditions or rules may apply:

    • Your employee works at a remote work location
    • Your employee works at a special work site
    • Your employee is a member of the clergy, a regular minister or a member of a religious order

    Learn more on the above exceptions using the links before you continue to the next step.

    Continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit or allowance is provided in a prescribed zone and if that place has a developed rental market.

  2. Determine if the benefit or allowance is provided in a prescribed zone and if that place has a developed rental market

    1. Determine if the benefit is provided in a prescribed zone

      Select a province or territory to determine if the benefit or allowance for housing, utilities or travel assistance is provided in a prescribed zone:

      • If the benefit is not provided in a prescribed zone, do not continue to the next step.

        Refer to: Housing or utilities or Travel expenses

      • If the benefit is provided in a prescribed zone, continue to: Step 2b - Determine if the housing or utilities benefit is provided in a place with a developed rental market.

    2. Determine if the housing or utilities benefit is provided in a place with a developed rental market

      If you provide housing or utilities to your employee who works in a prescribed zone , you must determine if the place where you provide the benefit has a developed rental market to determine the value of the benefit.

      The following cities and towns in prescribed zones have a developed rental market:

      • Dawson Creek, British Columbia
      • Fort McMurray, Alberta
      • Fort St. John, British Columbia
      • Grande Prairie, Alberta
      • Labrador City, Newfoundland
      • Thompson, Manitoba
      • Yellowknife, Northwest Territories
      • Wabush, Newfoundland
      • Whitehorse, Yukon

  3. Determine if the benefit or allowance is taxable

    Generally, if you provide a benefit or an allowance for housing, utilities or to your employee, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

    Non-taxable situation

    If you provide a benefit or an allowance for travel assistance  to your employee who works in a prescribed zone (step 2), the benefit or allowance is not taxable if one of the following apply:

    • The travel assistance was provided for business travel.
    • The travel assistance is provided for medical travel that was paid or reimbursed under the terms of a private health services plan, including payments you made because of an obligation under a collective agreement

    Taxable situation

    Unless the benefit or allowance for travel assistance was provided to your employee in one of the above situations, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

    You must report taxable travel assistance in your employee’s income in the year they receive it, no matter when your employee or members of their household travel.

    However, when you give your employees travel assistance benefits other than cash or refundable tickets (such as travel warrants, vouchers, or non-refundable tickets), the employees do not receive any benefit until they or members of their household take the trip. The benefit is income for the employees in the year the trip starts, and you should report it in that year.

    If you do not identify and report the amount of the taxable travel assistance which was for medical travel, the CRA will consider all of the travel assistance to be for vacation or other travel and your employee will not be entitled to claim a deduction for medical travel.

    • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations.

      Do not continue to the next step.

    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to: Step 4 - Calculate the value of the benefit .

  4. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

    Housing or utilities

    • With a developed rental market

      If the housing benefit is provided for a place in a prescribed zone with a developed rental market , use the following to calculate:

      Housing benefit
      • Fair market value (FMV)  of the rent
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Utilities benefit
      • Fair market value (FMV)  of the utilities
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer provided their employee with housing which has a fair market value of $4,000 for a place in a prescribed zone with a developed rental market. The accommodations were owned by the employer.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $4,000 is the FMV of the rent
      • minus $0 as the employee did not reimburse the employer
      • equals $4,000 is the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
    • Without a developed rental market - Housing you own

      If the housing benefit is provided for a place in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market for housing you own, use the following to calculate:

      Housing benefit
      • Lesser of the fair market value (FMV)  of the rent and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Utilities benefit
      • Lesser of the fair market value (FMV)  of the utilities and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer provided their employee with housing with a fair market value of $4,000 for a place in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market . The accommodations were owned by the employer. The ceiling value of the housing was $3,000.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $3,000 is the value of the housing because the ceiling amount was less than the FMV
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $3,000 the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
    • Without a developed rental market - Housing you rent from a third party

      If the housing benefit is provided for a place in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market for housing you rent from a third party, use the following to calculate:

      Housing benefit
      • Lesser of the amount you pay the third party for the rent and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Utilities benefit
      • Lesser of the amount you pay the third party for the utilities and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer paid a third party to provide their employee with housing with a fair market value of $4,000 for a place in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market. The accommodations were owned by the third party. The ceiling value of the housing was $3,000.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $3,000 is the value of the rent because the ceiling amount was less than the amount you pay the third party
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $3,000 is the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    Travel assistance

    • Value of the travel assistance
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of benefit for travel assistance to be included on the employee’s T4 slip using code 32 (and code 33 if part of the travel assistance is related to medical travel) and box 14
    Example - Calculation

    An employer provided their employee working in a prescribed zone with plane tickets for two distinct trips over the course of the tax year. One plane ticket, which had a FMV of $2,000, was for the employee to take a trip from the special work site to a nearby city to undergo a medical procedure which was not available in the area where they were working. The other plane ticket was provided by the employer to help the employee go on a family vacation. The FMV of the ticket was $850.

    • $2,850 ($2,000 + $850) is the total value of the travel assistance
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $2,850 is the value of benefit for travel assistance in a prescribed zone to be included on the employee’s T4 slip using code 32 and box 14, and $2,000 is the value of the benefit for medical travel assistance to be included using code 33

    If the allowance is taxable, the amount paid for the allowance is the value of the benefit.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  5. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions:

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, exception for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST in certain situations

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, exception for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone
      • CPP
      • EI

      Do not remit:

      • GST/HST (do not remit)
    Exception: Income tax withholding for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone

    For travel assistance payments in a prescribed zone (step 3), where your employee agrees in writing to use the payment entirely for vacation or medical travel, you may waive the requirement to withhold income tax depending on where they live:

    • If your employee lives in a prescribed northern zone, you may waive the full amount of the payment.
    • If your employee lives in a prescribed intermediate zone, you may waive 50% of the payment.

    Learn more: Places located in prescribed zones.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Learn how to calculate deductions and the GST/HST to remit: How to calculate - Calculate payroll deductions and contributions

  6. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on a T4 or T4A slip:

    • T4 slip - Employer

      • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 32 - Total travel assistance in a prescribed zone
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone
        • Code 40 - Other taxable allowances and benefits, including utilities

      • Cash: Option 2

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 32 - Total travel assistance in a prescribed zone
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone
        • Code 40 - Other taxable allowances and benefits, including utilities

    • T4A slip - Payer

      • Code 028 - Housing, utilities, travel assistance and other income
      • Code 116 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone
