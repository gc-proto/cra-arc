
<h3>Events that cause the employee benefit</h3>

<div id="ajax-taxable0">
<h4>Determine if the benefit is taxable</h4>
</div>
 
<div id="ajax-when0">
<hr>
<h4>Determine if you are a Canadian-controlled private corporation (CCPC) or not</h4>
<p>Depending if you are a <a href="#ajax-ccpc" aria-controls="ajax-ccpc" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">CCPC <span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> or not, will determine:</p>
 
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">The timing of when to include the benefit in your employee's income (step&nbsp2)</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Whether your employee is eligible to claim option benefit deductions (step&nbsp3)</li>
</ul>

	</header>	<div class="modal-body">	
	<div data-ajax-replace="https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/businesses/topics/corporations/type-corporation.html"></div>
	</div></section>


</div>

<details><summary>7(1)(a)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable1">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p id="event1">The benefit is <strong>always</strong> taxable to your employee if your employee chooses to <a href="#def-exercise" aria-controls="def-exercise" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">exercise <span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> their rights under a stock option agreement and acquires the shares under sections 7(1)(a) or 7(1.1).</p> 

<details><summary>When is an acquisition considered to take place</summary>
<p>An acquisition is considered to take place when:</p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Title to the shares passes to your employee </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">The employer has legal right to payment for the shares </li>
</ul>
<p>Generally, your employee notifies the employer that they wishes to exercise their options to acquire shares. This includes convertible preferred shares and short selling transactions.</p>
</details>

</div>

<div id="ajax-when1">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>year</strong> in which your employee:</p>

<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or">
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><h4 class="text-muted">CCPC</h4>
<p><strong>Disposes of the shares</strong> under section 7(1.1) </p>
</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><h4 class="text-muted">Non-CCPC or mutual fund trust</h4>
<p><strong>Acquires the shares</strong> under section 7(1)(a)</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</details>

<details><summary>7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable2">
<p id="event2"></p>
</div>
<div id="ajax-taxable2a">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable under sections under 5(1) or 6(1)(a) if all of the following apply: </p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>disposes of their rights </strong>under the security option agreement </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee has the <strong>option</strong> of receiving shares or cash </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><strong>You (as the employer)</strong> elects to cash out the rights </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>chooses</strong> to receive shares in lieu of a cash bonus already declared by the employer </li>
</ul>
</div>

<div id="ajax-taxable2b">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable under sections under 7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1) if all of the following apply: </p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>disposes of their rights </strong>under the security option agreement </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee has the <strong>option </strong>of receiving shares or cash </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your <strong>employee elects</strong> to cash out the rights </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>chooses </strong>to receive shares rather than a cash bonus before the bonus is declared by the employer</li>
</ul>

</div>
<div id="ajax-taxable2c">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable under sections under 7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1) if all of the following apply: </p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>disposes of their rights </strong>under the security option agreement </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>does not </strong>have the option to receiving shares or cash </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee has <strong>disposed </strong>of security option rights to a third party as part of a transaction that results in an ownership change in the corporation</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div id="ajax-taxable2d">
<p>The benefit from the transaction is <strong>not</strong> taxable under section 7(1.4) if your employee exchanges the option for a new option and <strong>all</strong> of the following apply:</p>

<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee exchanges their security options for new security options as a result of any type of corporate reorganization or capital restructuring within a <a href="#def-lenght" aria-controls="def-lenght" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">non-arm’s length&nbsp;<span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> corporate group </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee receives no consideration for the exchange other than the new option</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee does not derive an economic gain from the exchange </li>
</ul>
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable to your employee if transaction does not meet all of the above.</p>
<hr>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-ban text-danger fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>not</strong> taxable, you <strong>do not</strong> need to do any calculations. <strong>Do not</strong> continue to next step.</p></li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-arrow-down text-success fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>taxable</strong>, continue to: <a href="#when2">Determine when the benefit is taxable</a>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when2a">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income on the <strong>date the security option was cashed out</strong> under sections 5(1) or 6(1)(a).</p>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when2b">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the security option is <strong>cashed out</strong> under section 7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1).</p>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when2c">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the security option is <strong>disposed of the shares</strong> under sections 7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1).</p>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when2d">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the security option is <strong>disposed of the shares</strong> under sections 7(1)(b).</p>
</div>

</details>

<details><summary>7(1)(c)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable3">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p id="event3">The benefit is taxable under 7(1)(c) if your employee transfers their rights under a security options agreement to a <a href="#def-lenght" aria-controls="def-lenght" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">non-arm’s length&nbsp;<span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> person and that person exercises the option.</p>
</div>
<div id="ajax-when3">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in the income of your <strong>employee who initially received the option</strong> in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the security option is <strong>exercised</strong> under 7(1)(c).</p>
</div>
</details>

<details><summary>7(1)(d) and 7(1)(d.1)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable4">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p id="event4">The benefit is taxable if:</p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee has transferred security options to a <a href="#def-lenght" aria-controls="def-lenght" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">non-arm’s length&nbsp;<span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> person and that person (transferee) has disposed of the security options to an arm's length person under section 7(1)(d)</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">The rights of your employee under a security option agreement have, by one or more transactions between persons not dealing at arm's length, become vested in a non-arm's length person (transferee), and the transferee subsequently transfers or disposes of the rights to an employer with which the transferee does not deal at arm's length (or to a corporation or mutual fund trust not dealing at arm's length with such an employer) under section 7(1)(d.1)</li>
</ul>

</div>

<div id="ajax-when4">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in the income of your <strong>employee who initially received the option</strong> in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the security option is <strong> disposed of by the transferee</strong> under sections 7(1)(d) and 7(1)(d.1).</p>
</div>

</details>
 
<details><summary>7(1)(e)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable5">
<div id="ajax-taxable5a">
<h4 id="event5">Taxable situation is nil</h4>
<p>The value of options immediately <strong>after death is nil</strong> under section 7(1)(e) if the security option plan provides that the options <strong>expire your employee's death</strong>, even if the benefit is taxable. </p>
</div>
<div id="ajax-taxable5c">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable if your employee owns unexercised options under a security option agreement immediately before death and it <strong>does not</strong> expire upon death.</p>
</div>
<hr>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><div id="ajax-taxable5b"><p><span class="fas fa-ban text-danger fa-lg"></span> If the security option plan <strong>expires on an employee's death</strong>, you <strong>do not</strong> need to do any calculations. </div></p>
<p><strong>Do not</strong> continue to next step.</p></li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-arrow-down text-success fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>taxable</strong>, continue to: <a href="#when2">Determine when the benefit is taxable</a>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div id="ajax-when5">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in the income of your deceased employee in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which your <strong> employee died</strong> under section 7(1)(e).</p>
</div>

</details>


<details><summary>7(1.5)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable6">
<h4 id="event6">Non-taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit from the transaction is <strong>not</strong> taxable under section 7(1.5) if your employee exchanges the shares of a CCPC for new shares and <strong>all</strong> of the following apply:</p>

<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee exchanges their shares for new shares of the employer corporation, a corporation with which the employer corporation does not deal at <a href="#def-lenght" aria-controls="def-lenght" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">arm’s length&nbsp;<span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> immediately after the disposition or exchange of the old shares, the newly amalgamated or merged corporation, or a corporation with which the newly amalgamated or merged corporation does not deal at arm's length immediately after the amalgamation </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee receives no consideration for the exchange other than the new option  </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">The <a href="#def-fmv" aria-controls="def-fmv" class="overlay-lnk" role="button">fair market value <span class="far fa-question-circle"></span></a> of the new shares immediately after the disposition or exchange is not more than the fair market value of the old shares immediately before the disposition or exchange </li>
</ul>
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable to your employee if transaction does not meet all of the above.</p>
<hr>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-ban text-danger fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>not</strong> taxable, you <strong>do not</strong> need to do any calculations. <strong>Do not</strong> continue to next step.</p></li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-arrow-down text-success fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>taxable</strong>, continue to: <a href="#when2">Determine when the benefit is taxable</a>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when6">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the <strong>old shares are disposed of</strong> under section 7(1.1).</p>
</div>
</details>

<details><summary>7(1.7)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable7">
<div id="ajax-taxable7r1">
<h4 id="event7" class="mrgn-tp-lg">Non-taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is <strong>not</strong> taxable under section 7(1.7) if <strong>all</strong> of the following apply:</p>
<div id="ajax-taxable7a">
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee had rights to acquire shares under a security options agreement </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee is <strong>no longer able to exercise their rights</strong> under the security option agreement</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>did not </strong> receive consideration for the loss of the ability to exercise the option</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Your employee <strong>did not </strong> exchange their rights for rights under a new stock plan under sections 7(1)(b) or 7(1)(b.1)</li>
</ul></div></div>
<div id="ajax-taxable7b">
<details><summary>Example</summary>
<p>Examples of this situation includes merger where the previous corporation no longer exists for your employee to exercise the option and situations where the option is cancelled by the employer.</p>
</details></div>
<div id="ajax-taxable7r2">
<h4>Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable to your employee if your employee <strong>receives consideration </strong> for this loss, or does not meet all of the above.</p></div>
<hr>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><div id="ajax-taxable7r3"><p><span class="fas fa-ban text-danger fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>not</strong> taxable, you <strong>do not</strong> need to do any calculations. </p></div>
<p><strong>Do not</strong> continue to next step.</p></li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col"><p><span class="fas fa-arrow-down text-success fa-lg"></span> If the benefit is <strong>taxable</strong>, continue to: <a href="#when2">Determine when the benefit is taxable</a>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div id="ajax-when7">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income in the <strong>taxation year</strong> in which the <strong>rights to the option are disposed of by the transferee</strong> under section 7(1.7).</p>
</div>

</details>

<details><summary>5(1) and 6(1)(a)</summary>
<div id="ajax-taxable8">
<h4 id="event8" class="mrgn-tp-lg">Taxable situation</h4>
<p>The benefit is taxable under sections 5(1) and 6(1)(a) if one of the following apply:</p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">There is no stock option agreement or there is no intention in the agreement for the shares to be issued.</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">There is phantom stock plan or stock appreciation plan, this means it is an arrangement where your employee is compensated based on how your corporation shares performs on the open market. Your employee received cash on the share value increasing.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div id="ajax-when8">
<p>You must include the taxable benefit in your employee's income on the <strong>date that the benefit or the cash was received</strong> sections 5(1) and 6(1)(a).</p>
</div>
</details>
