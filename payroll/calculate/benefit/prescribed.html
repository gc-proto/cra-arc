Travel, board and lodging expenses - Provided to an employee in prescribed zones

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit for travel, board and loding expenses to your employee who works or lives in a location that is in a prescribed zone. Travel expenses include the cost of airplane, train or bus tickets, expenses related to the use of a vehicle, the cost of meals and lodging, and incidental expenses such as taxi fares, tolls and ferry charges. Housing can include a house, condominium, apartment or room. Utilities include electricity, natural gas, water, telephone and Internet.

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit is considered paid in a prescribed zone

    If you provide or pay for board, lodging, transportation, or travel assistance, the benefit is considered paid in a prescribed zone if your employee works or lives in locations that are in prescribed zones for purposes of the Northern residents deductions.

    • Do not continue to next step if the benefit is not considered paid in a prescribed zone
    • If the benefit is considered paid in a prescribed zone, continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable

  2. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    Generally, board, lodging, transportation or travel assistance you provide or pay to your employees are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Situations - Business travel assistance provided for travel away from the location of employment to your employee who works or lives in an area that is sufficiently northern such that it qualifies in a prescribed zone

    Situation: You provide or pay for travel assistance

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if the benefit is for travel assistance provided for travel away from the location of employment to your employee who works or lives in an area that is sufficiently northern such that it qualifies in a prescribed zone, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • Business travel assistance only
    • Provided in a prescribed zone
    • Form TD4 is completed

    You and your employee must fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site, to exclude board, lodging and transportation from your employee's income. You must keep a copy. The TD4 requires the exact location of the work site and the distance to your employee's principal place of residence.

    Taxable situation

    If the travel assistance you provide to your employee is:

    • For both business and personal purposes, the personal portion is taxable
    • Does not meet all of the conditions above, it is taxable

    Continue to Step 4 - Calculate the value of the benefit.

    Situation: You provide or pay for medical travel assistance

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if the benefit is for travel assistance provided for travel away from the location of employment to your employee who works or lives in an area that is sufficiently northern such that it qualifies in a prescribed zone, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • Medical travel assistance only
    • Provided in a prescribed zone
    • Paid or reimbursed under the terms of a private health services plan
    • Expenses eligible for the medical expense tax credit (METC)
    • Form TD4 is completed

    You and your employee must fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site, to exclude board, lodging and transportation from your employee's income. You must keep a copy. The TD4 requires the exact location of the work site and the distance to your employee's principal place of residence.

    Taxable situation

    If the travel assistance you provide to your employee is:

    • For both business and personal purposes, the personal portion is taxable
    • Does not meet all of the conditions above, it is taxable

    Continue to Step 4 - Calculate the value of the benefit.

    Situation: You provide or you pay for the cost of housing or utilities

    Taxable situation

    If you provide or you pay on behalf of your employee the cost for housing or utilities, it is a taxable benefit. However, your employee may be eligible to a reduction to the amount of the taxable benefit if the housing or utility benefit meets certain conditions.

    Continue to Step 3 - Determine if the housing or utilities are eligible for the reduction amount

  3. Determine if the housing or utilities are eligible for the reduction amount

    You need to determine if you can reduce the value of the benefit for the housing or utilities. The two factors that can reduce the value of the benefit applies in the following order:

    1. Suitability of size - You provide or pay for the cost of housing which is larger than the employee's needs. You can reduce the value of accommodation that is appropriate to the employee's needs.
    2. Loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment - You provide or pay for the cost of housing which contains elements that infringe on the employee's privacy or quite enjoyment of the accommodation. You can reduce the value of the accommodation that reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects the employee.

    Continue to Step 4 - Calculate the value of the benefit.

  4. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the travel, housing or ustility expenses
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 3)
    • minus Amount of the travel assistance that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals Value of the travel assistance that is taxable
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Amount of the travel assistance that is taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in boxes 14 and 32)
    • multiply by GST/HST calculation (if applicable)
    • equals Amount of GST/HST to remit
    Example 1 - Travel assistance calculations

    Employer paid an employee $2,000 for them to take a trip from the special work site to their principal place of residence and back in Ontario. Employer paid an employee $850 to assist them to make a personal shopping trip from the special work site in a prescribed zone to a different nearby community on a different trip. The special work site was located 27 kilometres from a town with a population of 43,000 people. Employer and employee completed Form TD4, Declaration of Exemption – Employment at a Special Work Site

    • $2,850 ($2,000 + $850) is the total value of the travel assistance
    • minus $2,000 is the amount of the travel assistance that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals $850 is the value of the travel assistance that is taxable
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $850 is the amount of the travel assistance that is taxable to be included the employee's T4 slip (box 14 and box 32)
    • multiply by 12/112 is the HST calculation in Ontario
    • equals $97.79 is the amount of HST to remit

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 2 - Housing and utilities calculations

    An employer provides an employee with board and lodging in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market with a fair market value of $4,000. The accommodations were owned by the employer. The ceiling value of the board and lodging was $3,000.

    • $4,000 is the total value of the board and lodging
    • minus $1,000 is the amount for the difference between the fair market value and the cleiling amount that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals $3,000 is the value of the board and lodging that is taxable
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $3,000 is the amount of the board and lodging that is taxable to be included the employee's T4 slip (box 14 and box 32)
    • multiply by 12/112 is the HST calculation in Ontario
    • equals $345.13 is the amount of HST to remit

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  5. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI

      Remit:

      • GST/HST - Reimbursements
      • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

  6. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report on a:

    • T4 slip if you are an employer, continue to Step 2a
    • T4A slip if you are third party who supply travel benefits to the employer of another employer, continue to Step 2b

    1. T4 slip - You are an employer

      • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment Income
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Other Information, housing
        • Code 31 - Special work site (exempt portion of the housing)
        • Code 32 - Travel in a prescribed zone (total amount of benefit)
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
        • Code 40 - Other Information, utilities

      • Cash: Option 2

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment Income
        • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Other Information, housing
        • Code 31 - Special work site (exempt portion of the housing)
        • Code 32 - Travel in a prescribed zone (total amount of benefit)
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
        • Code 40 - Other Information, utilities

    2. T4A slip - You are third party who supply travel benefits to the employer of another employer

      Report on:

      • Code 28 - Other income (total amount of travel benefit)
      • Code 116 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
      • Code 124 - Board and lodging at special work sites (exempt portion)
