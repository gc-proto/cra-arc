Determine if a benefit is taxable

Generally, fees for financial counselling services or income tax preparation you provide or pay on behalf of your employee are taxable. Depending on the situation, the benefit from certain counselling services may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act (ITA).

Situations

Situation: You pay for counselling services related to the mental or physical health of your employee or a person related to your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, certain benefits for counselling services in respect of the physical or mental health of your employee or a person related to the employee are not taxable, this includes:

  • Dealing with addictions, such as tobacco, drug and alcohol abuse
  • Stress management

Counselling services of this nature may be provided directly by you or through an employee assistance program.

Taxable situation

If amounts are paid for the use of a recreational facility or club, including situations where that membership provides an employee or a related person with access to counselling services, the benefit is taxable.

Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • You decide to pay for counselling on behalf of any staff member who would like to stop smoking. Your employee decides to take the employer-paid counselling. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • You offer a range of counselling services to your employees. Your employees may book sessions with a range of service providers. An employee hires a personal trainer for ten sessions. You reimburse the employee for half the cost. The benefit is taxable in this situation.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Situation: You pay for the cost of counselling services related to the re-employment of your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, certain benefits for counselling services in respect of the employee's re-employment are not taxable, this includes:

  • Re-employment with you
  • You are the primary beneficiary of the benefit

Taxable situation

If the fees paid for counselling services are for employment with another employer or include a financial counselling component, the benefit is taxable.

For example, retraining of an employee in the event of termination where it is not primarily for the benefit of the employer is a taxable benefit.

Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • A downsizing company is removing several of its order fulfillment staff. Employees are being offered other positions with the company, but must first complete appropriate training programs. An employee is offered a sales position, but first must complete a 3-week program. The full cost is paid by the employer. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • Included in a severance package offered to a long term employee is an offer to cover costs of retraining for prospective employment given that the employee is no longer employed due to downsizing. The re-training is not for the employer's benefit. The benefit is taxable in this situation.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Situation: You pay for the cost of counselling services related to the retirement of your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, certain benefits for counselling services in respect of your employee's retirement are not taxable, this includes:

  • Income and net worth analysis
  • Post-retirement expectations
  • Tax planning for investments
  • Estate planning
  • Course on basic financial planning

Taxable situation

If the fees paid for retirement counselling services include financial counselling on specific investments or tax preparation, the benefit is taxable.

Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • A company is downsizing due to declining sales. It is offering a retirement package to an employee who has been employed with the company as a salesperson for 25 years. The retirement package includes a one-day counselling seminar to prepare for retirement. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • A company offers all employees with at least 35 years of service discounted counselling upon retirement. The counselling is mainly related to investment and financial planning. A retired employee receives an amount for counselling in the year, but is required to pay a portion for the service. The benefit is taxable in this situation.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Situation: You pay for services related to financial counselling or income tax preparation

If you pay for services related to financial counselling or income tax preparation, the benefit is taxable, this includes:

  • Income tax preparation for personal tax returns or personal tax planning
  • Services through your accounting firm, or your employee or shareholder is reimbursed for the expense
  • Services for the preparation of out-of-country tax returns for an employee or shareholder

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Calculate the value of the benefit

If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

  • Counselling services fees paid by the employer that are taxable
  • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
  • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
  • multiply by GST/HST calculation (if applicable)
  • equals Amount of GST/HST to remit
Example 1 - Calculations

An employer has a contract with a service that provides employee counselling. Employees may use the service for issues related to their physical and mental health. Employees schedule appointments directly with the counsellor, but the employer is billed for the service. An employee, who has been dealing with stressful events in their life, books 6 appointments in the year. The employer is billed $120 for each appointment for a total of $720.

  • $0 because the counselling services paid by the employer is not taxable
  • minus Not applicable
  • equals Not applicable
  • multiply by Not applicable
  • equals Not applicable

The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

Example 2 - Calculations

An employer going through restructuring will be redeploying the employees with the same employer in the new company and terminating the employment of others. In all cases, the employer provides employment counselling services. An employee, who has been terminated, takes advantage of the services while looking for a new job. The employer is billed $800 for the services.

  • $800 is the counselling services fees paid by the employer
  • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
  • equals $800 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
  • multiply by 12/112 is the HST calculation in Ontario
  • equals $85.71 is the amount of HST to remit

The amounts must be included in the terminated employees pay period they were received or enjoyed.

Example 3 - Calculations

An employer offers counselling for retirees. Employees may separately schedule appointments for lifestyle counselling, financial counselling and tax preparation, which are billed directly to the employer. A retiring employee uses the lifestyle counselling for individuals transitioning to retirement, the cost is $400 for 2 sessions. He also uses financial and investment planning for $900 over the course of the year as well as a tax preparation of $300.

  • $1,600 ($400 + $900 + $300) is the counselling services fees paid by the employer
  • minus $400 is the amount for retirement counselling services that are not taxable
  • equals $1,200 ($900 + $300) is the value of the taxable benefit
  • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
  • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
  • multiply by 12/112 is the HST calculation in Ontario
  • equals $128.57 is the amount of HST to remit

The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI (do not withhold)

    Remit:

    • GST/HST

  • Cash: Option 2

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI

    Remit:

    • GST/HST - Reimbursements
    • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

Report the benefit on a slip

If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information

  • Cash: Option 2

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information
