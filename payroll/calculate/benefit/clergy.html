Housing and utilities - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order

You may be looking for:

  • Travel expenses - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you provide free or low-rent accommodation or utilities or an allowance  for housing or utilities to your employee in connection with or in the course of their employment duties, the benefit or allowance is taxable. This includes situations where you provide your employee with housing rent-free or for less than the fair market value (FMV) of the accommodation

What is the FMV

Depending on the situation, your employee who is a member of the clergy may be able to claim a deduction from their income for their residence when filing a personal income tax return. If they choose to claim this deduction, you may have to reduce the income used to calculate CPP and income tax withholdings by the amount of the deduction.

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit or allowance you provide to your employee is considered to be for housing and utilities or board and lodging

    You need to determine if the benefit or allowance you provide to your employee is considered to be for housing and utilities or board and lodging. For example:

    • If the benefit or allowance is board and lodging or other benefits that are not housing and utilities, do not continue to the next step. The same tax treatment that applies to most employees also applies to them.

      Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable

    • If the benefit or allowance is housing and utilities, continue to Step 2 - Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance.

  2. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance

    The benefit or allowance for housing or utilities you provide to your employee may not be taxable if the benefit or allowance is in one of the following situations:

    • Your employee is working at a remote location
    • Your employee works at a special work site, including a work site located in a prescribed zone
    • Your employee is a member of a religious order who has taken a vow of perpetual poverty

    • If the benefit or allowance is in one of the above situations, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more on the above exceptions using the links.

    • If the benefit or allowance is not in one of the above situations, continue to Step 3 - Determine if your employee is considered a member of the clergy.

  3. Determine if your employee is considered a member of the clergy

    Your employee is considered a member of the clergy for the purposes of the clergy residence deduction if they meet both the status and the function tests:

    • Status test

      Your employee’s status is one of the following:

      • Member of the clergy
      • Member of a religious order
      • Regular minister of a religious denomination

    • Function test

      Your employee’s function is one of the following:

      • Ministering to a diocese, parish or congregation
      • Engaged exclusively in full-time administrative service by appointment of a religious order or religious denomination
    Definitions
    Congregation
    A "congregation" is not defined by any particular church structure, by territorial boundaries nor by the number of people gathered in one place. It is an assemblage or gathering of persons to whom a minister provides spiritual counseling, advice, illumination and inspiration. A group of students assembled for academic instruction is not a congregation. Persons who meet the status test do not need to be in charge of a single, fixed congregation. They can serve multiple congregations. Congregations can be of a diverse and fluid makeup and require neither voluntary attendance nor homogeneity of religious belief. Chaplains in hospitals, jails, the armed forces and other such organizations are generally considered to minister to congregations.
    Member of the clergy
    A "member of the clergy" is a person set apart from the other members of the church or denomination as a spiritual leader. It is not necessary that the process of appointment be referred to as ordination or that the appointment is by someone higher up in the ecclesiastical hierarchy; it may be done by the congregation itself. It is sufficient that there be a formal or legitimate act of recognition, and it requires a serious and long-term commitment to the ministry. Priests, pastors, ministers, rabbis, imams, commended workers and other persons who have been commended, licensed, commissioned or otherwise formally or legitimately recognized for religious leadership within their religious organization may be members of the clergy.
    Regular minister

    A "regular minister" is a person who:

    • Is authorized or empowered to perform spiritual duties, conduct religious services, administer sacraments and carry out similar religious functions. Religious functions may include participation in the conduct of religious services, the administration of some of the rituals, ordinances or sacraments, and pastoral responsibilities to specific segments of the religious organization;
    • Is appointed or recognized by a body or person with the legitimate authority to appoint or ordain ministers on behalf of or within the denomination; and
    • Is in a position or appointment of some permanence.

    In the absence of a legitimate appointment or recognition, the mere performance of the duties of a minister will not suffice to constitute a "regular minister".

    Religious denomination
    A "religious denomination" is a group of individuals that is called or identified by the same name and that has a particular system of religious faith or belief. The set of religious beliefs or ideology or practices of the members of a religious denomination will be sufficiently distinct to distinguish the members from society as a whole.
    Religious order
    The term "religious order" means a group of people bound by the same religious, moral and social regulations and discipline. A religious order may comprise all of the members of an organization or only an identifiable group within that organization. A religious order will have an ascertainable faith with tenets and principles and a requirement for their adherence, and its members will be involved in carrying out the religious mission or ministry of the order. This may include evangelism, religious missions, religious education, social outreach, counseling or relief and development. Not every religious organization is a religious order. Whether or not an organization or a group of individuals within an organization is a religious order is a question of fact to be determined on a case-by-case basis.

    • If your employee does not meet both tests, your employee is not considered a member of the clergy or a religious order. Do not continue to the next step.

      Refer to the information for most employees: Housing and utilities.

    • If your employee meets both tests, your employee is considered a member of the clergy or a religious order. Your employee may be eligible for the clergy residence deduction. For your employee to claim the deduction, you and your employee must fill out Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction.

      Continue to: Step 4 - Determine if the benefit or allowance is taxable.

  4. Determine if the benefit or allowance is taxable

    • If the benefit or allowance is not for housing benefits, continue to: Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit.
    • If the benefit is for housing benefits, continue to: Step 5 - Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit.

  5. Determine if you can apply the clergy residence deduction

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 3, your employee may be eligible for the clergy residence deduction. Your employee must confirm that they are claiming the clergy residence deduction before you can apply the reduction.

    • Free or low-rent housing or utilities

      If you are providing free or low-rent housing or utilities to your employee, do not include the housing or utilities that are equal to the clergy residence deduction in your employee’s income when you calculate the employee’s CPP contributions and income tax deductions if they do both of the following:

      • Your employee completes parts A and C of Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, and requests that you complete part B to confirm that they meet the required conditions.
      • Your employee tells you in writing that they intend to claim the deduction, including the amount of the deduction that will be claimed.

      You can reduce the income used to calculate the CPP contributions and income tax by the amount indicated on line 11 of the Form or the amount indicated on the letter.

    • Employee owns or rents their residence

      If your employee owns or rents their residence and they pay for utilities out of their own money or you provide an allowance for housing or utilities, do not include an amount equal to the clergy residence deduction in your employee’s income when you calculate the employee’s CPP contributions and income tax deductions if they do all of the following:

      • Your employee completes parts A and C of Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, and requests that you complete part B to confirm that they meet the required conditions.
      • Your employee completes Form T1213 Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source
      • Your employee sends Forms T1213 and T1223 to the CRA at the address provided on Form T1213
      • Your employee provides you with the CRA letter of authority they received in response.

      You can only reduce the income used to calculate the CPP contributions and income tax by the amount indicated on the letter of authority.

    • If your employee confirms that they are claiming the clergy residence deduction, you can apply the clergy residence reduction when calculating their CPP contributions and income tax deductions.

      You must still report the housing or utilities benefit or allowance on the T4 slip using box 14 and any applicable codes even if you can reduce your employee’s income by the amount of the clergy residence deduction for the purpose of calculating tax deductions and CPP contributions.

      The deduction cannot be more than the amount of the employee's income for the year from the qualifying office or employment.

      Continue to: Step 7 - Report the benefit or allowance on a slip.

    • If your employee does not confirm that they are claiming the clergy residence deduction, do not apply the reduction when calculating their CPP contributions and income tax deductions.

      Continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

  6. Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit

    • If the benefit or allowance is excluded from your employee’s income, you must report the excluded benefit or allowance on a slip.

      You and your employee must fill out Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction.

      Continue to: Step 10 - Report the benefit or allowance on a slip.

    • If the benefit or allowance must be included in your employee’s income, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

      If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA, only the value over that is more than a reasonable amount for housing or utilities must be included in the employee’s income.

      Continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

  7. Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance

    If the benefit or allowance is taxable, the value of the benefit or allowance is equal to:

    Calculate the housing benefit

    • FMV  of the housing (do not include GST/HST)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 5a)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 5b)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals This is the total value of the housing benefit to be included on the T4 slip in code 30 and in box 14

    Calculate the utilities benefit

    • Amount for the utilities
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals This is the total value of the utilities to be included on the T4 slip in code 30 (if eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction) or code 40 (if not eligible) and in box 14

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 1 - Calculations

    A congregation paid its minister an annual salary of $35,000 and provided a rent-free residence valued at $18,000 annually. In addition, the congregation paid for eligible utilities valued at $1,500 annually. The minister will be claiming the clergy residence deduction and has given his employer a letter to that affect.

    Calculate the housing benefit

    • $18,000 is the FMV of the housing (do not include GST/HST)
    • minus $0 because the minister does not reimburse his employer for the rent
    • equals $18,000 is the total value of the housing benefit to be included on the T4 slip in code 30 and in box 14

    Calculate the utilities benefit

    • $1,500 is the amount for the utilities
    • minus $0 because the minister does not reimburse his employer for the utilities
    • equals $1,500 is the total value of the utilities to be included on the T4 slip in code 30 (if eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction) or code 40 (if not eligible) and in box 14
    Example 2 - Calculations

    A congregation paid its minister a salary together with a monthly cash allowance of $600 for his residence ($7,200 per year). The minister did not advise his employer that he was planning to take the deduction.

    • equals $7,200 is the total value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14

  8. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    You must withhold the following deductions:

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold unless cash earnings were also paid in the pay period)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST in certain situations

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI

      Do not remit:

      • GST/HST (do not remit)

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 1

    In 2024, a congregation paid its minister an annual salary of $35,000 and provided a rent-free residence valued at $18,000 annually. In addition, the congregation paid for utilities valued at $1,500 annually. The minister completed and provided their employer with Form 1223, will be claiming the clergy residence deduction, and has advised their employer in writing.

    As the minister is claiming the clergy residence deduction, the employer will not include the amount of the rent-free housing benefit or the utilities benefit in the income from which they withhold income tax deductions or CPP contributions.

    They will still withhold income tax and CPP contributions from the minister’s regular salary.

    The employer will withhold EI premiums on account of the non-cash housing benefit as it was received alongside cash earnings. They will not withhold EI premiums on account of the utilities benefit as it is considered non-cash.

    The employer will withhold payroll deductions on the following income as follows:

    • $35,000 is subject to income tax deductions ($35,000 salary)
    • $53,000 is subject to EI premiums ($35,000 salary + $18,000 housing benefit)
    • $35,000 is subject to CPP contributions ($35,000 salary)
    Example 2

    In 2024, a congregation paid its minister an annual salary of $35,000 and provided a housing allowance of $18,000 annually, paid monthly. The minister completed and provided their employer with Form 1223, completed Form T1213 and sent the two forms to the CRA. They received a letter of authority, authorizing them to reduce their taxable income by $17,000.

    As the minister is claiming the clergy residence deduction, the employer will not include the authorized reduction in the income from which they withhold income tax deductions or CPP contributions. They will still withhold income tax and CPP contributions from all of the minister’s income after subtracting the authorized reduction.

    The employer will still withhold EI premiums on account of the housing allowance as it was received in cash.

    The employer will withhold payroll deductions on the following income as follows:

    • $36,000 is subject to income tax deductions (($35,000 salary + $18,000 housing allowance) - $17,000 authorized reduction)
    • $53,000 is subject to EI premiums ($35,000 salary + $18,000 housing allowance)
    • $36,000 is subject to CPP contributions (($35,000 salary + $18,000 housing allowance) - $17,000 authorized reduction)
    Example 3

    In 2024, a congregation paid its minister an annual salary of $35,000 and did not provide any housing or utilities benefits or allowances. The minister completed and provided their employer with Form 1223, completed Form T1213 and sent the two forms to the CRA. They received a letter of authority, authorizing them to reduce their taxable income by $10,000.

    As the minister is claiming the clergy residence deduction, the employer will not include the authorized reduction in the income from which they withhold income tax deductions or CPP contributions. They will still withhold income tax and CPP contributions from all of the minister’s income after subtracting the authorized reduction.

    The employer will withhold payroll deductions on the following income as follows:

    • $25,000 is subject to income tax deductions ($35,000 salary - $10,000 authorized reduction)
    • $35,000 is subject to EI premiums ($35,000 salary)
    • $25,000 is subject to CPP contributions ($35,000 salary - $10,000 authorized reduction)

    Learn how to calculate deductions and the GST/HST to remit: How to calculate - Calculate payroll deductions and contributions

  9. Report the benefit or allowance on a T4 slip

    If the benefit or allowance is taxable, you must report the following on a T4 slip:

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Housing and utilities
      • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities (when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction)

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Housing and utilities
      • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities (when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction)

    Learn how to report the benefit or allowance: T4 slip - Information for employers.
