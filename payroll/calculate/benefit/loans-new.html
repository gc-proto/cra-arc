Determine if a benefit is taxable

Generally, if you provide a loan and it is considered received because of employment or considered received because of shareholdings, the interest benefit is taxable to the employee.

Situation: Loans received because of employment (previous, current or future) New CRA administrative policy

A loan is considered received because of employment if it is reasonable to conclude that the loan would not have been received, or the terms of the loan would have been different, had there been no current, previous, or intended employment.

Non-taxable situation

Under the CRA's administrative policy, if the loan is considered received because of employment, the interest benefit is not taxable if one of the following apply:

  • Condition 1

    Total amount of the loans received in the year are equal to or less than $10,000 per year and the term of the loan is equal to or less than 2 months

  • Condition 2

    Total amount of the loan benefit is equal to or less than $50 per year

Taxable situation

If the loan benefit does not meet either condition, the full amount of the interest benefit is taxable.

To calculate the benefit, refer to: Calculate the value of the benefit

Situation: Loans received because of shareholding New CRA administrative policy

If the loans were received or debts were incurred because of shareholdings, the benefit is taxable if all 3 of the following apply:

  1. The loan is received, or the debt is incurred, by a person or partnership (except when the person is a corporation resident in Canada or the partnership is one in which each partner is a corporation resident in Canada)
  2. The person or partnership is one of the following:
    • a shareholder of a corporation
    • connected with a shareholder of a corporation
    • a member of a partnership or beneficiary of a trust that was a shareholder of a corporation
  3. Because of these shareholdings, the person or partnership receives a loan from, or incurs a debt to one of the following:

    • the corporation

    • a corporation related to that corporation

    • a partnership of which the corporation or the related corporation was a member

A person may be an individual, a corporation, or a trust.

Non-taxable situation

Under the CRA's administrative policy, if the loan considered received because of shareholdings, the interest benefit is not taxable if one of the following apply:

  • Condition 1

    Total amount of the loans received in the year are equal to or less than $10,000 per year and the term of the loan is equal to or less than 2 months

  • Condition 2

    Total amount of the loan benefit is equal to or less than $50 per year

Taxable situation

If the loan benefit does not meet either condition, the full amount of the interest benefit is taxable.

To calculate the benefit, refer to: Calculate the value of the benefit

Forgiven loans you provide to your employees are always taxable.

Situation: Loans or debts that are forgiven

In general

Forgivable loans are types of loans that allow borrowers to have the balance of the loan partially or totally forgiven if they meet certain conditions.

The CRA's administrative policy does not apply if you forgive your employee's loan or debt. The amount forgiven is always taxable.

Specific to shareholdings

Forgivable loans received because of shareholdings and a subsequent forgiveness is not considered to be employment income even though the shareholder is also an employee at the time it was forgiven. Instead the forgiven amount is considered as income received by the person in their capacity as a shareholder.

Forgiven shareholder loans are taxable as income to the shareholder. Where a person is both a shareholder and an employee, it would be a question of fact whether the indebtedness arose as a result of the person's shareholdings or as a result of the office or employment.

Specific to pursuing education or research

If you provide an employee with a forgivable loan to enable them to pursue education or research on condition that they return to employment with you, the income is not to be included in their taxable income at the time they receive it. When the loan (or part of the loan) is later forgiven for the reason that the employment conditions were met, the forgiven part would be included as employment income in the year it was forgiven.

Forgivable loans do not include scholarships, fellowships, bursaries or research grants.

For the forgivable loan policy to apply, the amount must be a bone fide loan, repayable if certain conditions are not met.

Refer to: Scholarships, bursaries, tuition, and training

To calculate the benefit, refer to: Calculate the value of the benefit

Depending on your situation, the interest benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy.

Calculate the value of the benefit

If the benefit is taxable, the calculation of the benefit will depend on type of loan or debt received. You must use the correct prescribed interest rates for each quarter. There are multiple different rates for each quarter.

Calculation: Loans because of employment (previous, current, or future)

Once you have determined the benefit is considered received because of employment , the interest benefit is calculated in the tax year as follows:

Total of the following two amounts:

  • interest calculated at the prescribed interest rates for each quarter during the period in the year that the loan was outstanding
  • +plus total of all interest paid or payable in the year on the loan by the employer (or intended employer) and any other person (other than the debtor) related to the employer
  • -minus total of all interest paid for the year (by any person) on the loan not later than 30 days after the end of the year
  • -minus any portion of the interest referred to in (2) which is reimbursed in the year or within 30 days after the end of the year by the debtor to the person or entity that paid it
  • =eqauls Value of the benefit

When to include the benefit on the T4 or T4A slip

The loan does not need to be outstanding at the borrower's tax year-end for a benefit to be calculated.

If the employee makes a payment during the period

If the employee makes a payment during the period then two separate calculations are required for the period. Each calculation is based on the declining balance of the loan as payments are made.

If the loan is partially or fully forgiven

If the loan is partially or fully forgiven, the amount forgiven is employment income in the hands of the employee in the year the loan is forgiven. There may also be an interest benefit in prior years and up to the date the loan is forgiven.

If the loan is considered a repayable award

If the loan is considered a repayable award, the amount is employment income and must be included on the slip in the year received.

What to do with combined loans with low-interest and higher prescribed interest rates

If you provide two loans to your employee, one at low interest and the other at a rate higher than the prescribed interest rate, combine the loans to calculate the benefit.

Calculation: Home purchase loans or home relocation loans
Home purchase loan
A home-purchase loan is any part of a loan to an employee that the employee used to get or repay another loan to buy a residence. The residence has to be for that employee or a person related to that employee. This also applies to a shareholder or a person related to a shareholder.
Home relocation loan

Only a debt that is a loan can be a home relocation loan. A home-relocation loan is a loan you give to an employee or an employee's spouse or common-law partner when they meet all of the following conditions:

  • Your employee or your employee's spouse or common-law partner moves to start work at a new location in Canada
  • Your employee or your employee's spouse or common-law partner uses the loan to buy a new residence that is at least 40 kilometres closer to the new work location than the previous home
  • Your employee or your employee's spouse or common-law partner receives the loan because of the employee's employment
  • Your employee designates the loan as a home-relocation loan
  • The loan is used to acquire a residence or a share of the capital stock of a co-operative housing corporation acquired only to obtain the right to inhabit a residence owned by the corporation. The residence must be for the habitation of the employee and be their new residence

Once you determined the benefit is considered a home purchase loan or a home relocation loan, the interest benefit is calculated in the same manner as loans received because of employment (previous, current, or future)  with the following exceptions:

  • Exception: The amount of interest calculated as a taxable benefit should not be more than the interest that would have been charged at the prescribed rate in effect when the employee made the loan or incurred the debt
  • Exception: If it is a home purchase loan, the prescribed rate is set as a ceiling at the time the loan is granted, but may decrease if the prevailing prescribed rate falls
  • Exception: If the term of repayment for the loan is more than five years, the balance owing at the end of five years (from the day the loan was made) is considered a new loan. Treat the outstanding balance as a new loan on that date.
Examples: Home purchase loans or home relocation loans
  • January 1, 20X1, an employee borrows $100,000 from their employer.

Calculation: Loans because of shareholdings

Once you have determined the benefit is considered received because of shareholdings , the interest benefit is calculated in the same manner as loans received because of employment , except if the benefit is a loan that is considered to have been made under a back-to-back shareholder loan arrangement.

Learn more on back-to-back shareholder loans: Tax Measures: Supplementary Information

Refer to: Calculation: Loans because of employment (previous, current, or future).

Calculation: Forgiven loan or debt

Once you have determined the benefit is forgiven loan or debt , the amount forgiven must be included on slip in the tax year in which the amount is forgiven.

The amount of the interest benefit must also be calculated.

Refer to: Calculation: Loans because of employment (previous, current, or future).

Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  • Non-cash and near-cash (interest benefits): Option 1

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI (do not withhold)

    Remit:

    • GST/HST

  • Cash (forgiven loans and repayable awards): Option 2

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI

    Remit:

    • GST/HST - Reimbursements
    • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

Report the benefit on a slip

If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

  • T4 - Employee: Option 1

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 36 - Interest-free and low interest loans in Other Information (interest benefit)
    • Code 40 - Other Information (benefit not reimbursed for forgiven/repayable awards)

  • T4A - Person or partnership that was a shareholder: Option 2

    Report on:

    • Code 117 - Loan benefits in Other Information
