Medical expenses

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may pay directly or reimburse your employee for medical examinations or medical expenses.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit

    The benefit may not be taxable if the benefit is in one of the following situations:

    • Your employee has a severe or prolonged impairement
    • The benefit is for counselling services expenses

    • If the benefit is in one of the above situations, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more on the above exceptions using the links.

    • If the benefit is not in one of the above situations, continue to Step 2 - Determine if a benefit is taxable.

  2. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    Generally, if you pay directly or reimburse your employee for medical examinations and medical expenses, these amounts are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable.

    Non-taxable situation

    The benefit is not taxable if one of the following applies:

    • Medical examination

      You pay directly or reimburse your employee for medical examination and all of the following apply:

      • You requested your employee to take the medical examination (for example: to meet a condition of employment)
      • You can clearly demonstrate that you are the primary beneficiary of the benefit
      Who is considered the primary beneficiary of the benefit

      You are not considered to be the primary beneficiary if the medical expense provides only an indirect benefit to you.

      Example

      An employer reimburses an employee for a medical examination required by company policy As the reimbursement by the employer of theamount paid by the employee for the exam is primarily for the benefit of the employer, the amount would not be taxable.

    • Medical expenses

      You pay or reimburse the cost of medical expenses to your employee and they were paid under a private health services plan (PHSP)

      Examples of PHSP

    Taxable situation

    If the benefit does not meet one of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

    • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations.

      Do not continue to the next step.

    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to: Step 3 - Calculate the value of the benefit.

  3. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

    • Value of the benefit received or enjoyed
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip in code 40 and in box 14
    Example - Calculations

    An employer reimbursed their employee $300 throughout the year for prescription drugs but the amount was not paid under a private health services plan. Since it was not paid under the terms of a PHSC, the amount would be a taxable benefit.

    • $300 is the value of the benefit received or enjoyed
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $300 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip in code 40 and in box 14

  4. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  5. Report the benefit on a slip
