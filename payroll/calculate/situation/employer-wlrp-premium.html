You may provide XXX payments to your employee for salary, wages or commissions that they are expected to earn in their employment with you (for example, to perform future services). Generally, you make payroll deductions on XXX payments.

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip

    Situation: You pay an amount to your employee for 5/12 of any savings in the year in which you received the EI premium reduction

    If you pay an amount to your employee for 5/12 of any savings in the year in which you received the EI premium reduction (or within the first 4 months of the following year), the benefit is not taxable if it is considered non-cash or near-cash (exempt from taxation 6(1)(a)).

    For example, the benefit is provided indirectly through increased employer contributions to an employee's health and welfare trust, group sickness or accident insurance plan, private health services plan, or in any other manner.

    Taxable situation

    If you pay an amount to your employee in cash, such as a cash allowance or a cash rebate, the benefit is taxable.

    Situation: You pay for a top-up disability payment (as per subsection 6(17))

    Determine if the top-up disability payment you, as an employer, make is to the insurer or directly to the employee.

    Situation: You paid directly the insurer

    Non-taxable situation

    If you make a top-up disability payment, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • Insurer is making periodic payments under a disability plan to your employee that became insolvent
    • You make payments to the obligated insurer, either the insolvent insurer or another insurer that has assumed the obligations of the insolvent insurer
    • The obligated insurer continues periodic payments, in whole or in part, to your employee under the policy

    These are payments you makee direclty to the insurer and not to your employee. In this situation, this is not considered to be a benefit received or enjoyed by your employee.

    Taxable situation

    If the payments you make does not meet all of the above conditions, the benefit is taxable.

    Situation: You paid directly your employee

    Non-taxable situation

    If you make a top-up disability payment, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • Disability insurance plan was funded only by your employee contributions
    • Insurer is making periodic payments under a disability plan to your employee that became insolvent
    • You make payments directly to you employee
    • The payments replace, in whole or in part, the periodic payments that would have been made under the applicable insurance policy, if not for the insurer's insolvency
    • Under the arrangement, your employee reimburses you if they subsequently receive an amount from an insurer in respect of those same periodic payments

    Generally, these payments are considered an amount payable to your employee based on the relevant disability insurance plan and it is funded only by your employee contributions. In this situation, this is not considered to be a benefit received or enjoyed by your employee.

    Taxable situation

    If the payments you make does not meet all of the above conditions, the benefit is taxable.
