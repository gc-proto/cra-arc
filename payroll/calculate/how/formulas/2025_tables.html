This appendix provides example scenarios that illustrate the CPP2 contribution formulas.

Scenario 1

An employee is earning $4,000 per pay period. There are 24 pay periods in the year since this employee is getting paid semi-monthly.

C, C2 and F5 calculations for the 18th pay period are as follows:

PI                =             $4,000.00

PIYTD             =              $68,000.00

PM             =              12

P                =              24 

D               =               $3,898.44*   

D2             =               $0*

*See Table 1 for CPP contributions and year-to-date contributions for each pay period.

C  = The lesser of:

       (i) $4,034.10 × (PM/12) – D

       = $4,034.10 × (12/12) – $3,898.44

       = $135.66

 

(ii) 0.0595 × [PI – ($3,500/P)]

        = 0.0595 × [$4,000.00 – ($3,500.00/24)]

        = $229.32

 

C2 = The lesser of:

        (i) $396.00 × (PM/12) – D2

        = $396.00 × (12/12) – 0

        = $396.00

 

(ii) (PIYTD + PI – W) × 0.04

        = ($68,000.00 + $4,000.00 – $71,300.00) × 0.04

        = $28.00

 

W = The greater of:

         (i) PIYTD;

        = $68,000.00

 

 (ii) YMPE × (PM/12)

        = $71,300 × (12/12)

        = $71,300

 

F5 = C × (0.01/0.0595) + C2

     = $135.66 x (0.01/0.0595) + $28.00

     = $50.80
