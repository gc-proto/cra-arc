Payments to the CRA

Confirm a payment – Payments to the CRA

You can confirm that you have made or received a payment, or that you have a balance owing to the CRA. Some payments you make will not appear in your CRA account right away.

On this page

  • Check your CRA account
  • Transfer payments or balances
  • Resolve an issue with a payment
  • Make sure it's really the CRA contacting you

[Step 1: ]Allow time for your payment to be processed

Before checking to see if we have received a payment you sent, allow:

  • 3 business days for online payments
  • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque or money order

[Step 2: ]Check your CRA account

You can sign in to your online account to confirm CRA account payments and balances.

  • Individuals

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your “Individual” account
    3. Select “Accounts and payments” to review your account balance, available payments, and instalment payments

    If you cannot sign into your individual account, you can check your balance by [calling our automated service for individuals - Pop-up]

  • Businesses

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your "Business" account
    3. Select "View and pay account balance"

    If you cannot sign into your business account, you can check your balance by [calling our service representatives for businesses - Pop-up]

CRA sign in

If you cannot access your individual online account, you or your representative can use an automated service to check the tax account balance by calling 1-800-959-8281 and selecting option 4.

If you cannot access your business online account, you or your representative can contact us about your balance by phone.

[Step 3: ]Transfer payments or balances

You can sign in to your CRA account to transfer a payment.

If you have an authorized representative

Your representative can sign in to their Represent a client account and transfer the payment for you, if they have the appropriate authority level:

  • Level 2 or legal representative for individuals
  • Level 2 or delegate authority level 3 for businesses

  • Individuals

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select "Accounts and payments"
    3. Select "Transfer payment(s)"

    Payments on an individual tax account that have not been applied to a particular tax year can be viewed in the “Available payment(s)” section of the “Accounts and payments” page.

  • Businesses

    In My Business Account you can:

    • Transfer a payment from one interim period to another
    • Pay an amount owing within a program account
    • Transfer payments between program accounts of the same nine-digit business number

    Find out more about how to transfer business payments between accounts

Resolve an issue with a payment

If you had a debt on your account, we may have automatically used your refund, benefit or credit to pay down the debt.

You can contact us by phone for an issue on a payment made to, or expected from, the CRA:

Payment made to the CRA

Before you contact us, confirm if either:

  • Your payment was not applied to your CRA account as expected after:
    • 5 business days for online payments
    • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque
  • You are unable to transfer the payment or balance online
  • You need money returned that you accidentally sent to the CRA

Payment expected from the CRA

Before you contact us, confirm if either:

  • You have been notified that we sent you a payment but you have not received the payment after:
    • 5 business days for online payments
    • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque
  • You were not sent a payment you were expecting, and could not find an explanation online or in a notice we sent you

Calling on behalf of someone else

You can only call on behalf of someone else in the following 2 situations:

  • The person is present on the call to verify their identity in English or French and can provide verbal authorization for you to speak for them

  • You are the person's legal representative with full legal authority to speak for them
