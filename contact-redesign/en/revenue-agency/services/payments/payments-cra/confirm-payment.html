Payments to the CRA

Confirm a payment – Payments to the CRA

You can confirm that you have made or received a payment, or that you have a balance owing to the CRA. Some payments you make will not appear in your CRA account right away.

On this page

  • How to confirm or correct a payment
  • Make sure it's really the CRA contacting you

How to confirm or correct a payment

Follow these steps to confirm or correct a payment.

If you made a payment to the wrong account, you can correct the error by transferring between your accounts.

  1. Allow time for your payment to be processed

    Before checking to see if we have received a payment you sent, allow:

    • 3 business days for online payments
    • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque or money order

  2. Sign in to your CRA account to confirm your payment

    You can sign in to your CRA account to check your balances and confirm where your payments went.

    • Individuals

      1. Sign in to your CRA account
      2. Select your “Individual” account
      3. Select “Accounts and payments”

    • Businesses

      1. Sign in to your CRA account
      2. Select your "Business" account
      3. Select "View and pay account balance"

    If you did not receive a payment you were expecting, we may have automatically used your refund, benefit or credit to pay down a debt.

  3. Move a payment that went to the wrong place

    If your payment went to the wrong place in your CRA account, you can correct the problem yourself without calling.

    Sign in to your CRA account to transfer the payment to the right place.

    • Individuals

      1. Sign in to your CRA account
      2. Select your "Individual" account
      3. Select "Accounts and payments"
      4. Select "Payment transfer"

      If a payment has not been applied to a particular tax year, it will appear in the “Available payment(s)” section of the “Accounts and payments” page.

    • Businesses

      1. Sign in to your CRA account
      2. Select your “Business” account on the Welcome screen
      3. Find the account you made the payment for under the “Accounts” section of the Overview screen
      4. Select “View and pay account balance”
      5. Select “Transfer balance”

      Find out more about how to transfer business payments between accounts

    If you have an authorized representative

    Your representative can sign in to their Represent a client account and transfer the payment for you, if they have the appropriate authority level:

    • Level 2 or legal representative for individuals
    • Level 2 or delegate authority level 3 for businesses

  4. Resolve other payment issues

    Call us if:

    • You need money returned that you sent to the CRA by mistake
    • You did not receive a payment you were expecting, and could not find an explanation online or in a notice we sent you
    • Any of the previous steps did not work for you

Make sure it's really the CRA contacting you

If someone contacts you to say you owe money to the CRA, you can confirm whether or not this is true by checking your CRA account balance.
